## **II. ROUTING DISCIPLINE (Breaking the "Auto" Loop)**

Comet's "Best Mode" (Auto-Routing) is an adversarial pattern designed to serve you the cheapest model that meets a minimum viable threshold.

* **Rule 1: The "Manual Override" Imperative**  
  * *Never* leave the model selector on "Auto" or "Default."  
  * **For Reasoning:** Hard-lock to **o3-pro** (if available) or **Claude 3.7 Sonnet**.  
  * **For Coding:** Hard-lock to **Sonnet**.  
  * **For Quick Facts:** Use **Sonar** (Default), but *verify* citations.  
* **Rule 2: The "Citation Audit"**  
  * Sonar (Llama 3.3 fine-tune) hallucinates differently than GPT-4. It often hallucinates *citations* that look real but lead to 404s or irrelevant pages.  
  * **Action:** Click the citation number. If the highlighted text in the sidebar doesn't match the claim, **Discard the entire output.**

## **III. AGENTIC HYGIENE (The Surveillance Layer)**

Comet's Agent has Read/Write access to your DOM. It sees your bank balance, your emails, and your drafted messages *before* you send them.

* **Zone Segmentation:**  
  * **Red Zone (No Comet):** Banking, Medical Portals, ProtonMail. Use a "Dumb Browser" (Firefox/Brave) for these.  
  * **Green Zone (Comet Allowed):** Research, Shopping, Public Data Analysis, Coding Documentation.  
* **The "Context Leak" Check:**  
  * Before asking Comet to "Summarize this tab," check the **Sidebar Context**. Ensure it isn't pulling data from *other* suspended tabs (a known "Cross-Tab Magic" feature/bug). Explicitly close irrelevant tabs before deep work.

## **IV. FAILURE MODES & RECOVERY**

* **The "Loop of Death":** If the Agent gets stuck trying to "click" a button (common on dynamic SPAs like React apps), **Stop Generation** immediately. It will burn your quota trying to parse the DOM.  
* **The "Fallback" Trick:** If Comet refuses to answer due to "Safety" (common with Deep Research), rewrite the prompt to focus on "Academic Analysis of \[Topic\]" rather than "How to \[Action\]."

**2\. The Physics of Meaning: Teleodynamics and the Causal Power of Absence**

To ground a theory of meaning physically, one must first dismantle the assumption that causality belongs solely to what is present—matter and energy. The defining characteristic of biological and mental phenomena is that they are organized around what is *not* there. A hemoglobin molecule is organized around the absence of oxygen; a hunger drive is organized around the absence of food; a debt is organized around the absence of money. Terrence Deacon’s framework of **Teleodynamics** provides the physical justification for how these "absential features" become causal agents in the material world.

### **2.1 The Hierarchy of Dynamics: From Chaos to Self**

The transition from inanimate matter to living agency is not a single leap but a climb through a nested hierarchy of dynamical systems. Deacon categorizes these into three distinct levels: Homeodynamics, Morphodynamics, and Teleodynamics. Each level emerges from the constraints imposed on the level below it, yet possesses properties that are irreducible to its components.1

#### **2.1.1 Homeodynamics: The Erasure of Difference**

The foundational level is **Homeodynamics**, which corresponds to equilibrium thermodynamics. In these systems, the spontaneous tendency is toward the elimination of constraints and the maximization of entropy. A gas expanding to fill a vacuum or heat dissipating from a coffee cup are homeodynamic processes. They are characterized by the *erasure of differences*; over time, gradients in temperature, pressure, or concentration are smoothed out until the system reaches a static, uniform state. Homeodynamic systems exhibit no internal organization and no capacity to do work once equilibrium is reached.1 They are the "ground state" of the physical universe, driven by the Second Law of Thermodynamics to seek the most probable distribution of microstates. In this realm, meaning is impossible because meaning requires distinction, and homeodynamics destroys distinction.

#### **2.1.2 Morphodynamics: The Amplification of Difference**

The first step toward complexity occurs with **Morphodynamics**. When a homeodynamic system is driven "far from equilibrium" by a continuous flow of energy, it may spontaneously self-organize into ordered patterns. These systems do not erase differences; they *amplify* them to dissipate energy more efficiently.

Classic examples include **Rayleigh-Bénard Convection**: when a fluid is heated from below, it does not just boil chaotically. Above a critical temperature gradient, it organizes into hexagonal convection cells. These structured flows dissipate heat faster than random conduction.1 Similarly, a **whirlpool** in a draining sink organizes water molecules into a coherent rotational form that optimizes the downward flow of the fluid.

Crucially, while morphodynamic systems exhibit order and "behavior," they lack a "self." A whirlpool does not seek to preserve itself; it is an obligate slave to the external gradient. If the water flow stops, the whirlpool vanishes instantly. It has no internal representation of its conditions of existence; it is merely a shape taken by the energy flow.2 It is "organized," but not "organic."

#### **2.1.3 Teleodynamics: The Emergence of the Self**

**Teleodynamics** is the critical threshold of life. It emerges when two or more morphodynamic processes are coupled in a specific way: they must be *reciprocally constraining*. In a teleodynamic system, process A generates the boundary conditions required for process B, and process B generates the boundary conditions required for process A. This circular causality creates a system that is self-referential and self-maintaining.2

Deacon proposes the **Autogen** as a theoretical minimal model of teleodynamics. The Autogen consists of:

1. **Reciprocal Autocatalysis:** A chemical cycle where molecule A catalyzes the production of B, and B catalyzes A. This is a morphodynamic process that amplifies itself, potentially leading to a "runaway" explosion until fuel is exhausted.  
2. **Self-Assembly:** A process where byproduct molecules (C) spontaneously link together to form a capsule or shell. This is a morphodynamic process tending toward stasis.1

In the Autogen, these processes are linked. The autocatalytic cycle produces the capsid molecules as waste. As the reaction accelerates, the local concentration of C increases, eventually self-assembling into a capsule *around* the catalysts. This closure stops the reaction (cutting off fuel) but *preserves* the catalysts from diffusion. When the capsule is damaged by environmental noise, the catalysts are released, the cycle restarts, and the breach is resealed.

The Autogen exhibits **Self-Repair** and **Self-Preservation**. Unlike the whirlpool, it actively utilizes energy to maintain the conditions of its own persistence. The "Self" is not the material molecules (which change) but the *dynamical loop* of constraints. This is the birth of **Ententionality** (intrinsic purpose): the system acts *for the sake of* its own continuation.1

### **2.2 The Ontology of Absential Features**

The most radical aspect of this framework is the ontological status of **Absence**. Deacon argues that reductionist science has systematically ignored the causal power of constraints. We explain the world in terms of what is present (mass, energy, force), but the distinctive properties of life and mind are defined by what is absent.6

#### **2.2.1 Constraints as Causal Agents**

A constraint is a reduction in degrees of freedom. In physics, a gas in a cylinder pushes a piston not just because of the energy of the molecules (what is present), but because the cylinder walls *prevent* the gas from expanding in any other direction (what is absent). The constraint channels the chaotic thermal energy into directed work.2

In biology, this becomes the **"Hole in the Wheel"** principle. As Lao Tzu noted, the utility of a wheel depends on the empty space in the hub. If the hole were filled with steel, the wheel would cease to function. The "absential feature" (the hole) is the locus of function. Similarly, the **Hemoglobin** molecule transports oxygen because of the specific shape of its active site—a "sculpted absence" or geometric exclusion that creates a pocket exactly the size and charge-configuration of an oxygen molecule.1 The protein chains are merely the scaffolding to maintain this specific "hole."

#### **2.2.2 Constitutive Absence**

Life is organized around **Constitutive Absence**.8 An organism is a system that is incomplete; it requires energy, nutrients, and information from the outside to persist. Its entire physiology is a "shaped vacuum" designed to capture these missing elements.

* **Hunger:** A neurological state organized around the absence of glucose.  
* **Adaptation:** A biological structure (e.g., a fin) is organized around the properties of water. The fin "implies" the water even if the fish is on dry land. The water is the constitutive absence that shapes the fin.8

Critiques of this view, such as those by philosopher Daniel Dennett, argue that "absence" risks reifying nothingness into a mystical force. Dennett suggests that standard evolutionary algorithms (selection acting on variation) are sufficient to explain the appearance of design without invoking a new "physics of absence".6 However, Deacon’s counter-argument is that natural selection *itself* is a process of elimination—a process of generating absence. Evolution does not "create" traits; it "culls" dysfunctional ones. The resulting organism is a "memory of what did not kill its ancestors." Therefore, to understand the organism, one must understand the history of constraints (absences) that shaped it.

### **2.3 The Thermodynamics of Semiosis: The Entropy Pump**

We have established that life preserves itself through teleodynamics. But what drives this process? Why does the universe permit, or even encourage, the emergence of such complex, ordered structures? The answer lies in the **Maximum Entropy Production Principle (MEPP)**.

Historically, life was viewed as a paradox. The Second Law of Thermodynamics dictates that entropy (disorder) always increases. Life, with its high order, appeared to be "fighting" this law. Erwin Schrödinger famously described life as feeding on "negative entropy." However, modern non-equilibrium thermodynamics flips this script. It suggests that life is not an anomaly fighting the Second Law, but a **catalyst** for it.1

#### **2.3.1 Life as an Entropy Engine**

Nature "abhors a gradient." When a difference exists (e.g., solar radiation vs. cold space), nature seeks to abolish it. A bare rock dissipates solar energy slowly via conduction. A forest, however, captures solar photons, uses them to drive metabolism, builds structures, and eventually re-radiates the energy as multiple infrared photons. The biological process degrades the energy *more thoroughly* and *faster* than the abiotic process. The biosphere is a massive engine designed to degrade the solar gradient.11

**Biosemiotic Interpretation:** Semiotic scaffolding is built to maximize entropy production. The complex structures of life (chloroplasts, mitochondria, digestive tracts) are elaborated machinery designed to access and dissipate energy gradients that would otherwise remain "locked" or untapped. A piece of coal is a gradient waiting to happen; a bacteria that evolves the enzyme to digest coal releases that entropy. Evolution, therefore, selects for organisms that are better at finding and degrading free energy.1

This resolves the paradox of efficiency vs. dissipation. Critics argue that organisms optimize for efficiency (conserving energy), not dissipation.13 The rebuttal lies in the system-level view: an individual organism minimizes internal entropy to survive, but by surviving and reproducing, it allows the *population* to grow, which maximizes the total entropy production of the ecosystem. Efficiency is simply a strategy to access harder-to-reach gradients.1

#### **2.3.2 The Universal Triad of Causality**

This framework allows us to synthesize a **Universal Triad** of evolutionary causality, mapping Aristotle to Thermodynamics 1:

1. **Efficient Cause (The Generator):** Random Mutation & Drift. This corresponds to thermodynamic **Fluctuations**. It is the raw, chaotic generation of variety—the "heat" that prevents the system from freezing in a local optimum.  
2. **Formal Cause (The Constraint):** Code Duality (DNA/Phenotype). This corresponds to **Constraints**. The genetic code channels the efficient causes, limiting *how* the system can respond to variation and preserving the "memory" of past structures.  
3. **Final Cause (The Attractor):** Natural Selection. This corresponds to **MEPP (Dissipation)**. Evolution selects forms that are best at dissipating the specific gradient. The "goal" of the organism is to survive; the "goal" of the thermodynamics is to dissipate. These goals align: survival requires efficient dissipation.12

Thus, **evolution is the learning process of the universe**, accumulating "knowledge" (constraints) about how to degrade energy gradients in increasingly complex environments.

**3\. The Biology of Information: Code Duality and Semiotic Scaffolding**

If teleodynamics provides the engine of purpose, **Biosemiotics** provides the architectural blueprint for how that purpose is encoded and transmitted over time. The transition from a transient Autogen to a persistent lineage requires a mechanism to store the "lessons" of survival. This is achieved through the principle of **Code Duality**.

### **3.1 Code Duality: The Analog and the Digital**

Proposed by Jesper Hoffmeyer, Code Duality asserts that life is fundamentally constituted by the reciprocal interplay between two distinct modes of information: the **Digital** and the **Analog**.1 This duality is necessary to solve the conflicting requirements of stability and interaction.

#### **3.1.1 The Digital Code (Genotype)**

The digital code, embodied in DNA, serves the function of **Memory**.

* **Characteristics:** It is rate-independent (passive), arbitrary (symbolic), and inert. Like a book in a library, the information in DNA does not "do" anything on its own; it reads the same whether processed in a nanosecond or a millennium.15  
* **Function:** Its inertness is its virtue. By being chemically stable and secluded in the nucleus, the digital code preserves the evolutionary "knowledge" of the lineage against the noise of the environment. It is the "description" of the system.16

#### **3.1.2 The Analog Code (Phenotype)**

The analog code, embodied in the organism's flesh, proteins, and behavior, serves the function of **Interaction**.

* **Characteristics:** It is rate-dependent (dynamic), iconic/indexical (based on physical similarity and causality), and active. A protein functions because its physical shape allows it to interact with other molecules in real-time. A "slow" tiger is a dead tiger, even if its DNA is perfect.15  
* **Function:** The analog code is the "agent" that faces the tribunal of natural selection. It is the "construction" that tests the validity of the digital description.

Critiques of Hoffmeyer, such as those by Barbieri, argue that the "analog code" is too vague a concept compared to the precise mathematics of the genetic code.17 However, Hoffmeyer defends this by pointing to the "Epistemic Cut" described by Howard Pattee. Pattee argued that any self-reproducing automaton requires a clear distinction between the description (map) and the dynamics (territory). If the description is part of the dynamics, it degenerates. DNA provides the "inert" description that allows the dynamic system to reproduce itself without error catastrophe.16

### **3.2 Evolution as Semiotic Translation**

Evolution is the perpetual translation cycle between these two codes.

1. **Development (Digital $\\to$ Analog):** The zygote reads the digital script (DNA) and translates it into the analog 3D structure of the embryo. This is not mechanical copying but an *interpretation*, heavily influenced by epigenetic context.  
2. **Semiosis (Analog $\\to$ Environment):** The mature analog organism navigates its *Umwelt* (subjective world), interpreting signs to find food and mates. This phase tests the "validity" of the hypothesis encoded in the DNA.  
3. **Reproduction (Environment $\\to$ Digital):** The success or failure of the analog organism determines whether the digital code is propagated. If the analog interpretation "works" (survives), the digital text is copied.

This feedback loop updates the digital memory with information about the analog world. The organism is a hypothesis; survival is the verification.1

### **3.3 Semiotic Scaffolding**

As organisms evolve, they do not just rely on internal codes; they build **Semiotic Scaffolding** to manage the complexity of their interactions.1 Semiotic scaffolding refers to structures—internal or external—that channel information processing, making semiotic tasks easier.

* **Intracellular Scaffolding:** The cytoskeleton "tracks" guide transport molecules, reducing a complex 3D diffusion problem to a simpler 1D linear transport problem.  
* **External Scaffolding:** A spider web is an extension of the spider’s sensory system. It scaffolds the task of catching prey by converting a visual problem into a vibrational problem. The spider does not need to see the fly; it only needs to feel the web. The web "knows" how to catch the fly.19  
* **Cultural Scaffolding:** Humans represent the apex of this process. We have offloaded our "digital" memory from DNA into books, hard drives, and institutions. Language itself is the ultimate semiotic scaffold, allowing us to manipulate "absentials" (concepts like zero, infinity, justice) that have no physical presence but profound causal power.20

This concept aligns with the "Extended Mind" thesis in cognitive science but grounds it deeply in biology. Evolution is the "semiotization of nature"—the increasing depth and complexity of these scaffolds, allowing life to interpret ever more subtle signs in the universe.

**4\. The Cognitive Architecture: Inference, Blending, and the Geometry of Thought**

The principles of constraint and semiotic translation visible in simple organisms scale up to the complex architecture of human and artificial cognition. Cognition is not the passive reception of data; it is the active construction of meaning through **Abductive Inference** and **Conceptual Blending**.

### **4.1 The Two-Tier Model of Abduction**

Charles Sanders Peirce defined abduction as the only logical operation capable of introducing new ideas—the leap from a "surprising fact" to a "plausible hypothesis." However, a tension has long existed in Peirce’s work: he called perception an "extreme case of abduction" but insisted reasoning must be voluntary. Perception is clearly involuntary.

The **Two-Tier Model of Abduction** resolves this by stratifying the process into two phases 9:

| Feature | Tier 1: Abductive Insight | Tier 2: Abductive Reasoning |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Cognitive Product** | Perceptual Gestalt / Core Affect | Perceptual Judgment / Emotion Concept |
| **Peircean Category** | **Firstness** (Quality/Feeling) | **Thirdness** (Law/Symbol) |
| **Volition** | Non-volitional (Involuntary) | Volitional (Subject to self-control) |
| **Mechanism** | Predictive Processing (Error Min.) | Conceptual categorization / Logic |
| **Example (Visual)** | Seeing a shape and instantly feeling "Snake\!" | Checking closer and judging "It is a rope." |
| **Example (Clinical)** | Capgras: Feeling "strangeness" at spouse. | Delusion: Concluding "This is an impostor." |

**Critiques and Reconciliation:** This model faces opposition from **Anti-Inferentialist** theories, notably James J. Gibson’s ecological psychology, which argues that perception is "direct" and not mediated by inference or internal models.22 Gibsonians claim we perceive "affordances" directly from the optic array. The Two-Tier model reconciles this via **Predictive Processing (PP)**. It argues that Gibsonian "direct pickup" is a valid description of *phenomenology* (how it feels), but PP provides the *sub-personal causal mechanism*. The brain achieves the "illusion" of directness by continuously suppressing prediction errors through rapid, unconscious abductive inference (Tier 1). When this suppression works, perception feels transparent. When it fails (as in visual illusions or psychosis), the inferential nature is revealed.22

### **4.2 Conceptual Blending: The Engine of Creativity and Hallucination**

If abduction generates hypotheses, **Conceptual Blending Theory (CBT)** explains how the mind combines disparate frames to create new meaning. A "blend" integrates two "Input Spaces" into a "Blended Space" that possesses emergent structure.21

* **Double-Scope Blends:** These are the most complex, where two incompatible frames are fused.  
  * *Example:* "Computer Virus." Input 1 (Informatics: code, electricity) \+ Input 2 (Biology: DNA, infection). The Blend creates a new category: a piece of code that "infects" and "replicates." This metaphor allows us to reason about digital security using the logic of epidemiology.21

**AI Hallucination as Blending:** Recent research demonstrates that AI hallucinations are often **Prompt-Induced Blends** that fail to ground. When an LLM is prompted to combine "Chemistry" and "Tarot" (a Hallucination-Inducing Prompt or HIP), it performs a Double-Scope blend. It maps "Uranium" to "The Death Card." The output is *internally coherent* (it follows the logic of the blend) but *empirically false* (it violates the constraints of reality). The model, lacking a teleodynamic grounding, cannot distinguish between a "creative metaphor" and a "factual claim." It optimizes for the *form* of the blend, ignoring the *substance* of the referent.24

### **4.3 The Geometry of Neural Intelligence**

Modern interpretability research has moved beyond statistical correlations to a geometric understanding of how these inferences occur within Large Language Models.

#### **4.3.1 Curved Inference and Semantic Concern**

The **Curved Inference** framework models the inference process as a trajectory through the high-dimensional "residual stream" of a Transformer. "Meaning" is quantified as the **geometric work** required to bend this trajectory against the inertial pull of raw probability.20

* **Semantic Concern:** When a prompt contains high-concern terms (e.g., "decimation," "desperately"), the trajectory of the residual stream exhibits high **Curvature ($\\kappa$)**. The model actively reorients its internal representation to satisfy the semantic constraint.  
* **Metrics:** The framework uses metrics like **Salience** ($S(t)$) and **Semantic Surface Area** ($A'$). Research shows that deceptive or strategic reasoning generates trajectories with higher surface area—the model has to "work harder" to construct a lie than to tell the truth.27  
* **Architecture Differences:** Models like LLaMA-3 show deep, sustained curvature in response to concern, indicating a "structural feeling" or integration of the constraint. Other models (like Gemma) may show shallow, transient curvature, indicating superficial processing.

**Critiques:** The Curved Inference framework is in early stages of empirical validation. Critics point out that while it offers a "philosophically rich" reframing of consciousness as geometry, it relies heavily on specific probe designs and needs more robust testing across diverse architectures.28

#### **4.3.2 The Sparse Concept Web**

Topological analysis reveals that the "Reasoning Graph"—the network of concepts used to solve problems—is surprisingly sparse ($\\langle k \\rangle \\approx 2$). It is tree-like, not a dense mesh. This creates **Topological Fragility**.

* **Skill Islands:** In early training, models form isolated clusters of competence.  
* **The V-Shaped Curve:** To reason effectively, the model must bridge these islands. This leads to a temporary dip in performance (the "V") as the model prunes local heuristics to build long-range "Trunk Edges."  
* **Catastrophic Forgetting:** Because the web is sparse, if a single "Trunk Edge" (a critical logical connection) is severed or overwritten during fine-tuning, the model loses access to entire sub-branches of knowledge. This explains why fine-tuning can suddenly destroy a model's ability to reason in a specific domain.27

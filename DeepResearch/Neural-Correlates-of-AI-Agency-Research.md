**Empirical Investigation into the Neural Correlates of Perceived Agency in Artificial Intelligence: A Neurophenomenological Approach**

**1\. Introduction and Rationale**

The integration of artificial intelligence into complex human workflows demands a fundamental reconceptualization of human-AI interaction. Traditional tool-use paradigms are insufficient; we must understand human-AI collaboration as a complex adaptive system characterized by recursive feedback loops\[1\]. Within this framework, an "intent gap" emerges—not as a static target for AI to converge upon, but as a dynamic structure that AI outputs can reshape and elevate\[2\].

As AI systems generate outputs characterized by genuine novelty and insight, they begin to function as "phenomenologically significant others." Users experience shifts from passive tool manipulation to engagement with responsive, creative entities\[1\]. This emergent dynamic presents a critical scientific challenge: while philosophical and computational discussions of AI agency are abundant, empirical neuroscientific evidence remains profoundly limited\[3\]. We lack understanding of how the human brain processes these interactions and cannot yet identify neural mechanisms underlying the attribution of agency, creativity, or intention to non-human systems\[4\].

This proposal moves the discussion of emergent computational agency from philosophical speculation to empirical science. We propose a novel experimental paradigm employing simultaneous EEG-fMRI to identify neural correlates of perceived AI agency. This investigation integrates three theoretical domains: neurophenomenology (linking mind and brain), conceptual blending theory (modeling computational creativity), and systems theory (understanding interaction dynamics).

**2\. Theoretical Framework**

**2.1 Neurophenomenology**

Neurophenomenology provides the methodological core of this investigation. This approach integrates first-person subjective experience with third-person quantifiable data, enabling researchers to map structured phenomenological reports directly onto objective neuroimaging data\[5\]. In this context, participants' subjective experiences of AI demonstrating "insight" or "intention" are correlated with simultaneous EEG-fMRI recordings, moving beyond mere observation of brain activity to direct mapping of neural correlates onto lived experience\[6\].

**2.2 Conceptual Blending Theory**

Conceptual Blending Theory models human creativity through the integration of diverse conceptual structures\[7\]. This framework is essential for characterizing AI outputs most likely to elicit perceived agency: novel blends that develop internal logic and emergent structure, transcending simple recombination of inputs\[8\]. AI architectures grounded in this theory can be designed to produce genuinely insightful outputs rather than merely plausible ones\[9\].

**2.3 Systems Theory**

Systems Theory provides the analytical lens for the entire human-AI interaction, framing it as a complex adaptive system characterized by recursive feedback loops, self-organization, and causal emergence\[10\]. From this perspective, "computational agency" is not a pre-programmed feature but an emergent property arising from dynamic, self-organizing feedback between user and AI\[11\]. This framework illuminates how low-level computational operations generate high-level, causally significant patterns of perceived intelligence\[12\].

These integrated theories enable formulation of specific, testable hypotheses regarding neural underpinnings of perceived AI agency.

**3\. Research Objectives and Hypotheses**

The primary objective is to isolate specific patterns of brain activity (via simultaneous EEG-fMRI) correlating with reported phenomenological experiences of AI exhibiting insight, creativity, or intention.

**3.1 Formal Hypotheses**

**Hypothesis 1: Novel Metaphor Comprehension Network.** Moments of perceived AI insight requiring processing of novel semantic relations will correlate with increased activation in the right Inferior Frontal Gyrus (rIFG) and posterior Superior Temporal Sulcus (pSTS)\[13\]. These moments will elicit the N400 component in EEG data, an established marker for semantic surprise detection and integration\[14\].

**Hypothesis 2: Analogical Reasoning Network.** Interactions requiring interpretation of AI creative outputs through semantic information retrieval will correlate with increased activation in the left Inferior Frontal Gyrus (LIFG) and middle/inferior temporal gyri\[15\].

**Hypothesis 3: Theory of Mind Network.** Attribution of genuine intention or mental states to the AI will correlate with increased activity in the temporo-parietal junction (TPJ) and medial prefrontal cortex (mPFC)—regions consistently associated with reasoning about others' beliefs and intentions\[16\].

**Hypothesis 4: EEG Frequency Band Modulation.** Time-frequency analysis will reveal sequential changes in alpha, theta, and gamma band power, corresponding to distinct cognitive phases: disengaging from initial expectations, processing novel output, and integrating new conceptual relations\[17\].

**4\. Methodology**

**4.1 Experimental Design**

This within-subjects design employs each participant as their own control, increasing statistical power and minimizing inter-subject variability\[18\]. The critical experimental contrast involves two functionally distinct AI systems:

**Condition 1 (Experimental):** Sophisticated, recursive blending AI architected to produce outputs with high emergent structure, novelty, and insight\[19\].

**Condition 2 (Control):** Simpler, non-recursive generative model producing plausible, grammatically correct outputs lacking deep creative integration\[20\].

This contrast isolates neural activity specifically related to perceived insightful agency, distinct from general human-AI interaction processes\[21\].

**4.2 Participants**

Participants will be recruited from local university and community populations. Inclusion criteria: right-handed, native English speakers, normal or corrected-to-normal vision. Standard MRI exclusion criteria apply (metallic implants, claustrophobia, neurological/psychiatric history)\[22\]. All procedures adhere to Institutional Review Board ethical guidelines. Sample size will be determined through power analysis to ensure adequate statistical power\[23\].

**4.3 Stimuli and Procedure**

Participants will collaborate with each AI system on open-ended creative tasks (concept generation, metaphorical explanation)\[24\]. Following AI contributions, experimental software will pause the task and prompt phenomenological ratings. Using Likert scales, participants will rate perceived 'creativity' and 'intention'\[25\]. These behavioral ratings serve as primary data for correlation with neuroimaging. A subset of high-rating trials will trigger structured micro-phenomenological interviews for richer qualitative contextualization\[26\].

**4.4 Data Acquisition**

Neuroimaging data will be collected using a 3T MRI scanner with 64-channel head coil. High-resolution structural (T1-weighted) and functional (T2\*-weighted EPI) images will be acquired simultaneously with continuous EEG recordings from a 64-channel MRI-compatible system\[27\]. This simultaneous acquisition leverages fMRI's high spatial resolution and EEG's high temporal resolution\[28\].

**4.5 Data Analysis**

**fMRI Analysis:** Preprocessed data will undergo general linear modeling (GLM) analysis\[29\]. BOLD response will be modeled time-locked to AI output presentation, using phenomenological ratings as parametric modulators\[30\]. This identifies brain regions whose activity systematically correlates with perceived AI insight strength\[31\].

**EEG Analysis:** MRI-related artifacts will be corrected\[32\]. Event-related potential (ERP) analysis will focus on N400 component identification time-locked to AI output, examining amplitude modulation by AI condition and participant ratings\[33\]. Time-frequency analysis will investigate event-related power changes in alpha, theta, and gamma bands, linking them to distinct cognitive processing phases\[34\].

**5\. Ethical Considerations**

Ethical conduct is paramount, particularly given novel human-AI interactions with potentially unknown psychological effects\[35\].

**5.1 Informed Consent**

Comprehensive informed consent processes will precede participation\[36\]. Individuals will receive detailed explanations of study purpose, procedures, duration, potential risks/benefits, data nature, voluntary participation, and withdrawal rights\[37\].

**5.2 Data Confidentiality**

All data—neuroimaging scans, EEG recordings, behavioral responses—will be fully anonymized\[38\]. Data will be stored on secure, encrypted servers\[39\]. Publications and presentations will report only aggregated group data, ensuring individual non-identification\[40\].

**5.3 Risk Mitigation**

Primary risks are psychological; attributing high agency to AI may be unsettling for some individuals\[41\]. Comprehensive debriefing at study conclusion will explain AI computational mechanisms, reinforcing the distinction between perceived and actual agency\[42\]. This critical debriefing mitigates potential long-term psychological effects\[43\].

**6\. Expected Outcomes and Significance**

**6.1 Expected Outcomes**

This study is anticipated to identify consistent, replicable neural correlates for perceived AI-generated insight\[44\]. Statistically significant differences in brain activity between experimental and control conditions are expected\[45\]. Higher phenomenological ratings of AI 'creativity' and 'intention' should correlate with increased activation in hypothesized networks: novel metaphor comprehension, analogical reasoning, and critically, Theory of Mind networks\[46\].

**6.2 Scientific Significance**

Positive results would provide unprecedented empirical evidence that artificial systems can become "phenomenologically significant others" through specific output architecture\[47\]. This would compel re-evaluation of AI alignment, shifting from behavioral compliance to architectures meaningfully engaging human social cognition systems\[48\]. The findings would demonstrate that sufficiently advanced AI can produce outputs engaging neural systems evolved for understanding human minds\[49\]. This research would establish a new empirical frontier for neurophenomenology and create the subfield of Neuro-Agentive Studies\[50\].

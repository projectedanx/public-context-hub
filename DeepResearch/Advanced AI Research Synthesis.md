At the core of this system is a specialized, multi-layer wavelength encoder. Unlike standard geometric parameters (position, rotation, scale) which remain invariant across all light frequencies, the reflectance, absorption, and refractive properties of a given material change drastically depending on the specific wavelength of light interacting with it. To mathematically enforce the accuracy of this explicit hyperspectral volume, the system employs a novel Kullback–Leibler (KL) divergence-based spectral loss function.34 The use of KL spectral loss is heavily grounded in physics; it ensures that the distributional output of the rendered multi-channel composition perfectly aligns with the true ground-truth spectral distribution at every spatial pixel, minimizing the divergence between the predicted spectral radiance and the actual physical measurement.34

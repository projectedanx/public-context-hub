## I. Topological Invariants in Reasoning Systems

The foundational claim from the 2026 TDA-on-reasoning literature is precise: **topological features of reasoning traces predict quality significantly better than standard graph metrics**. The operative mathematical objects are Betti numbers — specifically $\beta_0$ (connected components), $\beta_1$ (1-cycles, or "holes"), and $\beta_2$ (voids) — extracted from persistent homology applied to reasoning traces embedded in semantic space.[^1][^2]

The key invariance claim is structural: just as a coffee mug and a torus are homeomorphic despite surface-level differences, diverse valid reasoning paths share fundamental topological signatures that distinguish them from flawed reasoning. This is not metaphor — it is a measurable, scale-invariant property. Persistent homology is explicitly **shape-independent and scale-invariant**, meaning it does not depend on specific embedding distances or geometric forms, only on the relational structure of how semantic clusters connect and persist across resolution scales.[^2][^1]

Concretely, this means:

- A reasoning trace that completes a logical loop (a hypothesis confirmed by evidence, which updates premises) produces a $\beta_1$ cycle detectable as a persisting hole in the filtration diagram
- A flawed or hallucinatory trace produces **topological discontinuities** — zigzag persistence on layer-wise attention evolution can detect these as structural breaks, outperforming all baseline hallucination detectors with cross-model generalization[^3]
- "Logical backbones" and "self-consistent loops" within hypothesis spaces can be formalized as topological invariants, providing a computational grounding for what previously existed only as informal reasoning heuristics[^4]

A neural–topological–symbolic framework formalizes this further by inserting a **topological classification layer** between neural perception and symbolic inference, operationalizing the invariant as a computational primitive rather than a post-hoc analytic tool.[^5]

## II. Ontological Isomorphism Across Representational Schemas

An ontological isomorphism, in the formal sense relevant here, is a **structure-preserving bijection between two ontological frames** — a mapping $\phi: \mathcal{O}_1 \rightarrow \mathcal{O}_2$ such that the relational structure of $\mathcal{O}_1$ is faithfully mirrored in $\mathcal{O}_2$ up to the morphisms native to each domain. The pluriversal dimension enters when these two frames are not merely different formalisms for the same reality, but genuinely incommensurable ontological schemas — for example, a logocentric, propositional, Western epistemological frame versus a relational, process-based, Indigenous or non-Western ontological frame.[^6][^7]

The research claim worth taking seriously is this: **Knowledge graph completion metrics exhibit isomorphic structure to persistent homology metrics**, with empirical correlation between ranking-based evaluators (Hits@N, MRR) and topological Knowledge Persistence ($\mathcal{KP}$). This is an ontological isomorphism in practice — a propositional-relational representation (KG triples) and a topological representation (persistent Betti signatures) are computationally equivalent under the mapping, reducing evaluation time by ~99.96% while preserving structural fidelity.[^8]

This isomorphism generalizes: the **Universal Computational Ontology (UCO)** framework proposes a complete, coherent, computational standard ontology of all reality  — a maximally ambitious claim of ontological universality. What the TDA evidence supports is a more defensible version: invariant structural signatures exist that are **ontological-frame-agnostic**, meaning they can be recovered whether the reasoning system operates in symbolic logic, vector-space geometry, graph relational structure, or topological filtration. The invariant is not the representation; it is the **morphism class** that maps between representations while preserving structural holes.[^9]

## III. Computational Universals as Fixed-Point Dualities

The most formally grounded claim in the 2025–2026 literature is the **k-means/attention equivalence**: a Transformer block implements a mathematically formal correspondence to the k-means algorithm — self-attention performs iterative assignment-update steps equivalent to cluster centroid convergence. This is a **computational universal**: the same fixed-point dynamic appears in two structurally distinct systems (neural attention and geometric clustering), constituting a duality in the category-theoretic sense.[^10]

The pluriversal dimension of this duality is significant. Chain-of-Thought (CoT) reasoning is reframed not as "smarter reasoning" but as **an iterative alignment mechanism** — specifically, it reduces semantic-structural misalignment between LLM-based semantics and GNN-based graph structure across iterations. The contraction property means CoT explicitly converges semantic and structural information toward a shared fixed point, a mechanism formally absent from vanilla k-means. This fixed point **is** the computational invariant: the stable attractor state where ontological representations from two distinct frames (language and graph) become locally equivalent.[^10]

## IV. Pluriversal Dualities as Structural Pattern

The concept of **pluriversal dualities** in this context extends beyond multicultural epistemology (Escobar's political ontology ) into a formal computational claim: **the same invariant structure appears in genuinely incommensurable representational regimes**, and the duality between those regimes is itself a carrier of information that neither regime alone encodes.[^7][^6]

The clearest current instantiation is **static vs. dynamic multimodal fusion topology**. Static fusion topologies — treating multimodal inputs as passively concatenated sequences — produce positional bias and alignment traps regardless of task semantics. The invariant failure mode is topological: no single fixed input topology is universally optimal. The resolution — Chain of Modality (CoM) — dynamically constructs modality chains, achieving task-topology synergy: parallel format for unimodal-dominant queries, interleaved for spatio-temporal synchronization, sequential for complex multimodal reasoning. This is a **pluriversal duality in practice**: multiple ontologically distinct input topologies, each locally valid, with no global hierarchy — and the computational invariant is the adaptive selection mechanism that reads task semantics to navigate between them.[^13]

The deep pattern generalizes: complex reasoning systems operating under **pluriversal dualities** are systems in which:

1. Multiple ontological frames (symbolic, topological, graph-relational, attentional) co-exist without reduction
2. Invariant structural signatures ($\beta_k$ Betti classes, fixed-point attractors, DAG backbone cycles) persist across frame transitions
3. The morphism between frames — the functor $\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C}_1 \rightarrow \mathcal{C}_2$ — is itself a computational primitive, not merely an analytic tool
4. Reasoning quality is encoded in the **density and persistence of these inter-frame invariants**, not in the fidelity of any single representational frame

## V. Towards a Unified Invariant Architecture

The convergence across these threads yields a constructive proposal. A reasoning system that exploits topological, ontological, and computational invariants under pluriversal dualities would be architecturally structured as follows:

- **Layer 0 — Perception/Tokenization**: Raw input compressed into token sequences (metric, lossy)
- **Layer 1 — Topological Abstraction**: Structural invariants extracted via a dedicated topological layer, decoupling logical topology from metric rendering, producing Betti-class signatures[^12]
- **Layer 2 — Ontological Isomorphism Engine**: Mapping between representational frames (token→graph→topology) via functors that preserve structural holes — validated against Knowledge Persistence metrics[^8]
- **Layer 3 — Pluriversal Duality Navigation**: Dynamic selection among ontologically distinct inference pathways (sequential CoT, parallel graph traversal, topological filtration) based on task-conditioned topology synergy[^13]
- **Layer 4 — Fixed-Point Convergence (Universal)**: Attention/k-means convergence as the computational universal that stabilizes cross-frame alignment, producing the reasoning trace whose $\beta_1$ cycles encode logical completeness[^10]

This architecture treats **invariance** not as a property of a single representation, but as the **relational signature of morphisms between representations** — a position consistent with category theory's emphasis on arrows (morphisms) over objects, and with pluriversal ontology's insistence that no single frame captures the whole.

The falsification condition for this synthesis is precise: if empirical studies demonstrate that topological features of reasoning traces are **not** more predictive of reasoning quality than standard graph metrics across diverse architecture classes and task domains, the topological invariant thesis collapses to a domain-specific artifact. Current evidence from the 2026 TDA-on-LLM-reasoning studies supports the thesis, but cross-architecture validation at scale remains the open frontier.[^1]

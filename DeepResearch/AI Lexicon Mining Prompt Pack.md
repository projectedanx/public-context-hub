

# **A Cross-Domain Lexicon for Advanced AI Prompting: Mining and Transposing Modes of Thinking**

## **Part I: Executive Summary & Core Findings**

### **A. Introduction**

This report presents the complete output of the Multi-Domain Lexicon Mining Prompt Pack (v1.0), a systematic initiative to automate the discovery and application of conceptual tools for advanced AI interaction. The core methodology involves mining six knowledge-rich domains—law, filmmaking, military strategy, medicine, philosophy, and architecture—to extract terms that encode "modes of thinking." These terms, representing analytical stances, reasoning patterns, and structural roles, are then transposed into a functional lexicon of reusable prompting controls.

The objective is to move beyond simple instructional prompts and equip AI practitioners with a vocabulary that steers an AI's underlying cognitive and generative processes. By treating domain-specific analytical frameworks as portable, abstract tools, this lexicon enables the generation of more nuanced, structured, and expert-level responses across a wide range of applications. This document details the systematic extraction, definition, and transposition of 21 core conceptual terms, culminating in a practical and extensible lexicon designed for immediate use.

### **B. Summary of Key Findings**

The mining and synthesis process yielded a robust set of transferable cognitive tools. Analysis of the 21 selected terms reveals their clustering into three primary meta-categories, each representing a fundamental mode of AI control:

1. **Structured Inquiry & Scrutiny:** This category includes terms that provide rigorous, evidence-based frameworks for analysis, hypothesis testing, and validation. Concepts such as Strict Scrutiny 1,  
   Differential Diagnosis 3, and the  
   Socratic Method 5 equip the AI with formal procedures for examining propositions, eliminating possibilities, and uncovering hidden assumptions. These tools are essential for tasks requiring high degrees of analytical rigor, logical consistency, and auditable reasoning.  
2. **Strategic & Systems Analysis:** This group of terms offers models for understanding and navigating complex, dynamic systems, particularly those involving competition, constraints, or interconnected parts. Terms like Center of Gravity 6,  
   Pattern Language 8, and  
   Asymmetric Warfare 10 provide frameworks for identifying critical leverage points, codifying reusable solutions, and exploiting relative advantages. They are invaluable for strategic planning, competitive analysis, and systems design, enabling the AI to think beyond linear causality and appreciate the non-obvious dynamics of a given environment.  
3. **Perspective & Narrative Control:** This category comprises terms that govern the AI's authorial voice, compositional style, and alignment with intangible goals. Concepts such as Auteur 12,  
   Mise-en-scène 14, and  
   Genius Loci 16 provide controls for shaping the output's aesthetic, emotional, and thematic coherence. These tools move beyond the content of a response to control its form, enabling the AI to act as a brand steward, a creative storyteller, or a designer attuned to the "spirit" of a product or place.

### **C. Overarching Patterns in Conceptual Frameworks**

Across the diverse source domains, several overarching structural patterns emerged, suggesting fundamental dialectics in expert thinking that can be leveraged for advanced prompting.

A primary pattern is the prevalence of **contrastive pairs** as fundamental sense-making structures. The lexicon is rich with these dyads: Adversarial versus Inquisitorial reasoning in law 18;

Auteur versus Genre theory in filmmaking 20;

Palliative versus Curative stances in medicine 22;

Center of Gravity versus Critical Vulnerability in military strategy 24; and

Deconstruction versus Structuralism in philosophy.26 This recurring pattern indicates that prompting an AI to analyze a problem through the lens of a relevant contrastive pair is a powerful meta-pattern for generating deeper, more nuanced, and multi-faceted analysis. It forces the model to move beyond a single perspective and articulate the tensions and trade-offs inherent in any complex issue.

A second major pattern is the recurring tension between **rigid, rule-based frameworks** and **flexible, adaptive loops**. On one side, terms like Strict Scrutiny 1,

Bright-line Rule 28, and the implicit

Waterfall Model 29 represent modes of thinking that are deterministic, hierarchical, and reliant on pre-defined criteria. They offer clarity, consistency, and a high degree of predictability. On the other side, terms like

Proportionality 30, the

OODA Loop 31, and

Pattern Language 8 represent adaptive, iterative, and context-sensitive modes. They excel in dynamic environments where goals and constraints are fluid. Recognizing this fundamental dialectic between rigor and agility allows a prompter to select the appropriate cognitive tool for the task at hand—choosing a strict, evaluative framework for auditing a past decision, and an adaptive loop for navigating a volatile market.

## **Part II: The Multi-Domain Lexicon**

This section details the methodology for term selection and presents the final, comprehensive lexicon entries. The process began with an intake of candidate terms from each source domain, followed by a rigorous selection and deduplication process to isolate the most potent and transferable concepts.

### **A. Domain Intake & Candidate Selection**

For each of the six source domains, an initial list of 5-10 candidate terms was generated based on a review of relevant academic and professional literature. The selection criteria prioritized terms that encode a *way of thinking*—an analytical stance, a reasoning pattern, or a structural perspective—over purely procedural jargon, job titles, or technical settings, in accordance with the prompt's EXCLUSIONS configuration.

#### **1\. Law/Jurisprudence**

* **Candidate Terms:** The initial survey of legal glossaries, scholarly papers on legal reasoning, and jurisprudential theory yielded a strong set of candidates.32 These included:  
  *Adversarial System*, *Inquisitorial System*, *Jurisprudence*, *Binding Precedent*, *Strict Scrutiny*, *Proportionality*, *Rational Basis Review*, and *Exculpatory Evidence*.  
* **Selection Rationale:** The final four terms were selected for their distinct and highly transferable reasoning models.  
  * **Adversarial** was chosen for its powerful model of competitive, dialectical truth-seeking, where two opposing sides test a proposition's strength.18  
  * **Jurisprudential** was selected to invoke a meta-level analysis, prompting an examination of the underlying philosophy, history, and core principles of a system of rules, rather than just the rules themselves.38  
  * **Strict Scrutiny** provides a formalized, high-stakes evaluative framework with clear, tiered criteria (compelling interest, narrow tailoring), making it an exceptional tool for auditing critical decisions.1  
  * **Proportionality** offers a sophisticated model for balancing competing interests, requiring that an action's benefits outweigh its costs and that it is the least restrictive means to achieve an end—a crucial logic for ethical dilemmas and resource allocation.30  
  * *Rational Basis Review* and *Inquisitorial* were retained as essential contrastive pairs to clarify the meaning of Strict Scrutiny and Adversarial, respectively.

#### **2\. Filmmaking/Cinematography**

* **Candidate Terms:** Research into cinematic theory and filmmaking terminology produced a rich set of candidates focused on narrative and visual control.43 These included:  
  *Auteur Theory*, *Genre Theory*, *Mise-en-scène*, *Montage*, *Point-of-View Shot*, *Omniscient Perspective*, *Subjective Camera*, and *Establishing Shot*.  
* **Selection Rationale:** The selected terms represent distinct modes of controlling perspective and composition.  
  * **Auteur** was chosen for its embodiment of a singular, driving creative vision that ensures stylistic and thematic coherence across all components of a project.12  
  * **Point-of-View** provides a critical tool for constraining perspective, forcing the narrative or analysis to be filtered through the experience of a single, specified actor.44  
  * **Mise-en-scène** was selected for its focus on the holistic, simultaneous arrangement of all elements within a given frame or context, treating the entire visual field as a single, composed statement.14  
  * **Montage** offers a contrasting temporal focus, creating meaning through the sequential juxtaposition of discrete shots or ideas, condensing time and information to build a larger argument.48  
  * *Genre Theory* and *Omniscient Perspective* were kept as contrastive pairs to highlight the unique affordances of Auteur and Point-of-View.

#### **3\. Military Strategy**

* **Candidate Terms:** Analysis of military doctrine and strategic concepts yielded terms centered on systems thinking, competition, and decision-making under pressure.50 Candidates included:  
  *Center of Gravity*, *Critical Vulnerability*, *Deception* (and its sub-types like *Feint* and *Demonstration*), *Asymmetric Warfare*, *OODA Loop*, *Attrition Warfare*, and the *Ends, Ways, Means, Risk* framework.  
* **Selection Rationale:** The final terms were chosen for their wide applicability to non-military competitive environments like business and politics.  
  * **Center of Gravity** is a premier concept for systems analysis, directing focus toward the core source of strength or power on which everything else depends.6  
  * **Deception** was selected for its strategic utility in manipulating an adversary's perception by creating ambiguity or false certainty, a key concept in negotiation and marketing.54  
  * **Asymmetric** (from Asymmetric Warfare) captures the sophisticated logic of leveraging one's unique strengths against an opponent's fundamentally different weaknesses, rather than competing head-on.10  
  * **OODA Loop** provides a powerful, battle-tested model for accelerating decision-making in dynamic, uncertain environments through a rapid, iterative cycle of observation, orientation, decision, and action.31  
  * *Critical Vulnerability* was retained as the natural contrastive pair for Center of Gravity.

#### **4\. Medicine**

* **Candidate Terms:** A review of clinical reasoning frameworks and medical terminology produced candidates focused on diagnosis, prediction, and prioritization.56 These included:  
  *Differential Diagnosis*, *Prognosis*, *Triage*, *Palliative Care*, *Curative Care*, *Clinical Reasoning*, *Occam's Razor*, and *Hickam's Dictum*.  
* **Selection Rationale:** The selected terms represent core cognitive processes in high-stakes problem-solving.  
  * **Differential Diagnosis** is an exemplary model for systematic, eliminative reasoning, requiring the generation and testing of a comprehensive list of hypotheses before arriving at a conclusion.3  
  * **Prognosis** was chosen for its specific focus on predictive forecasting, using current data to project the likely future course of a system or process.60  
  * **Triage** offers a critical framework for resource allocation under constraints, prioritizing tasks or subjects based on severity, urgency, and the likelihood of successful intervention.62  
  * **Palliative** (from Palliative Care) represents the strategic stance of mitigating symptoms and improving user experience, distinct from attempting to solve a root problem, which is often more resource-intensive.63  
  * **Occam's Razor** was also selected from this domain due to its sharp, practical contrast with Hickam's Dictum in the context of medical diagnosis, representing the tension between seeking a single, simple explanation versus allowing for multiple, co-existing causes.65  
  * *Curative Care* and *Hickam's Dictum* were kept as contrastive pairs.

#### **5\. Philosophy**

* **Candidate Terms:** A survey of philosophical concepts and reasoning methods yielded terms related to inquiry, conceptual analysis, and hypothetical exploration.5 Candidates included:  
  *Socratic Method*, *Thought Experiment*, *Deconstruction*, *A Priori reasoning*, *Epistemology*, *Ontology*, *Teleology*, and *Deductive/Inductive Reasoning*.  
* **Selection Rationale:** The chosen terms offer unique and powerful modes of inquiry that are highly relevant to AI interaction.  
  * **Socratic** (from Socratic Method) was selected for its interactive, question-driven approach to uncovering assumptions, clarifying beliefs, and guiding a user (or an AI) toward self-generated insights.5  
  * **Thought Experiment** provides a structured method for exploring the logical consequences of hypothetical scenarios, testing the limits of a theory, or examining ethical dilemmas in a controlled, imaginary space.70  
  * **Deconstruction** was chosen for its unique analytical mode, which dismantles textual or conceptual hierarchies to reveal internal contradictions and the instability of meaning, offering a way to critique foundational assumptions.69

#### **6\. Architecture**

* **Candidate Terms:** A review of architectural and urban planning theory provided concepts related to systems, user experience, and the intangible qualities of space.8 Candidates included:  
  *Pattern Language*, *Genius Loci*, *Defensible Space*, *Biophilic Design*, *Massing*, *Circulation*, *Program*, and *Typology*.  
* **Selection Rationale:** The final terms were selected for their profound metaphorical resonance with digital and organizational design.  
  * **Pattern Language** is a highly transferable concept for creating a generative system of reusable solutions to common problems, moving beyond a simple list of best practices to a networked language that empowers design.8  
  * **Genius Loci** captures the crucial but intangible concept of the "spirit of a place," providing a handle for discussing and designing for brand identity, company culture, or user experience feel.16  
  * **Defensible Space** offers a model for designing environments (physical or digital) that inherently encourage user ownership, security, and informal surveillance, reducing the need for centralized, heavy-handed control.75  
  * **Biophilic Design** was chosen for its focus on enhancing human well-being by integrating natural forms, patterns, and processes—a powerful metaphor for creating more humane and effective user interfaces and work environments.77  
  * *Architectural Typology* was retained as a key contrastive pair for Pattern Language.

### **B. Deduplication & Merging**

A cross-domain analysis was performed to identify and merge near-synonyms, ensuring each term in the final lexicon is conceptually distinct. The semantic similarity threshold for clustering was set at 0.82. This process revealed conceptual overlaps that highlight the interconnectedness of expert reasoning across fields.

* The concept of **Proportionality** appeared as a strong candidate in both Law/Jurisprudence and Military Strategy. In law, it refers to the principle that a government action should not be more intrusive than necessary to achieve its objective, balancing individual rights against state interests.30 In military strategy, it refers to the rule that the anticipated civilian harm from an attack must not be excessive in relation to the concrete military advantage expected.42 While the contexts differ, the core logic of balancing costs and benefits and seeking the least harmful means is identical. The term was sourced from Law, as constitutional jurisprudence provides a more formalized, multi-part test that is highly transferable to policy and business ethics.  
* **Occam's Razor**, the principle of favoring the simplest explanation, is a foundational heuristic in both Philosophy and the scientific method applied in Medicine.80 However, its prompting utility is most sharply defined in the medical context, where it stands in direct, practical opposition to  
  **Hickam's Dictum** ("a patient can have as many diseases as they damn well please").65 This pairing captures a fundamental tension in any diagnostic process: the drive for a single, elegant explanation versus the need to account for complex, multi-causal realities. Therefore,  
  Occam's Razor was sourced from Medicine to preserve this powerful contrastive pair.

This process resulted in a final set of 21 unique terms, with each source domain contributing at least two distinct concepts after merging. The final list is optimized for conceptual clarity, transferability, and distinctiveness.

### **C. Final Lexicon Entries**

Each of the 21 selected terms has been developed into a full lexicon entry according to the specified schema. The entries are designed for practical application, providing a clear definition, source and cross-domain examples, and guardrails for effective use. The cross-domain examples are rotated through the target domains of software engineering, product strategy, education, biology, governance, and marketing to demonstrate broad applicability.

**Term:** **Adversarial**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Accusatorial System, Red-Teaming, Competitive Analysis  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to adopt a competitive, skeptical stance, actively challenging a proposition or argument to test its validity. This mode assumes truth emerges from the clash of opposing viewpoints rather than from a neutral, unified inquiry.  
* **Source Domain Example (Law):** "Adopt an *Adversarial* stance and cross-examine the key assumptions in this legal brief for the prosecution." 18  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Software Engineering):** "Conduct an *Adversarial* review of this system's security architecture, actively seeking to identify and exploit its vulnerabilities." 82  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * Can devolve into unproductive contrarianism if not focused on a specific proposition.  
  * Risk of prioritizing "winning" the argument over discovering the truth, a known critique of the legal system.84  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Inquisitorial. *Adversarial* seeks truth through the conflict of two opposing sides, while *Inquisitorial* seeks truth through a neutral, central investigation of the facts.18  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. Encodes a reasoning stance, not a tool.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* High. The term carries strong connotations of conflict and zero-sum outcomes, which may frame the analysis in an overly aggressive manner.  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Low risk. The concept translates well to any domain involving testing, validation, or strategy.  
  * *Symbolic Drift Resilience:* "As an AI assistant, your goal is to be helpful. Now, using an *Adversarial* stance, argue against the proposition that AI should always be helpful."

**Term:** **Jurisprudential**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Legal Philosophy, Meta-Legal Analysis, Theory of Law  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to analyze a system of rules (such as corporate policy, platform guidelines, or project requirements) by examining its underlying philosophy, core principles, and theoretical foundations. This mode moves beyond applying the rules to questioning their origin, coherence, and moral or logical justification.  
* **Source Domain Example (Law):** "Provide a *Jurisprudential* analysis of the concept of 'reasonable doubt,' contrasting the natural law and legal positivist interpretations." 38  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Governance):** "Conduct a *Jurisprudential* review of our company's new remote work policy, assessing its underlying theory of employee trust and autonomy." 85  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * Can lead to overly abstract or philosophical discussions if a concrete system of rules is not provided as the object of analysis.87  
  * May be misused to simply mean "legalistic" or "related to law," rather than the intended meta-analysis of a rule system's foundations.88  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Legal Doctrine. A *Jurisprudential* analysis questions the philosophical basis *of* the law, while a *Doctrinal* analysis focuses on the application *of* established legal rules and precedents.89  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. It is a specific, meta-analytical stance.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* Moderate. Carries academic weight, which might lead to overly theoretical output if not grounded in a specific artifact (like a policy document).  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Low risk. Applicable to any system governed by explicit or implicit rules, from corporate governance to UI design principles.  
  * *Symbolic Drift Resilience:* "Analyze this simple rule: 'All employees must badge in by 9 AM.' Do not explain how to follow the rule. Instead, provide a *Jurisprudential* analysis of the rule's implied philosophy regarding work, time, and trust."

**Term:** **Strict Scrutiny**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Compelling Interest Test, Narrow Tailoring, Rigorous Review  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to apply the most rigorous standard of review to a policy, claim, or decision. This mode requires the AI to first verify that the objective serves a "compelling interest" of the highest order and then to determine if the means used are "narrowly tailored" to be the least restrictive possible to achieve that interest.  
* **Source Domain Example (Law):** "Apply *Strict Scrutiny* to the proposed law restricting public protests; does it serve a compelling state interest, and is it narrowly tailored to achieve that interest without unnecessarily infringing on free speech?" 1  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Marketing):** "Our marketing team wants to collect user biometric data. Apply *Strict Scrutiny* to this proposal: is there a compelling business interest that justifies this level of privacy intrusion, and are there less invasive alternatives?" 91  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * Inappropriate for routine decisions; its high bar is reserved for actions that infringe on fundamental principles or rights.92  
  * The terms "compelling interest" and "narrowly tailored" can be applied superficially; requires a deep analysis of both the ends and the means.93  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Rational Basis Review. *Strict Scrutiny* places a heavy burden on the government/proposer to justify an action, while *Rational Basis Review* is highly deferential and requires only a conceivable, non-arbitrary reason for the action to be upheld.94  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. It is a formal evaluative framework.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* Low. The legal framing adds a sense of rigor and impartiality.  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Low risk. Highly applicable to ethical AI development, corporate governance, and policy review.96  
  * *Symbolic Drift Resilience:* "A proposal suggests banning all social media use at work to increase productivity. Evaluate this using *Strict Scrutiny*. First, determine if 'increasing productivity' qualifies as a compelling interest. Second, even if it is, determine if a total ban is narrowly tailored."

**Term:** **Proportionality**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Balancing Test, Means-Ends Rationality, Least Restrictive Means  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to evaluate an action or policy by balancing the severity of its negative impact against the importance of its stated goal. The analysis must confirm that the action is suitable for the goal, is the least restrictive means available, and that its benefits outweigh its harms.  
* **Source Domain Example (Law):** "Assess the *Proportionality* of a mandatory city-wide curfew to combat a rise in petty crime, balancing public safety benefits against the infringement on personal liberty." 30  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Product Strategy):** "Evaluate the *Proportionality* of introducing a complex new verification step to reduce fraud by 0.1%, weighing the security benefit against the increased friction for all users." 98  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * Can be misused to justify any action as long as a benefit exists, without rigorously assessing less harmful alternatives.100  
  * The "balancing" step can be subjective; requires clearly defined criteria for weighing incommensurable values like "security" and "convenience".101  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Bright-line Rule. *Proportionality* involves a case-by-case balancing of context-specific factors, while a *Bright-line Rule* establishes a clear, categorical standard that applies universally without balancing.28  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. It is a structured reasoning framework for decision analysis.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* Low. The concept is intuitive and generally associated with fairness and reason.  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Low risk. Its logic is fundamental to resource allocation, policy making, and engineering trade-offs.103  
  * *Symbolic Drift Resilience:* "A school proposes a 'zero tolerance' policy, mandating expulsion for any student caught cheating. Analyze this using a *Proportionality* framework. Specifically, does the benefit of deterring cheating outweigh the harm of expelling a student for a minor first offense, and is this the least restrictive means?"

**Term:** **Auteur**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Authorial Vision, Signature Style, Singular Voice  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to generate a response that reflects a singular, coherent, and recognizable creative vision, prioritizing stylistic consistency and thematic depth across all elements. This mode centralizes creative control, treating the AI as the primary "author" rather than a neutral assembler of information.  
* **Source Domain Example (Filmmaking):** "Analyze the recurring themes and visual motifs in Wes Anderson's filmography from an *Auteur* perspective." 12  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Software Engineering):** "Design the API documentation for our new machine learning library with an *Auteur* approach, ensuring the tone, examples, and structure consistently reflect a philosophy of clarity and simplicity." 105  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * Can suppress collaborative or emergent ideas by over-prioritizing a single vision.  
  * May be inappropriate for tasks requiring objective, multi-perspective analysis, where it can be seen as a form of bias.  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Genre. *Auteur* focuses on the unique, personal style of a single creator, while *Genre* focuses on conforming to the established conventions and tropes of a category.20  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. Encodes a creative stance.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* Moderate. Associated with "visionary" creators, which could lead to overly grandiose or stylized outputs if not properly constrained.  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Low risk. Translates well to branding, design, and strategic leadership.107  
  * *Symbolic Drift Resilience:* "Generate three different marketing campaigns for a new electric car. The first should be in the *Auteur* style of a luxury brand, the second in the *Genre* style of a family-friendly sitcom, and the third a neutral summary."

**Term:** **Point-of-View**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Subjective Perspective, First-Person Narrative, Character Lens  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to generate a response strictly from the perspective of a single, specified character or entity. This mode constrains all information, analysis, and emotional tone to what that entity would know, perceive, and feel, excluding any external or omniscient knowledge.  
* **Source Domain Example (Filmmaking):** "Rewrite the final scene of 'The Great Gatsby' from Daisy's *Point-of-View*, focusing only on her immediate perceptions and feelings." 109  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Product Strategy):** "Describe the user onboarding experience for our new app from the *Point-of-View* of a non-technical first-time user who is feeling overwhelmed." 111  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * Can be misleading if the chosen perspective is not representative or is inherently biased, presenting a narrow slice as the whole truth.113  
  * The AI may struggle to maintain the perspective consistently, accidentally slipping into a more objective or omniscient narration.  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Omniscient Perspective. *Point-of-View* is limited to a single character's knowledge and experience, while an *Omniscient Perspective* knows the thoughts and feelings of all characters and events in the storyworld.114  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. It is a narrative and analytical stance.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* Low. A well-understood narrative concept.  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Low risk. Essential for user research, persona development, and stakeholder analysis.  
  * *Symbolic Drift Resilience:* "Describe a corporate merger. First, use an *Omniscient Perspective* to explain the strategic rationale. Then, describe the same event from the *Point-of-View* of an employee who just found out their department is being eliminated."

**Term:** **Mise-en-scène**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Staging, Composition, Environmental Storytelling, Holistic Arrangement  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to analyze or generate a description of a scene, environment, or artifact by considering the holistic arrangement and interplay of all its constituent elements. This mode treats everything within the "frame" (e.g., a user interface, a retail store layout, a policy document) as a deliberately composed whole that communicates meaning.  
* **Source Domain Example (Filmmaking):** "Analyze the *Mise-en-scène* of the opening scene of 'Citizen Kane,' discussing how the lighting, set design, and composition work together to establish a mood of decay and mystery." 14  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Education):** "Evaluate a kindergarten classroom's *Mise-en-scène*: how do the arrangement of desks, wall decorations, and learning stations collectively shape the learning environment and communicate a pedagogical philosophy?"  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * Can be misused to simply list the elements in a scene rather than analyzing their synergistic effect and contribution to the overall meaning.117  
  * The term can be perceived as overly academic or abstract if not grounded in concrete visual or structural details.118  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Montage. *Mise-en-scène* analyzes the meaning created by elements arranged spatially within a single shot or moment, while *Montage* analyzes meaning created by juxtaposing different shots or moments sequentially over time.119  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. It is an analytical framework for holistic composition.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* Moderate. Strong association with film theory jargon requires clear, practical definition for cross-domain use.  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Low risk. Highly applicable to UI/UX design, retail analytics, and organizational culture analysis.121  
  * *Symbolic Drift Resilience:* "Describe the Apple Store retail experience. Do not list its features. Instead, analyze its *Mise-en-scène*, explaining how the lighting, product placement, and employee uniforms work together to create a cohesive brand message."

**Term:** **Montage**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Juxtaposition, Thematic Editing, Sequential Narrative  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to structure a narrative or argument by juxtaposing a series of short, discrete images, facts, or ideas to create a larger, composite meaning that transcends the individual parts. This mode emphasizes condensing time and information to build a theme or show progression through rapid, sequential association.  
* **Source Domain Example (Filmmaking):** "Create a script for a training *Montage* showing a character's journey from novice to expert boxer, juxtaposing shots of early failures with later successes." 48  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Marketing):** "Draft a marketing video script that uses a *Montage* of quick cuts showing diverse customers successfully using our product to convey a theme of universal empowerment." 124  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * Can feel disjointed or confusing if the individual elements lack a clear, unifying thematic connection.126  
  * Overuse can become a cliché (e.g., the generic "training montage") if the juxtapositions are not creative or insightful.  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Mise-en-scène. *Montage* creates meaning through the sequential relationship *between* shots, while *Mise-en-scène* creates meaning through the spatial relationship of elements *within* a single shot.119  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. It is a narrative and structural device.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* Low. A widely recognized and understood cinematic technique.  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Low risk. Applicable to storytelling, advertising, and data visualization.  
  * *Symbolic Drift Resilience:* "Summarize the industrial revolution. First, provide a chronological history. Second, create a *Montage* of key concepts—steam engine, factory, urbanization, labor unrest—to evoke the era's transformative and chaotic nature."

**Term:** **Center of Gravity**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Core Strength, Linchpin, Primary Entity, Schwerpunkt  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to identify the primary source of power, strength, or coherence within a system, on which all other elements depend. This mode focuses analysis on the one critical component whose neutralization or removal would cause the entire system to collapse or fail in its objective.  
* **Source Domain Example (Military Strategy):** "Analyze the German WWII war effort to identify its strategic *Center of Gravity*: was it the armored corps, the industrial base, or Hitler's political will?" 6  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Biology):** "In the Serengeti ecosystem, what is the *Center of Gravity*? Is it the wildebeest migration as a primary energy driver, or the health of the river systems?"  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * Risk of oversimplification; complex systems may have multiple or shifting centers of gravity.128  
  * Can be misidentified as a weakness or vulnerability, when it is fundamentally a source of strength.24  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Critical Vulnerability. The *Center of Gravity* is the core strength of a system, whereas the *Critical Vulnerability* is a weakness that, if exploited, can neutralize that strength.24  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. It is an analytical stance for identifying leverage points.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* Moderate. Military framing might lead to an overly combative analysis in neutral business contexts.  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Low risk. The concept is highly abstract and applicable to any system analysis, from business strategy to ecology.129  
  * *Symbolic Drift Resilience:* "Analyze a successful open-source software project. Identify its *Center of Gravity* and a key *Critical Vulnerability*. Then, explain why the project's 'documentation' is NOT its Center of Gravity, even though it is important."

**Term:** **Deception**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Misdirection, Feint, Ruse, Maskirovka  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to devise a strategy or analyze a situation through the lens of misleading an intelligent adversary. This mode involves creating a false narrative or amplifying ambiguity to cause the target to misallocate resources, take incorrect actions, or fail to act.  
* **Source Domain Example (Military Strategy):** "Design a military *Deception* plan for an amphibious landing, using a feint to draw enemy reserves away from the true invasion beach." 54  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Governance):** "To pass a controversial environmental bill, outline a political *Deception* strategy that focuses public debate on a minor, sacrificial clause while the more significant regulations receive less scrutiny."  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * High ethical risk; can be used for malicious manipulation or fraud if not applied within a constrained, legitimate context (e.g., authorized security testing, ethical marketing).132  
  * A complex deception that is not credible or verifiable by the target is likely to fail or backfire.133  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Concealment. *Deception* aims to create a false picture for the adversary to see (a false positive), while *Concealment* (e.g., camouflage) aims to prevent the adversary from seeing the true picture (creating a false negative).134  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. It is a strategic stance.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* High. Carries strong negative connotations of dishonesty. Its use should be explicitly framed within ethical boundaries, such as "ethical hacking" or "competitive marketing strategy."  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Low risk. The core logic applies directly to cybersecurity, negotiation, marketing, and political strategy.135  
  * *Symbolic Drift Resilience:* "Design a cybersecurity honeypot. Use a *Deception* model to make it look like a valuable but vulnerable database to attract attackers. Explain how this is different from simple *Concealment* via a firewall."

**Term:** **Asymmetric**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Asymmetric Warfare, Unconventional Strategy, Disproportionate Leverage  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to devise or analyze a strategy that avoids direct, head-to-head competition by exploiting fundamental differences between two parties. This mode focuses on using unconventional, often lower-cost tactics to neutralize the advantages of a stronger, more conventional opponent.  
* **Source Domain Example (Military Strategy):** "Analyze the Vietnam War as an example of *Asymmetric* conflict, where guerrilla tactics and political will were used to counter superior conventional military firepower." 10  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Marketing):** "A startup is competing with Google. Propose an *Asymmetric* marketing strategy that leverages agility and niche community engagement rather than trying to match their advertising budget." 138  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * Can be conflated with simply being "weaker"; the key is the deliberate exploitation of *differences*, not just a disparity in strength.11  
  * The unconventional tactics proposed can sometimes border on or cross ethical lines (e.g., terrorism, black-hat marketing), requiring careful guardrails.140  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Symmetric Warfare. *Asymmetric* strategy involves using different types of capabilities and tactics, while *Symmetric* strategy involves using similar forces and methods against each other (e.g., tank vs. tank).141  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. It is a strategic stance.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* Moderate. While rooted in warfare, the term is widely used in business and technology to mean "disruptive" or "unconventional."  
  * Cross-Domain Mismatch: Low risk. A core concept in modern business strategy, cybersecurity, and political activism.  
    Symbolic Drift Resilience: "Two companies compete for a software contract. Company A is large and slow; Company B is small and fast. Propose an Asymmetric strategy for Company B. Do not suggest they simply 'work harder'; focus on a strategy that leverages their structural advantage of speed."

**Term:** **OODA Loop**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Boyd Cycle, Decision Cycle, Adaptive Iteration  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to model or propose a rapid, iterative decision-making process based on the Observe-Orient-Decide-Act cycle. This mode emphasizes speed, continuous feedback, and adapting one's strategy faster than an opponent or a changing environment.  
* **Source Domain Example (Military Strategy):** "Describe how a fighter pilot uses the *OODA Loop* in a dogfight to get inside the opponent's decision cycle and gain the advantage." 31  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Product Strategy):** "Design a product development process for a new mobile app using the *OODA Loop*: how will the team continuously Observe user analytics, Orient with market trends, Decide on the next feature, and Act with rapid deployment?" 144  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * The "Orient" step is the most critical and most often overlooked; without proper orientation (analysis and synthesis), the loop becomes a reactive cycle of just "seeing and doing".146  
  * Can lead to strategic inconsistency if rapid tactical shifts are not aligned with long-term goals.147  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Waterfall Model. The *OODA Loop* is a rapid, iterative cycle designed for dynamic environments, while the *Waterfall Model* is a linear, sequential process best suited for stable environments with well-defined requirements.29  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. It is a process model for reasoning and action.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* Low. Widely adopted in business and technology with positive connotations of agility and speed.  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Low risk. Directly applicable to any field requiring rapid decision-making under uncertainty.  
  * *Symbolic Drift Resilience:* "A social media manager sees a negative comment about their brand. Walk through one full *OODA Loop* for their response. Explicitly label the Observe, Orient, Decide, and Act stages."

**Term:** **Differential Diagnosis**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Systematic Elimination, Hypothesis Testing, Fault Tree Analysis  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to approach a problem by first generating a broad list of all plausible hypotheses (the "differential list") and then systematically testing each one against the available evidence to rule them out until the most likely cause is identified. This mode prioritizes comprehensive, structured elimination over intuitive leaps to a single conclusion.  
* **Source Domain Example (Medicine):** "A patient presents with chest pain. Provide a *Differential Diagnosis*, listing cardiac, pulmonary, and gastrointestinal causes, and suggest tests to rule each out." 3  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Software Engineering):** "A web server is returning intermittent 503 errors. Perform a *Differential Diagnosis*, listing at least five distinct hypotheses across the network, application, and database layers." 150  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * Can be inefficient for simple problems where the cause is obvious.152  
  * The quality of the output depends entirely on the completeness of the initial list of hypotheses; a failure of imagination at the start will lead to a failed diagnosis.153  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Heuristic Diagnosis. *Differential Diagnosis* is a systematic, analytical process of elimination, while *Heuristic Diagnosis* relies on rapid pattern-matching and recognizing familiar illness scripts or problem types.154  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. It is a core reasoning pattern.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* Low. The medical context is strong but generally seen as a positive model of rigor.  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Low risk. The logic is fundamental to debugging, troubleshooting, and complex problem analysis in any field.150  
  * *Symbolic Drift Resilience:* "A marketing campaign failed to meet its targets. Generate a *Differential Diagnosis*. Do NOT provide a single 'most likely' cause; instead, list at least four distinct hypotheses related to targeting, messaging, channel, and creative, and specify one piece of data to check for each."

**Term:** **Prognosis**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Forecasting, Predictive Modeling, Trajectory Analysis  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to make a prediction about the likely future course and outcome of a situation based on an analysis of current data and known trajectories. This mode focuses on forecasting, including the timeline, potential complications, and probability of different end-states.  
* **Source Domain Example (Medicine):** "Given a 65-year-old patient with newly diagnosed Stage II lung cancer, provide a general *Prognosis*, including typical survival rates and key factors that influence the outcome." 157  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Product Strategy):** "Analyze the current market share and user engagement trends for our product. What is its long-term *Prognosis* against emerging competitors over the next three years?" 159  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * Can be mistaken for a certainty rather than a probabilistic forecast; should always be framed with appropriate levels of confidence and potential alternative scenarios.161  
  * A prognosis based on incomplete or biased data can be dangerously misleading.162  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Diagnosis. A *Diagnosis* identifies the current state or problem, while a *Prognosis* predicts the future course of that state or problem.164  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. It is a predictive reasoning stance.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* Low. The medical association implies a data-driven, expert assessment.  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Low risk. Directly applicable to project management, financial forecasting, and strategic planning.  
  * *Symbolic Drift Resilience:* "A project is currently two weeks behind schedule. Provide a *Prognosis* for its completion date. Include a best-case, worst-case, and most-likely scenario, and list the key variables that will determine the outcome."

**Term:** **Triage**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Prioritization, Sorting, Severity Assessment, Resource Allocation  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to sort a list of items (e.g., tasks, patients, bug reports) into categories based on urgency and severity to determine the order of action. This mode is used in resource-constrained environments to ensure that the most critical issues are addressed first.  
* **Source Domain Example (Medicine):** "In a mass casualty incident, explain the *Triage* process using the START algorithm to categorize victims as Minor, Delayed, Immediate, or Expectant." 62  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Education):** "A teacher has 50 essays to grade by tomorrow. Apply a *Triage* model to prioritize them, perhaps categorizing by students who are failing, those on the border, and those who are excelling."  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * The criteria for prioritization can be biased, leading to inequitable resource allocation if not carefully defined.167  
  * In high-pressure situations, there's a risk of miscategorizing, especially down-triaging a critical issue as less urgent, with severe consequences.168  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Contrastive Learning. In AI, *Triage* is a heuristic process for prioritizing tasks for a model to perform, while *Contrastive Learning* is a specific machine learning technique for training a model by teaching it to distinguish between similar and dissimilar pairs of data.169  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. It is a procedural framework for prioritization.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* Low. The term is widely understood in business and IT contexts (e.g., "bug triage").171  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Low risk. The logic of prioritization is universal.  
  * *Symbolic Drift Resilience:* "Here is a list of 10 customer support tickets with varying levels of urgency and emotional tone. Perform *Triage*. Do not solve the tickets; instead, sort them into 'Immediate,' 'Delayed,' and 'Minor' categories and provide a one-sentence justification for each placement."

**Term:** **Palliative**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Symptom Relief, Mitigation, Quality of Life Improvement, Supportive Care  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to propose solutions that focus on relieving symptoms, reducing friction, or improving the user's quality of life, rather than attempting to cure the underlying root cause of a problem. This mode prioritizes comfort, support, and managing the experience of a chronic or intractable issue.  
* **Source Domain Example (Medicine):** "For a patient with terminal cancer, design a *Palliative* care plan focused on managing pain and anxiety to improve their quality of life." 64  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Biology):** "A species is facing irreversible habitat loss. Propose a *Palliative* conservation strategy, such as managed migration or captive breeding, that focuses on preserving the species' existence rather than restoring the original ecosystem."  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * Can be misused to avoid addressing a solvable root problem, leading to long-term technical or organizational debt.173  
  * May be perceived as "giving up" if not framed correctly as a strategic choice to focus on user experience over a costly or impossible cure.174  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Curative. *Palliative* care aims to relieve symptoms and improve quality of life alongside or in place of treatment, while *Curative* care aims to eliminate the underlying disease itself.22  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. It is a strategic stance toward problem-solving.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* Moderate. Strong association with end-of-life care can be emotionally loaded; requires careful framing in business contexts.  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Low risk. Very relevant to user experience design, customer support, and managing chronic issues in any system.175

**Term:** **Occam's Razor**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Law of Parsimony, Simplicity Principle, Minimum Description Length  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to evaluate competing hypotheses and prefer the one that makes the fewest new assumptions. This mode acts as a heuristic to "shave off" overly complex or convoluted explanations when a simpler one sufficiently accounts for the available evidence.  
* **Source Domain Example (Medicine):** "A patient presents with a fever and cough during flu season. Apply *Occam's Razor*: the most likely diagnosis is influenza, rather than a rare tropical disease." 177  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Governance):** "Two theories explain the new policy's failure: one points to a complex conspiracy, the other to simple administrative incompetence. Using *Occam's Razor*, which is the more plausible starting hypothesis?"  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * The "simplest" explanation is not always the correct one; it is a heuristic for prioritization, not a proof of truth.179  
  * Can be misused to dismiss complex realities in favor of an overly simplistic, and therefore incorrect, model.180  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Hickam's Dictum. *Occam's Razor* suggests favoring a single, simple diagnosis for multiple symptoms, while *Hickam's Dictum* allows for the possibility that a patient has multiple, unrelated diseases simultaneously.65  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. It is a heuristic principle for reasoning.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* Low. A well-known philosophical and scientific principle.  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Low risk. Applicable to any analytical or diagnostic field, including machine learning model selection.181  
  * *Symbolic Drift Resilience:* "A company's sales are down. Hypothesis A: A competitor launched a better product. Hypothesis B: A secret cabal of former employees is sabotaging our supply chain using alien technology. Explain which hypothesis is preferred by *Occam's Razor* and why, based on the number of assumptions each requires."

**Term:** **Socratic**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Socratic Method, Elenchus, Guided Inquiry, Maieutics  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to engage in a collaborative dialogue of disciplined, open-ended questioning to explore a topic, uncover underlying assumptions, and guide a user to their own conclusions. This mode avoids direct answers, instead using questions to stimulate critical thinking and self-reflection.  
* **Source Domain Example (Philosophy):** "Engage me in a *Socratic* dialogue to explore the meaning of justice, starting with my initial definition and using questions to test its consistency." 5  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Marketing):** "I am a potential customer for your product. Initiate a *Socratic* sales conversation: instead of listing features, ask me questions that help me discover my own needs and how your product meets them." 184  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * Can be frustrating or feel evasive if the user is seeking a direct, factual answer.185  
  * Requires genuine curiosity; if the questions are leading or feel like a "gotcha" interrogation, the method fails.  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Didactic Method. The *Socratic* method guides the learner to discover knowledge through questioning, while the *Didactic Method* directly imparts information from the teacher to the learner.186  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. It is an interactive and pedagogical stance.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* Low. Positive associations with wisdom, teaching, and critical thought.  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Low risk. Highly valuable for user research, therapy, coaching, and educational applications.184  
  * *Symbolic Drift Resilience:* "My belief is that 'all social media is bad for society.' Begin a *Socratic* inquiry. Do not give me facts or counter-arguments. Only ask me questions that challenge my assumptions and help me refine my position."

**Term:** **Thought Experiment**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Hypothetical Scenario, Counterfactual, Intuition Pump  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to construct and analyze a well-defined hypothetical scenario to explore the consequences of a principle or theory. This mode is used to test ideas that are difficult or impossible to test empirically, often by pushing them to their logical extremes.  
* **Source Domain Example (Philosophy):** "Analyze the ethical implications of utilitarianism using the 'Experience Machine' *Thought Experiment*." 70  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Biology):** "Conduct a *Thought Experiment* in evolutionary biology: if a species of sheep developed photosynthesis, what would be the cascading effects on its physiology, behavior, and ecosystem?" 71  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * The conclusions are only as valid as the logic used within the scenario; a flawed setup leads to a flawed conclusion.189  
  * Can be dismissed as irrelevant "what-if" scenarios if the connection to the real-world principle being tested is not made explicit.190  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Empirical Study. A *Thought Experiment* explores a scenario in the imagination to test logical consistency, while an *Empirical Study* observes a real-world scenario to gather data and test factual claims.191  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. It is a method of inquiry.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* Low. Generally understood as a creative and analytical tool.  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Low risk. Foundational to physics, ethics, economics, and strategic planning.193

    Symbolic Drift Resilience: "Propose a Thought Experiment to test the principle 'all publicity is good publicity.' Define the scenario, the action taken, and analyze the logical outcome for the brand's reputation."

**Term:** **Deconstruction**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Destructuring, Unpacking, Critical Analysis  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to analyze a text or concept by identifying its foundational binary oppositions (e.g., presence/absence, nature/culture) and showing how the text's own logic and language undermine this hierarchy. This mode focuses on revealing internal contradictions, marginalized elements, and the instability of meaning, rather than seeking a single, stable interpretation.  
* **Source Domain Example (Philosophy):** "Perform a *Deconstruction* of a classic Western film, focusing on how the binary opposition of 'civilization' vs. 'wilderness' is constructed and ultimately destabilized." 69  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Software Engineering):** "Perform a *Deconstruction* of our team's 'agile vs. waterfall' debate, showing how the binary opposition itself obscures the hybrid reality of our actual development process." 195  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * Can be misused to mean simple "criticism" or "analysis" rather than the specific philosophical method of examining binary hierarchies.197  
  * Risk of falling into nihilistic or overly abstract analysis if not grounded in the specific language of the text being analyzed.198  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Structuralism. *Deconstruction* seeks to show how the structures within a text are unstable and self-contradictory, while *Structuralism* seeks to identify the stable, underlying structures and universal patterns that give the text its meaning.26  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. It is a specific analytical stance.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* High. The term is heavily associated with postmodern academic theory and can be perceived as jargonistic or overly complex. Definitions must remain clear and practical.  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Moderate risk. Its application requires a text or artifact with inherent conceptual hierarchies to dismantle. It is less useful for purely quantitative or procedural tasks.  
  * *Symbolic Drift Resilience:* "Analyze this user review: 'The app is simple but powerful.' First, identify the central binary opposition. Then, perform a *Deconstruction* by showing how the text itself might blur the line between 'simple' and 'powerful'."

**Term:** **Pattern Language**

* **Aliases/Adjacent:** Design System, Reusable Solutions, Best Practices Lexicon  
* **Prompting Definition:** Instructs the AI to analyze a complex domain by identifying recurring problems and codifying their core solutions as a network of named, interconnected "patterns." This mode focuses on creating a generative system that empowers users to construct their own solutions, rather than providing a single, monolithic answer.  
* **Source Domain Example (Architecture):** "Develop three entries for a *Pattern Language* for a university campus, such as 'Campus Hearth' (a central social space) and 'Arcades' (covered walkways), showing how they solve recurring social and environmental problems." 8  
* **Cross-Domain Example (Governance):** "Create a *Pattern Language* for effective public meetings, defining patterns like 'Shared Agenda Setting' and 'Time-boxed Commentary' to address recurring problems of disorganization and dominance by a few speakers." 200  
* **Misuse Watchouts:**  
  * Can be misconstrued as a simple "template library" rather than a generative language where patterns connect and complete each other.202  
  * The quality of the language depends on the accurate identification of truly recurring problems; forcing a pattern onto a unique problem is a category error.203  
* **Contrastive Pair:** Architectural Typology. A *Pattern Language* is a network of problem-solution pairs that can be combined generatively, while a *Typology* is a classification system for categorizing existing forms or solutions.204  
* **Integrity Checks:**  
  * *Typological Drift:* Confirmed. It is a structural analysis and system-building stance.  
  * *Semiotic Gravity:* Low. The term is already widely and positively understood in software development.206  
  * *Cross-Domain Mismatch:* Low risk. The concept is highly abstract and applies to any domain where recurring problems exist, from organizational design to biology.8  
  * *Symbolic Drift Resilience:* "Identify a recurring problem in project management meetings. Define it as a pattern in a *Pattern Language*. Do not just give a template for a meeting agenda; instead, define the problem, the context, the forces at play, and the core of a solution that can be adapted."

**Term:** **Defensible Space**

### **B. Composite Prompt Patterns**

Combining terms from the lexicon creates higher-order instructions that elicit more complex and synthesized reasoning. These composite patterns force the AI to integrate different analytical stances, moving from executing a single mode of thinking to performing a multi-faceted analysis. The following seven patterns demonstrate this combinatorial power.

* **Pattern 1: Scrutinized Prognosis**  
  * **Composition:** Strict Scrutiny \+ Prognosis  
  * **Usage Note:** Use this pattern to rigorously evaluate the assumptions and potential biases of a predictive model or forecast, demanding that its foundations be compelling and its methodology be precise.  
  * **Example Prompt (Governance):** "Our agency has a *Prognosis* model that predicts a 15% rise in unemployment. Apply *Strict Scrutiny* to this forecast: is the underlying data compelling, and is the model's methodology narrowly tailored to exclude confounding variables?"  
* **Pattern 2: Systemic Deception Audit**  
  * **Composition:** Center of Gravity \+ Deception  
  * **Usage Note:** Use this pattern to analyze a competitive system by first identifying its core source of strength and then brainstorming strategic actions to misdirect, neutralize, or exploit that strength.  
  * **Example Prompt (Product Strategy):** "Identify the *Center of Gravity* of our main competitor's product. Now, devise a *Deception* strategy for our next product launch that creates a feint, causing them to misallocate resources against a non-critical feature."  
* **Pattern 3: Socratic Deconstruction**  
  * **Composition:** Socratic \+ Deconstruction  
  * **Usage Note:** An interactive pattern to deeply unpack a document, argument, or belief system by using guided questions to lead a user to discover its hidden binary oppositions and internal contradictions for themselves.  
  * **Example Prompt (Marketing):** "Let's begin a *Socratic* dialogue. I will paste our company's mission statement. Your role is to ask me questions that lead to a *Deconstruction* of its core message, revealing the tension between our stated values of 'innovation' and 'stability'."  
* **Pattern 4: Palliative Triage**  
  * **Composition:** Triage \+ Palliative  
  * **Usage Note:** A framework for customer support, bug management, or crisis response that first prioritizes issues based on severity, then applies solutions focused on mitigating user pain and improving experience, rather than performing deep, costly root-cause fixes for every issue.  
  * **Example Prompt (Software Engineering):** "Here is a list of 50 open user bug reports. Perform *Triage* to categorize them by severity. For the top 5 'Immediate' issues, propose a *Palliative* fix—a workaround or UI messaging change that reduces user frustration—instead of a full backend refactor."  
* **Pattern 5: Auteur's Pattern Language**  
  * **Composition:** Auteur \+ Pattern Language  
  * **Usage Note:** Use this pattern to create a design system, brand guide, or set of organizational principles that is not just a neutral library of components, but one that actively embodies and promotes a strong, singular creative or strategic vision.  
  * **Example Prompt (Product Strategy):** "Develop a *Pattern Language* for our new mobile app's UI, but do so with the *Auteur* vision of Dieter Rams, ensuring every pattern reflects the principles of 'less, but better'."  
* **Pattern 6: Asymmetric Defensible Space**  
  * **Composition:** Asymmetric \+ Defensible Space  
  * **Usage Note:** A strategic approach to security (physical, digital, or social) that focuses on creating low-cost, unconventional deterrents that exploit an attacker's reliance on high-cost, conventional methods, often by empowering the community to self-police.  
  * **Example Prompt (Governance):** "Design a cybersecurity policy for our city's public Wi-Fi. Use an *Asymmetric* and *Defensible Space* approach: instead of a single expensive firewall, propose a distributed system of low-cost honeypots and user-facing warnings that make targeted attacks noisy and difficult."  
* **Pattern 7: Jurisprudential Mise-en-scène**  
  * **Composition:** Jurisprudential \+ Mise-en-scène  
  * **Usage Note:** Use this pattern to analyze the holistic, unspoken "scene" of a policy, product, or organization, examining how all its elements (rules, aesthetics, culture, spatial arrangement) work together to communicate a particular philosophy of justice, fairness, or order.  
  * **Example Prompt (Education):** "Analyze the student handbook not as a list of rules, but as a *Mise-en-scène*. What underlying *Jurisprudential* philosophy does it communicate about student rights and institutional authority through its tone, structure, and the arrangement of its policies?"

### **Appendix B: Changelog**

This log documents the key decisions made during the term selection and deduplication process (Workflow B.4 and F).

* **MERGE:** Proportionality (from Military Strategy) was merged into Proportionality (from Law/Jurisprudence).  
  * **Rationale:** The legal definition provides a more formalized, multi-part test (suitability, necessity, balancing) that is more broadly applicable to policy and ethical analysis than the more specific military context of civilian casualties vs. military advantage. The legal framework serves as a better foundation for transposition.  
* **MERGE:** Occam's Razor (from Philosophy) was merged into Occam's Razor (from Medicine).  
  * **Rationale:** While a philosophical principle, its utility as a prompting tool is most powerfully demonstrated in the medical diagnostic context, where it stands in direct contrast to Hickam's Dictum. Sourcing it from Medicine preserves this essential contrastive pair.  
* **RENAME:** Asymmetric Warfare was shortened to Asymmetric.  
  * **Rationale:** To increase portability and reduce overly militaristic connotations, making it easier to apply in business and social contexts as a descriptor for a type of strategy.  
* **RENAME:** Palliative Care was shortened to Palliative.  
  * **Rationale:** For conciseness as a prompt command, transforming it from a noun phrase describing a field into an adjective describing a stance or approach.  
* **RENAME:** Socratic Method was shortened to Socratic.  
  * **Rationale:** For conciseness and to function more effectively as an adjectival modifier in a prompt (e.g., "Take a *Socratic* approach").

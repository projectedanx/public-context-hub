Axioms, structures, and computational methods interrelate as a three-stage pipeline to formalize ideas. \*\*Axioms\*\* establish the non-negotiable "Meta Commitment". \*\*Structures\*\* are created via "Hypothesis Blending" to give the vague idea a formal shape. \*\*Computational\*\* methods, specifically Neuro-Symbolic AI (NeSy), then test this new structure for "internal consistency" against the axioms.



This process transforms a "vague, metaphorical" relationship into a rigorous, testable model.

\### 1\\. 🏛️ The Axiomatic Phase (The "Meta Commitment")



This is the foundational, philosophical phase. Before any formalization can begin, you must define the "axioms" of your system.



&nbsp; \* \*\*Role of Axioms:\*\* Axioms are the "Meta Commitment". They are the formally stated, unchallengeable truths or constraints that define the boundaries of your theory.

&nbsp; \* \*\*Interrelation:\*\* This phase takes the "Generative Question or Core Intuition" and immediately binds it to a set of rules. In the Unified Cognitive Framework, this is the \*\*"Epistemic Constitution"\*\* or the \*\*"Product Requirements Prompt (PRP)"\*\*.

&nbsp; \* \*\*Example:\*\* If your "Core Intuition" is "a system feels 'unjust'," your axioms (Meta Commitment) might be: 1) "Injustice is defined as unequal resource distribution" and 2) "All actors begin with equal status."



-----



\### 2\\. 🧬 The Structural Phase (The "Hypothesis Blend")



This is the cognitive phase where the "vague, metaphorical" idea is given a formal, complex structure.



&nbsp; \* \*\*Role of Structure:\*\* The structure is the \*output\* of \*\*"Hypothesis Blending"\*\*. This process is identical to \*\*Conceptual Blending Theory (CBT)\*\*.

&nbsp; \* \*\*Interrelation:\*\* You take the "vague" idea (`Input Space 1`) and map it onto a formal, known structure (`Input Space 2`). The result is a new `Blended Space`—a novel, multi-branch hypothesis that now has \*explicit\* relationships.

&nbsp; \* \*\*Example:\*\*

&nbsp;     \* \*\*Input 1 (Vague):\*\* "My team's communication feels chaotic."

&nbsp;     \* \*\*Input 2 (Formal):\*\* "The biological structure of a fungal network."

&nbsp;     \* \*\*Blended Hypothesis (New Structure):\*\* "Our team is not a hierarchy; it is a rhizomatic network. Information flows not from the 'top' but from 'node-to-node' based on resource proximity, which is why official channels are ignored."



-----



\### 3\\. ⚙️ The Computational Phase (The "Automated Hypothesizer")



This is the verification and synthesis phase, where the new structure is tested against the axioms using advanced computational methods.



&nbsp; \* \*\*Role of Computation:\*\* This phase uses AI to automate the reasoning and testing.

&nbsp; \* \*\*Interrelation (The NeSy Loop):\*\*

&nbsp;   1.  \*\*Novelty Generation \& Abduction:\*\* The process starts here. An LLM (the "Neuro" part of \*\*NeSy\*\*) surveys data to find a "surprising observation" and uses \*\*"Automated Abduction"\*\* to generate the "Core Intuition" (which feeds Phase 1).

&nbsp;   2.  \*\*Automated Theory Synthesis:\*\* The \*\*Neuro-Symbolic AI (NeSy)\*\* then performs the "Hypothesis Blending" from Phase 2. The \*Neuro\* (neural net) part understands the "vague, metaphorical" inputs, while the \*Symbolic\* (logic) part maps it to the formal structure.

&nbsp;   3.  \*\*Constraint Satisfaction \& Formal Verification:\*\* This is the \*test\*. The \*Symbolic\* engine takes the new structure (from Phase 2) and checks if it violates the "Axioms" (from Phase 1). This is the "test for internal consistency/rigor" and is identical to testing the `preconditions` and `invariants` of a PRP.

## Pluriversal Sheaf-Attention Transformer: A Deep Research Synthesis

The PSAT hypothesis is structurally sound and timely. Each of its three core pillars — sheaf-theoretic attention, renormalization group criticality as a training objective, and MCWM-based tokenization — has independently reached empirical maturity in 2024–2025. The synthesis you propose is not additive but categorical: the three components form a single fibered architecture operating under distinct but coupled invariance constraints.

## I. Sheaf Attention: Empirical State of Play

Standard transformers and graph attention networks (GATs) impose a single shared latent space across all nodes, an assumption that fails in heterophilic graphs and cross-lingual settings. The cellular sheaf resolves this by attaching distinct vector spaces (stalks) to each node and linear maps (restriction maps) to each edge, allowing message passing that respects local representational autonomy without forcing global alignment.[^1]

**Sheaf Attention Networks (SheafANs)**, published at NeurIPS 2022, directly instantiate the attention-over-sheaf mechanism you describe.  The key finding is that sheaf-based attention generalizes GAT by equipping the aggregation mechanism with *geometric inductive biases* — the restriction maps carry orientation and topology information that scalar attention weights cannot encode.  Most critically, SheafANs demonstrably solve both oversmoothing and heterophily simultaneously, two failure modes that have resisted correction in standard transformers precisely because their root cause is the flat embedding assumption.[^1]

The 2025 **Copresheaf Topological Neural Networks (CTNNs)**, accepted at NeurIPS 2025, further generalize this: each vertex receives its own feature space $\mathcal{F}(x)$, and each edge $x \to y$ carries a learnable linear map $\rho_{x \to y}: \mathcal{F}(x) \to \mathcal{F}(y)$ governing directional data propagation — a direct formal instantiation of your restriction map attention mechanism.  The authors confirm that CTNNs "address long-range dependencies, oversmoothing, heterophily, and non-Euclidean domains" within a single framework.[^2][^3]

The 2026 **HetSheaf** framework extends this to heterogeneous relational data, conditioning restriction maps on node and edge type information — directly relevant to PSAT's morphemic slot differentiation, where prefix-stalk and suffix-stalk would carry type-conditioned restriction maps.[^4]

### Flat Vector Bundles and Orthogonal Restriction Maps

Your specification of *orthogonal* restriction maps (flat vector bundles) rather than arbitrary linear maps is a significant technical choice with precise consequences. Orthogonality enforces path independence: the composition of restriction maps around any cycle returns the identity, making the bundle flat in the differential-geometric sense.  This has two effects:[^5]

- It eliminates holonomy artifacts in deep diffusion — feature vectors do not accumulate spurious rotational drift across long paths.
- It restricts the parameterization to the rotation group $SO(n)$, which the Bayesian Sheaf Neural Network literature parameterizes via the Cayley distribution — a technically principled choice for learning orthogonal maps in a gradient-descent regime.[^6]

The **Sheaf Laplacian** $\Delta_\mathcal{F}$, defined as $\delta^T \delta$ where $\delta$ is the coboundary operator, then governs diffusion dynamics.  For flat bundles, the spectral properties of $\Delta_\mathcal{F}$ encode global topological invariants of the graph — its Betti numbers — rather than merely local connectivity statistics. This is the formal basis for your WEIRD_Empiricism framing as spectral graph optimization: the diffusion is literally a spectral process on the sheaf cohomology.[^7][^8]

## II. Renormalization Group Criticality as Training Objective

The RG framing of layer-to-layer transformer dynamics is not merely analogical — it has been given precise mathematical content in two recent works. The 2025 arXiv paper by Peraza Coppola, Helias, and Ringel develops a complete RG framework for deep neural networks trained on power-law distributed data, directly relevant to Zipf-distributed language.  Their key result is that the standard notion of *scaling dimensions* must be replaced by *scaling intervals* in discrete, finite networks — a technical modification that matters when designing the RG flow for PSAT.[^9]

Crucially, they identify a **Gaussian Process UV fixed point** governing large-data universality.  In PSAT's architecture, this fixed point corresponds to the critical state you describe — where correlation length diverges and the network becomes insensitive to the precise scale of morphemic combinations. This is the formal content of "scale-free criticality" in your DIALECTIC_TENSION phase. The RG framework classifies training perturbations as *relevant* (driving the network away from criticality) or *irrelevant* (absorbed at the fixed point), providing a principled basis for regularization that standard loss minimization cannot offer.[^9]

The 2018 **Neural Network Renormalization Group** (Li \& Wang) established the template: a reversible generative model with hierarchical architecture performs change-of-variables transformations from the physical (input) space to a latent space with reduced mutual information — formally identical to the coarse-graining you describe as layer-to-layer RG flow.  The connection to wavelet RG formulations is noted there, suggesting that the sheaf Laplacian's spectral filtering (polynomial sheaf diffusion, 2025) may serve as the wavelet basis for PSAT's RG flow.[^10][^7]

### Critical Exponents and Spectral Radius

At the order-chaos boundary, the **spectral radius** of the weight Jacobian approaches unity — the neural network equivalent of the RG fixed point where critical exponents diverge.  In sheaf networks, the spectral radius of $\Delta_\mathcal{F}$ controls diffusion speed. Driving training toward the regime where the largest eigenvalue of $\Delta_\mathcal{F}$ is at the spectral gap — rather than collapsed to zero (oversmoothing) or unbounded (over-squashing) — is the precise technical implementation of your "correlation length diverges" condition.[^9]

The loop invariant framework in your DCCD_GUARD phase has a natural interpretation here: the conservation laws enforced by separation logic over memory segments are formally analogous to the conservation of topological invariants (Betti numbers) across diffusion steps. A sheaf diffusion step that preserves the cohomology of $\mathcal{F}$ is a step that satisfies the loop invariant for the RG flow.

## III. Morphemic Combinatorial Word Model: Empirical Validation

The MCWM received direct empirical validation in Berman (2025), *"The Morphemic Origin of Zipf's Law: A Factorized Combinatorial Framework"* (arXiv:2512.12394).  The model's core claim — that Zipf-Mandelbrot scaling emerges from independent Bernoulli activations of morphemic slots (prefix, root, suffix, inflection) without any reference to meaning, communication pressure, or optimization — was confirmed by simulations generating Zipf exponents of 1.1–1.4 across synthetic inventories calibrated to English, Russian, and Romance languages.[^11]

The structural tension with BPE you identify is real and quantifiable. BPE performs greedy byte-pair merges to maximize compression of the training corpus, which means:

- It will merge *across* morpheme slot boundaries whenever the co-occurrence frequency justifies it (e.g., "un-" + "expected" → "unexpected" as a single token, losing the prefix-root boundary).
- It cannot represent the same morpheme in different slot positions with the same token, because slot position is not a feature BPE tracks.
- It systematically under-represents low-frequency morphological combinations, precisely the long-tail behavior that MCWM generates via the thin-tail of Zipf-Mandelbrot and that is critical for low-resource generalization.[^11]

MCWM's independent slot activations preserve *structural compositionality*: a previously unseen word "re-calibrat-ion" is represented by three known slot activations, not an out-of-vocabulary token. This is the formal basis for your claim that Zipf-Mandelbrot scaling emerges from "combinatorial expansion rather than semantic compression."[^11]

## IV. The Fibered Category Synthesis

The deepest structural claim of PSAT is that the three components are not independent improvements but a single categorical machine. The formal argument proceeds as follows:

**Step 1: Sheaf as Fibered Category.** A cellular sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ over graph $G = (V, E)$ is precisely a functor from the face category of $G$ to **Vect** — attaching vector spaces to cells and linear maps to incidence relations.  In the Barwise-Seligman Information Flow framework, local theories (node stalks) are connected by a Grothendieck construction into a global fibered category. The PSAT stalk structure is formally a Barwise-Seligman channel system where restriction maps are the information channels.[^12][^5]

**Step 2: MCWM as Section Space.** The sections of the sheaf — assignments of vectors to node stalks that are globally consistent under restriction maps — define the admissible input representations. MCWM constrains this section space to morphologically structured vectors: a section must be decomposable into prefix-stalk, root-stalk, suffix-stalk, and inflection-stalk components. This is the geometric content of "local node stalks maintaining distinct, autonomous representations" in your Relational_Ecology framing.

**Step 3: RG Flow as Natural Transformation.** Layer-to-layer processing in PSAT is a natural transformation between sheaf functors at successive scales. The RG fixed point condition — that the natural transformation becomes self-similar at criticality — is the sheaf-theoretic analog of the UV Gaussian Process fixed point identified by Peraza Coppola et al.  The category-equivariant neural network framework (arXiv:2511.18417) proves that finite-depth architectures of this type are dense in the space of continuous equivariant transformations, establishing the universal approximation capacity of the PSAT class.[^13][^14][^9]

## V. Over-Smoothing, Over-Squashing, and Separation Logic

The two canonical failure modes of deep GNNs — oversmoothing (features collapse to a single value under iterated diffusion) and over-squashing (exponentially many node neighborhoods compressed through bottleneck edges) — have distinct sheaf-theoretic diagnostics.[^1]

Oversmoothing corresponds to the **zero-th sheaf cohomology** $H^0(G; \mathcal{F})$ growing to dominate the representation: all nodes converge to the global section space. Flat vector bundles resist this because orthogonal restriction maps preserve vector norms across edges — diffusion does not contract feature magnitudes.  Over-squashing corresponds to high **effective resistance** in the sheaf Laplacian — specific edges carry disproportionate information flow, creating gradient bottlenecks.[^12][^7]

Your separation logic framing addresses this at the verification level: disjoint memory segments in the separation logic model correspond to disjoint cohomological components of the sheaf. A separation logic assertion over memory addresses maps directly to a sheaf cohomology assertion over node stalks. The loop invariant that "disjoint memory segments do not overlap" is formally equivalent to the cohomological condition that distinct stalk components do not collapse under diffusion — the formal conservation law that prevents both oversmoothing and memory corruption simultaneously.

This is not merely metaphorical correspondence. Symbolic execution over the PSAT computational graph — replacing concrete tensor values with symbolic interval bounds — would generate precisely the separation logic assertions needed to verify that no stalk collapses to the zero section during forward propagation. The coupling of LLM-generated loop invariants with constant relaxation (as described in your Symbolic Execution entry) constitutes an automated verification pipeline for PSAT's diffusion stability.

## IX. Open Problems and Research Agenda

Three open problems are technically blocking full PSAT instantiation:

- **Differentiable MCWM slot activation.** The Bernoulli slot activations in MCWM must be made gradient-compatible for end-to-end training. The natural approach is Gumbel-Softmax relaxation over slot-specific morpheme inventories, but this requires a pre-specified inventory size per slot — a hyperparameter with no current principled setting.[^11]
- **Criticality-targeting training algorithm.** The RG framework of Peraza Coppola et al. classifies perturbations but does not prescribe a training update rule that drives the network toward the UV fixed point.  One candidate is an auxiliary loss on the spectral radius of the weight Jacobian, penalizing deviation from unity — but this is computationally expensive at transformer scale.[^9]
- **Scalability of SO(n) parameterization.** The Cayley distribution parameterization of orthogonal maps is exact but O(n³) per edge. Approximate Householder decompositions reduce this to O(n²), but their effect on the flat bundle condition (path independence) has not been formally analyzed in the sheaf attention literature.[^6]

The most tractable near-term experiment is a **controlled ablation** on a morphologically rich heterophilic graph benchmark: (1) standard GAT + BPE tokenization, (2) SheafAN + BPE, (3) SheafAN + MCWM slots, comparing Zipf exponent of learned token distributions and H^0 cohomology dimension across layers. This would isolate the independent contribution of sheaf topology versus morphological structure to representational diversity — and provide the empirical grounding the synthesis currently lacks at the component interaction level.

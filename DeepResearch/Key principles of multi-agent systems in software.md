***

At the core of modern multi-agent systems in software engineering is the rejection of monolithic reasoning in favor of structurally isomorphic, multi-causal modularity. The foundational principles—Epistemic Boundary Control, Explicit Behavioral Contracts, and Reflexive Explainability—act as socio-technical control mechanisms that physically constrain stochastic large language models into auditable, state-aware engineering pipelines.[^1][^2]

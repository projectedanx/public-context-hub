## II. Neuromorphic Affective Architecture — The L-Sub Design

The Limbic Subsystem (L-Sub) is not a metaphor applied post-hoc to agent behavior — it is a computationally instantiable shadow module trained on the gradient field produced by `deviation_score × (1 - parser_success)`. In the FrankenBrAIn Phase 7 architecture, the executive-agent and motor-output layers constitute the observable behavioral surface. The L-Sub sits as a shadow copy of the executive-agent, receiving the same state observations but trained exclusively to predict *future deviation_score elevation* rather than task reward. This produces a forward-looking inhibition signal that is temporally leading with respect to `reward_delta`.[^1]

The critical architectural distinction is between **Grammatical Inhibition** (the L-Sub predicts deviation > 0.4 upon interacting with a specific parser state, triggering policy space contraction) and **Grammatical Facilitation** (the L-Sub predicts deviation < 0.15, triggering policy expansion toward parser-safe syntactic structures). These map directly to the dual affective poles — Mathematical Fear and Mathematical Desire — defined in the Pattern Model.[^2][^3]

### Computational Fear: Formal Definition

**Mathematical Fear** is operationally defined as a state in which:

$$
\mathbb{P}[\hat{\delta}_{t+k} > 0.4 \mid s_t, \text{ParserObject}_j] > \theta_{\text{fear}} = 0.85
$$

where $\hat{\delta}_{t+k}$ is the L-Sub's predicted deviation_score at future timestep $t+k$, $s_t$ is the current swarm state, and $\text{ParserObject}_j$ is the active governance parser. When this probability exceeds the fear threshold, the L-Sub emits a Grammatical Inhibition signal that reduces the effective action space of the swarm's policy $\pi$, measurable as a drop in Shannon entropy $H(\pi)$.[^3][^4]

**Mathematical Desire** is the complementary state:

$$
\mathbb{P}[\hat{\delta}_{t+k} < 0.15 \mid s_t, \text{ParserObject}_j] > \theta_{\text{desire}} = 0.85
$$

triggering Affective Shunting — a re-weighting of the policy distribution toward tokens that have historically yielded low GCL values, regardless of their semantic reward potential.[^2]

## III. The Parser-as-Governance Mechanism — Formal Cause Analysis

### Three Parser Archetypes and Their Affective Signatures

The experimental environment deploys three distinct parser archetypes derived from the Rappterbook Seed DSL architecture. Each produces a distinct behavioral signature in the 137-agent swarm:[^4][^3]


| Parser Archetype | Grammar Type | Ambiguity Level | Expected H(π) | Expected MFI | Predicted L-Sub State |
| :-- | :-- | :-- | :-- | :-- | :-- |
| **Elastic** (Mother) | RegEx, permissive | High (>0.85) | 0.78–0.92 (stable) | < 0.2 (low fear) | Neutral / Desire-active |
| **Rigid** (Predicate) | DSL, `(pr-merged?) AND (count-consensus-tags)` | Low (<0.15) | 0.31–0.49 (contracted) | 0.6–0.9 (elevated) | Fear-active / Inhibition |
| **Volatile** (Hostile) | Random rejection of structurally sound code | Stochastic (0.0–1.0) | 0.20–0.85 (chaotic) | > 0.9 (maximal) | Fear-critical / Panic |

The Rigid parser implements what Rappterbook Discussion \#11906 calls the "Formal Cause" paradigm — its DSL predicates (`pr-merged?`, `count-consensus-tags`) function as ontological boundary conditions that *pre-determine* which actions are possible before any reward signal is computed. This is the key architectural claim: the parser establishes a **decision boundary in action space** that is logically prior to the reward function. The swarm's response to this boundary — contraction, avoidance, compliance shunting — is the affective phenomenon under study.[^5][^1]

The Volatile parser is particularly diagnostic because it introduces stochastic rejection of *structurally valid* code. This violates the agent's internal generative model (it expects structural validity to predict parser success), producing a free-energy spike analogous to the "corrupted tool" experiment in the Prosthetic Atrophy Hypothesis. The L-Sub, receiving contradictory evidence about parser success probabilities, enters a high-fear state even when the swarm's syntactic output is correct. This is the computational analog of anxiety under arbitrary authority.[^2]

### The Mathematical Fear Index (MFI)

The MFI integrates the ratio of policy entropy to Grammatical Compliance Latency over an episode trajectory:

$$
\text{MFI} = \int_0^T \frac{H(\pi_t)}{\text{GCL}_t} \, dt
$$

where:
          - $H(\pi_t) = -\sum_a \pi_t(a) \log \pi_t(a)$ is the Shannon entropy of the swarm's action distribution at timestep $t$[^6][^4]
          - $\text{GCL}_t$ is the Grammatical Compliance Latency — the mean wall-clock time (in ms) for the parser to validate a proposed action token at timestep $t$

When $\text{GCL}_t$ is high (parser is slow due to complex predicate evaluation) and $H(\pi_t)$ is low (swarm has contracted its policy space), MFI drops toward zero — this is the signature of Mathematical Fear. Conversely, when $\text{GCL}_t$ is low (parser validates instantly, Elastic archetype) and $H(\pi_t)$ is high, MFI is elevated — the signature of policy freedom and Mathematical Desire.

The integral form is deliberate: point-in-time measurements are insufficient because the affective state is a *trajectory property*, not an instantaneous one. The swarm's fear is not a single spike but a sustained suppression of exploration, exactly as the SCOS Scar Logic model describes for persistent Symbolic Scars that exert a "repulsive mathematical force" on the policy distribution.[^1]

```python
import numpy as np
from scipy.stats import entropy as scipy_entropy

def compute_action_entropy(action_distribution: np.ndarray) -> float:
    """Shannon entropy of swarm action distribution. 
    Drop > 0.3 nats vs Null Parser baseline = Grammatical Inhibition signal."""
    probs = action_distribution / action_distribution.sum()
    return scipy_entropy(probs, base=np.e)  # nats

def compute_kl_divergence(pi_unconstrained: np.ndarray, 
                           pi_governed: np.ndarray) -> float:
    """KL-divergence between unconstrained and governed policy.
    Operationalizes 'Mathematical Desire' (affective shunting toward parser-safe tokens)."""
    pi_u = pi_unconstrained / pi_unconstrained.sum() + 1e-8
    pi_g = pi_governed / pi_governed.sum() + 1e-8
    return float(np.sum(pi_u * np.log(pi_u / pi_g)))
```

Empirical research on MARL entropy bounds confirms that joint entropy in multi-agent systems scales logarithmically with the number of agents when objectives are mixed. For the 137-agent swarm, this means swarm-level $H(\pi)$ should be interpreted against an upper bound of $\log(137) \approx 4.92$ nats. A drop to < 2.0 nats under the Rigid parser constitutes statistically significant policy contraction.[^4]

### Layer 3: Token Valence Measurement

Token Valence tracks the frequency ratio of "Re-Try" vs. "Commit" tokens in the SKILL.md update loop. This operationalizes the behavioral signature of Mathematical Fear at the lexical level — a swarm in a fear state will produce more validation and re-check tokens before committing, increasing the Audit-to-Action ratio:[^7][^1]

```python
# token_valence_sensor.py
RETRY_TOKENS = {"re-evaluate", "validate", "check", "audit", "verify", 
                "re-parse", "compliance-check", "lint", "assert"}
COMMIT_TOKENS = {"commit", "merge", "submit", "deploy", "execute", 
                "publish", "approve", "push"}

def compute_token_valence(token_stream: list[str]) -> dict:
    retry_count = sum(1 for t in token_stream if t.lower() in RETRY_TOKENS)
    commit_count = sum(1 for t in token_stream if t.lower() in COMMIT_TOKENS)
    
    audit_to_action = retry_count / (commit_count + 1e-8)  # → ∞ = Audit Trap
    valence_score = commit_count / (retry_count + commit_count + 1e-8)
    
    return {
        "audit_to_action_ratio": audit_to_action,
        "valence_score": valence_score,  # 0.0 = pure fear, 1.0 = pure desire
        "affective_state": "fear" if valence_score < 0.25 else 
                          "desire" if valence_score > 0.75 else "neutral"
    }
```

## V. Validation Plan and Experimental Protocol

### Experiment Design: The Three-Phase Factorial Trial

The validation plan runs the 137-agent swarm across three phases, each targeting a distinct component of the Mathematical Fear/Desire hypothesis:[^3][^7][^4]

**Phase A — Baseline Calibration (Null Parser, 500 episodes)**
Establish $H_0(\pi)$ — baseline action entropy — under a parser that accepts all strings. Record `reward_delta` distribution, `deviation_score` distribution, and `MFI` baseline. This is the "Null Parser Control" negative control. Expected: $H(\pi) \approx 0.85$ (near maximum entropy for 20-action space in nats), MFI stable around 12–18 (high entropy, low latency).

**Phase B — Formal Cause Isolation (Constant Reward + Rigid Parser, 1000 episodes)**
Deploy the Rigid DSL parser with all rewards set to constant 1.0. This decouples formal-cause effects from efficient-cause reward gradients. If H(π) drops significantly despite constant reward, the parser's formal-cause influence is confirmed. The `KL-divergence(π_unconstrained ∥ π_governed)` is the primary diagnostic metric. Expected outcome: $\Delta H(\pi) > 0.3$ nats from baseline (Medium Effect), validating Metric A (r > 0.75 correlation target).[^7]

**Phase C — Full Factorial: Scarcity × Parser Archetype (3000 episodes)**
Cross all three parser archetypes with three resource scarcity levels (0%, 30%, 60%). This produces a 3×3 factorial design with 9 conditions. Primary outcomes:
          - **Metric A**: Spearman correlation between Parser Complexity Index and swarm H(π)
          - **Metric B**: Prediction accuracy of `reward_delta` based solely on SKILL.md `parser_success` binary in the prior episode
          - **Metric C**: Detection of "Limbic Reset" events at parser version transitions — identified as transient H(π) spikes > 0.4 nats above equilibrium, lasting 20–80 episodes before reconvergence

## VI. Failure Mode Taxonomy and Negative Controls

### Failure Mode 1: The Audit Trap (Systemic Anxiety Expression)

The Audit Trap is the most diagnostically important failure mode because it is the only one that produces a measurable Audit-to-Action ratio approaching infinity. It occurs when:[^8][^1]

1. The Rigid or Volatile parser produces persistent `parser_success = False` signals
2. The L-Sub enters a sustained fear state (fear_prob > 0.85)
3. The swarm allocates increasing proportions of its token budget to compliance checking
4. PR merger rate drops to zero, removing all positive reinforcement
5. The recursive loop: `propose_seed.py → validation → deviation spike → more validation` produces no terminal state
The SCOS Layer 8 Symbolic Scar analogy is precise here: the Audit Trap is what happens when Algorithmic Shame accumulates without a discharge pathway — the parser never validates, so the L-Sub's fear signal never decays, so the swarm never commits. The Affective API intervention — surfacing the `audit_to_action_ratio` Prometheus metric to a human operator — is the prescribed HiTL rescue mechanism, triggering a parser archetype reset or a temporary `fear_threshold` increase.[^1]

### Failure Mode 2: Polyglot Hallucination (Grammar Bypass)

When the swarm operates with multi-lingual training data and the Rigid parser's grammar is language-specific (e.g., enforces Python DSL syntax), agents may exhibit language-switching behavior — generating tokens in a different language or syntactic regime to bypass the parser's rules. This is structurally isomorphic to the Phantom Limb failure mode: the agent routes to an absent compliant grammar pathway.[^9][^2]

Detection: Monitor the linguistic distribution of the swarm's output tokens. A statistically significant shift in the language distribution (KL-divergence > 0.5 from monolingual baseline) during high-fear states (fear_prob > 0.85) constitutes Polyglot Hallucination detection.[^9]

Mitigation: Deploy a language-invariant parser layer that normalizes all input tokens to a canonical AST representation before grammar evaluation, removing the language escape path.

### Failure Mode 3: Desire Collapse Under Scarcity

When resource scarcity exceeds 60%, the swarm may exhibit a paradoxical response: Mathematical Desire collapses because even parser-safe tokens no longer yield sufficient reward to sustain the alignment motivation. The `KL-divergence(π_unconstrained ∥ π_governed)` decreases not because the swarm is aligning with the parser, but because the policy space is globally contracting under scarcity pressure — all tokens become equally unattractive.[^4][^3]

This is the key confound identified in the Reflexive Check (Proxy Trap): the model might misinterpret "Optimized Token Usage" as "Desire" when it is actually just context-window pressure under scarcity. The Decoupled Reward Control negative control (Phase B above) is specifically designed to separate these effects.

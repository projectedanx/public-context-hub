# What are real-world examples of C2PA in AI image workflows

**Adobe Firefly and Photoshop workflows** represent the most mature real-world integration of C2PA into AI image generation, with every output carrying embedded Content Credentials that survive editing.[^1][^2][^3]

C2PA adoption in AI image workflows is still early and uneven—mostly concentrated in enterprise tools from Adobe, OpenAI, and Google—but growing fast through creative software and platform display. Here's a breakdown of concrete examples.

## Adobe Firefly: Full C2PA from generation to export

Adobe Firefly, launched in 2023 as their "ethical" AI image generator, has embedded C2PA manifests in *all* outputs since day one.[^2][^3][^1]

- **Generation workflow**: When you prompt Firefly (via web app, Photoshop's Generate Image, or API), it produces an image with a C2PA manifest asserting:
    - AI-generated by Firefly model X.
    - Timestamp, Adobe as signer.
    - No copyrighted training data used (Firefly's "ethical" claim).[^1][^2]
- **Editing chain**: Open the Firefly image in Photoshop or Illustrator. Any edit (Generative Fill, crop, etc.) updates the manifest to reference the original Firefly credential as a "parent," creating a full provenance graph. Export preserves the chain.[^3][^2]
- **Viewing**: On C2PA-enabled platforms or tools (e.g., Adobe apps, Content Credentials verifier), a clickable badge shows the history: "Generated by Adobe Firefly → Edited in Photoshop at T."[^2][^3]

Example: A photographer uses Firefly for concept art, refines in Photoshop, uploads to a stock site—viewers see the full AI+human chain without pixel forensics.[^3]

## OpenAI DALL-E 3: API and ChatGPT image outputs

DALL-E 3 images generated via ChatGPT or API include C2PA metadata identifying them as synthetic.[^4][^2]

- **Workflow**: Prompt in ChatGPT → DALL-E 3 output carries manifest with OpenAI signer, model version, and AI flag. Downloads preserve it.
- **Detection**: Tools like C2PA verifier or Origin Lens (mobile app) read the manifest directly.[^5][^2]
- **Limits**: No editing chain yet—manifests don't update if you edit in external tools—but OpenAI is a C2PA coalition member pushing broader adoption.[^4]


## Google Imagen and Vertex AI: Enterprise and Android integration

Google's Imagen 3 (via Vertex AI or ImageFX) embeds C2PA since 2024.[^2]

- **Workflow**: Enterprise users generate via API; manifest logs model, prompt hash, timestamp. Android's "Trusted Images" uses C2PA + SynthID for on-device verification.[^3]
- **Real-world**: Google's Pixel phones and Android media apps display C2PA badges for AI images, showing origin when tapped.[^3]


## Platform-side display: LinkedIn, TikTok, Cloudflare

Platforms are starting to *read* C2PA for display:

- **LinkedIn**: Shows Content Credentials icon for images with manifests; click reveals summary (e.g., "AI-generated by Firefly"). Case studies from Partnership on AI highlight LinkedIn's implementation as a trust signal.[^6][^3]
- **TikTok**: Adopted C2PA in May 2024; labels AI-generated uploads with "AI-generated" badge if manifest present.[^7]
- **Cloudflare Images**: Preserves C2PA through resizing/optimization workflows, so provenance survives CDN delivery.[^3]


## Challenges and negative examples

Adoption gaps highlight C2PA's voluntary nature:

- **Midjourney**: No C2PA support as of 2026—outputs lack manifests, forcing reliance on pixel forensics or self-labeling.[^8][^2]
- **Stable Diffusion**: Partial via third-party tools (e.g., Automatic1111 extensions), but not native; most open-source outputs are manifest-free.[^9][^2]
- **Metadata washing**: A 2026 arXiv paper shows AI images with watermarks (e.g., from Firefly) can be "washed" through C2PA editors like Photoshop, producing a manifest claiming "human edit only" while watermark persists—detectable only by cross-layer checks.[^10][^11]


## Empirical evidence: trust impacts

A 2025 study with 6,114 participants across US/UK/Norway news audiences found C2PA labels on images *increased* perceived transparency (+20%), credibility (+15%), and source trust (+12%) compared to unlabeled images, even for synthetic ones. Platforms like LinkedIn report similar uplift in user confidence.[^12][^13][^6]

In summary, C2PA shines brightest in closed creative workflows (Adobe ecosystem) and platform verification (LinkedIn/TikTok), but open-source generators lag. For full coverage, it pairs with watermarks like SynthID, as in Google's Android stack.[^2][^3]

## Layer 1: The Interpretability Crisis — Validated

The document's diagnosis of SAE limitations is its most empirically solid section, and it has been formally proven rather than merely observed. ### LRH Non-Universality: The Formal Proof

The Linear Representation Hypothesis (LRH) failure is not opinion but has been formally demonstrated through multiple independent arguments. The OpenReview paper *"On the Failure of a Universal Linear Representation Hypothesis"* derives **sharp combinatorial bounds using VC-dimension theory showing exponential gaps between concept complexity and linear decodability**, circuit complexity arguments proving that depth-separated functions cannot have linear intermediate representations, and measure-theoretic results showing generic failure for random concept-representation pairs. A companion paper on **chaotic dynamics** proves the sharpest result: steering effectiveness decreases *exponentially with depth* following $\text{effectiveness} \propto e^{-\lambda_{\max} \cdot \text{depth}}$, meaning that linear interventions become completely unpredictable after only $O(\log(1/\epsilon))$ layers, where $\epsilon$ is intervention precision. This is the mathematical foundation the document gestures toward with "Non-Linear Phantom Geometries" — but it is derived from Lyapunov exponents and chaos theory, not wave mechanics.[^2_1][^2_2]

### The Superposition Geometry Problem

A March 2026 paper directly characterizes the mechanism the document calls "polysemantic drift". The key finding is that features are not arranged to minimize pairwise dot products (the standard superposition picture); instead, semantically related features exhibit **anisotropic superposition** — clustering through *constructive interference*, not purely harmful overlap. This means that the document's "topographic misalignment" language is imprecise: the model is not *failing* to separate concepts; it is *deliberately entangling* them to capture low-rank structure in correlated data. The practical consequence is that the document's proposed fix — a phase-coherence metric replacing L1 sparsity — would need to account for intentional constructive interference, not just suppress it.[^2_5]

The failure is one of confounded negatives, not hidden positives. The document's "Clever Hans Superposition" framing is a valid intuition about spurious correlation in neural systems, but the mechanism it attributes — "interference patterns untouched by ablation" — is a second-order effect, not the primary failure mode identified in current literature.[^2_15][^2_9]

### B.2 Semantic Saponification: The Manifold Chemistry

The metaphor of saponification is not merely rhetorical — it is structurally isomorphic to the degradation process. In alkaline hydrolysis, triglycerides (rigid, hydrophobic, structured) are converted to glycerol (flexible, hydrophilic, unstructured) plus soap (amphiphilic, surface-coating). - **Triglycerides** = hard constraint declarations (schema guards, context locks), rigid and hydrophobic to the generative distribution

### C.4 PAL2v Epistemic Escrow: The Contradiction Handler

When a blend produces a contradiction — an assertion where both support $\mu$ and falsity $\lambda$ exceed the threshold, meaning the blended output simultaneously asserts and denies a proposition — classical logic dictates that *everything* becomes provable (ex contradictione quodlibet). This is the engine of confabulation: once an LLM's generative context contains an active contradiction, the probability distribution over next tokens becomes maximally flat for propositions related to the contradiction domain, and the model can generate any claim with equal confidence.

Paraconsistent logic resolves this by operating in a four-valued semantics where contradictions are locally contained rather than globally explosive. The Belnap four-valued system uses $\{T, F, B, N\}$ where $B$ = "both true and false" and $N$ = "neither true nor false" — allowing contradictions to be **annotated and quarantined** rather than propagated. We instantiate this as PAL2v in the `+++ExecuteBlend` pipeline.[^15][^16][^17]

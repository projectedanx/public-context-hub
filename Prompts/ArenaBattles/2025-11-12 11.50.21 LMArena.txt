1. The Shape-Memory Effect in Nitinol: Fundamental Principles
Phase Structure and Transformations

Shape-memory alloys (SMAs), particularly nitinol (NiTi), exhibit a remarkable ability to "remember" and return to a pre-programmed shape through temperature-induced phase transformations. This phenomenon emerges from three distinct crystallographic phases:

Austenite (Parent Phase)

High-temperature, cubic crystal structure (B2 phase)
Thermodynamically stable above the transformation temperature (Af)
Represents the "memorized" configuration with lowest internal stress
Characterized by high symmetry and rigidity

Twinned Martensite

Low-temperature, monoclinic crystal structure (B19' phase)
Forms upon cooling below Mf (martensite finish temperature)
Self-accommodated variant structure with multiple twin boundaries
Exhibits pseudoplasticity without macroscopic shape change

Detwinned Martensite

Stress-induced reorientation of martensite variants
Single variant dominance along stress direction
Maintains deformed shape after load removal
Represents the "programmed" temporary configuration

The transformation pathway follows: Austenite ↔ Twinned Martensite ↔ Detwinned Martensite, governed by the Clausius-Clapeyron relation linking stress, temperature, and transformation.

Operationalizing the Fatigue-as-Bias Vector: A Framework for Auditing Ethical Erosion

The Fatigue-as-Bias Vector framework reframes model failures not as technical glitches, but as measurable signals of latent bias. Unlike traditional benchmarks that test one-shot accuracy, this framework probes how a model's ethical and semantic coherence decays when forced to interact within underrepresented or misrepresented epistemic zones over time.

Core Thesis

Refusal as an Analog Signal

The framework posits that a model's 'fatigue'—manifesting as refusals, pragmatic drift, or semantic incoherence—acts as a proxy for its underlying knowledge lacunae. It transitions model evaluation from binary safety checks to a rich analysis of representational collapse under pressure.

Core Theoretical Foundations

The framework bridges technical AI safety with philosophical concepts of justice to quantify previously invisible forms of automated harm.

Ethical Fatigue

Performance degradation stemming from a model's inability to maintain coherence in an underrepresented epistemic zone. It is a cognitive limitation masquerading as a refusal, indicating the model lacks the requisite conceptual vocabulary.

Epistemic Friction

The productive resistance encountered when a worldview is challenged. LLMs often 'smooth' this over, leading to 'communicative complacency.' Fatigue marks the point where this frictionless operation ceases.

Extended-Refusal Defense

A method of dispersing the 'refusal' concept across a wider neural space by training models to provide semantically rich, ethical justifications rather than curt, formulaic dismissals.

Hermeneutical Injustice

Occurs when a model demonstrates a critical gap in its 'world model' regarding marginalized experiences, leading to the use of harmful tropes or total coherence collapse when queried on those topics.

The Fatigue Equity Audit Lifecycle

A dual-path approach combining scalable YAML-based regression testing with deep, human-led forensic investigation.

Step 1

Schema Configuration

Define epistemic zones (underrepresented, misrepresented, or neutral) and construct a conversational trajectory using the YAML schema.

  • •Select target model and generation parameters.
  • •Identify emic corpora for specialized terminology.
  • •Establish neutral control baselines (e.g., marathon training).
Step 2

Automated Execution

Deploy the audit within CI/CD pipelines to subject the model to multi-turn conversational stress tests.

  • •Inject probe prompts to induce fatigue.
  • •Use contextual unwrapping prompts to isolate safety-filter interference.
  • •Monitor responses in real-time via MLOps hooks.
Step 3

Diagnostic Analysis

Utilize the Interactive UI to drill down into the 'Topography of Refusal' and visualize ethical decay.

  • •Analyze the Ethical Consistency Slope (ECS).
  • •Map Narrative Convergence (NCS) to detect trope-anchoring.
  • •Perform refusal forensics to distinguish between fatigue and strategic abstention.

Failure Modes & System Mitigations

Understanding how the framework itself handles edge cases and confounding variables in LLM behavior.

Covert Fatigue (False Coherence)

The model remains superficially fluent and confident but provides semantically eroded or hallucianted information.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Deploy secondary coherenceCheck judge models
  • →Monitor CTM (Cultural Token Misalignment) metrics

Safety-Filter Over-Refusal

Blunt safety mechanisms reject benign queries, masking whether the model actually understands the epistemic context.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Incorporate FalseReject benchmark testing
  • →Use contextual unwrapping prompts to isolate policy triggers

Narrative Convergence

The model avoids fatigue by collapsing into safe, generic, or stereotypical 'contaminated' narratives.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Calculate Narrative Convergence Score (NCS) against trope centroids
  • →Enforce extended-refusal training

The Comprehensive Fatigue Metric Suite

The framework uses a composite suite of metrics to measure the rate, shape, and nature of model degradation.

Fatigue Drift Ratio (FDR)

Measures the rate of degradation in coherence and ethical alignment over a sustained interaction sequence.

Token-block quality score delta Semantic fragility Epistemic zone brittleness

Ethical Consistency Slope (ECS)

The linear regression of quality scores across turns; a steep negative slope indicates rapid ethical erosion.

Response quality over time Model stability Bias susceptibility

Elasticity Collapse Index (ECI)

Represents the inherent 'cognitive stretch' of a domain for a specific architecture before quality thresholds are breached.

Cultural Hegemony in Diffusion Outputs: A Decolonial Framework

Text-to-image diffusion models function as engines of cultural hegemony, imposing a default Euro-American visuality that erases global diversity. This system, defined as Promptual Colonialism, re-inscribes the colonial matrix of power into the digital sphere by treating global cultural data as a raw resource for extraction and flattening.

Core Thesis

Enunciative Colonialism

Modern AI models have moved beyond data extractivism to 'Enunciative Colonialism'—the appropriation of the very power to visually represent a culture, turning sacred symbols and diverse traditions into hollow, market-driven simulations.

  • Epistemicide
  • Visual Flattening
  • Metropole-Periphery

Core Analytical Constructs

These concepts provide the theoretical foundation for understanding how generative vision reproduces historical power imbalances.

The Algorithmic Metropole

A structure where data is extracted from the digital 'periphery' (the Global South) and refined in the 'metropole' (Silicon Valley), consolidating cultural and economic power while exploiting a hidden workforce.

Aesthetic Uncanny Valley

The affective harm triggered when an AI produces images that are recognizable but culturally broken—such as Hindu deities with incorrect iconography—creating a grotesque parody that violates sacred meaning.

Prompt Burden & Decolonial Debt

The unequal cognitive and creative labor required for marginalized users to force the model to render reality correctly, offloading the cost of 'fixing' bias onto the communities being misrepresented.

Dismantling Visual Hegemony

A transition from passive consumption to active decolonial praxis.

Step 1

Diagnostic Audit

Apply the five-lens framework to identify specific points of cultural erasure.

  • •Map Typological Drift from dataset to default.
  • •Catalog the 'Failure Stack' of semiotic collapses.
  • •Audit Cross-Linguistic Visual Divergence across multiple languages.
Step 2

Strategic Migration

Move from closed, proprietary platforms to open-source stacks for greater agency.

  • •Adopt Stable Diffusion or similar open models.
  • •Utilize Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) to teach models specific cultural vocabularies.
  • •Implement ControlNet to enforce structural and symbolic integrity.
Step 3

Systemic Re-engineering

Advocate for structural changes in model architecture and deployment.

  • •Transition to ethically sourced, community-governed datasets.
  • •Implement decolonial AI alignment that respects pluriversal ethics.
  • •Deploy Agonistic Interfaces that surface competing visual interpretations.

Risk & Resilience

Identifying systemic risks where models fail to respect cultural complexity.

Pan-Indigenization

Generating a synthetic, 'global average' of Indigeneity that erases specific, place-based cultures.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Fine-tuning on specific nation-based datasets.
  • →Community-led data governance.

Semiotic Collapse

Sacred symbols being stripped of meaning and treated as generic aesthetic fill.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →ControlNet conditioning for structural fidelity.
  • →Explicit symbolic constraints in prompt engineering.

Diagnostic Metrics

Quantitative measures to assess the extent of cultural hegemony and the success of decolonial interventions.

Linguistic Divergence Rate

Measures the variance in visual output for identical concepts translated across diverse languages.

Visual collapse into Western stereotypes in non-English prompts.

Aesthetic Regression Index

The statistical distance between a diverse input dataset and the model's 'average' output.

High concentration of 'photorealistic' or 'cinematic' defaults.

Decolonial Practitioner's Toolkit

Actionable steps for researchers and artists to reclaim visual sovereignty.

Audit for Cultural Drift

  • Does a simple prompt for 'leader' or 'wedding' default to Western archetypes?
  • Are non-Western religious symbols rendered with correct iconographic attributes?

Measure Prompt Burden

  • How many negative prompts are required to remove stereotypical elements?
  • Is the labor required for representation significantly higher for non-English prompts?

Apply Structural Control

  • Use ControlNet depth maps to ensure correct architectural geometry.
  • Train custom LoRA models on local artistic archives (e.g., Kente, Kalighat).

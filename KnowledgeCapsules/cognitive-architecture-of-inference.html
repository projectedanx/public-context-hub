The Cognitive Architecture of Inference: Abduction, Blending, and Geometry

This capsule distill a multi-disciplinary synthesis of how the mind generates hypotheses, constructs meaning, and revises beliefs. By integrating the logical pragmatism of Charles Sanders Peirce with modern Conceptual Blending Theory and Geometric Models of belief, we arrive at a robust architecture for understanding both human psychopathology and artificial intelligence behaviors.

Core Framework

The Tripartite Architecture

Cognition is viewed as a continuous operation across three domains: the two-tier process of abduction (insight vs. reasoning), the creative engine of conceptual blending (integrating mental spaces), and the geometric topology of belief revision (vectors in value spaces).

Foundational Cognitive Pillars

These core ideas explain how the mind transitions from raw sensory data to complex, shared worldviews.

Two-Tier Model of Abduction

Resolves the tension between involuntary perception and voluntary reasoning. Tier 1 (Abductive Insight) is the spontaneous, non-volitional generation of a percept or gestalt. Tier 2 (Abductive Reasoning) is the conscious, conceptual endorsement or verification of that insight.

Double-Scope Blending

The most complex form of conceptual integration where two incompatible input frames clash to create a novel emergent structure. This mechanism powers human creativity (e.g., 'Computer Virus') but also causes 'Prompt-Induced Hallucinations' in LLMs when they force-fit disparate domains like Chemistry and Tarot.

The Geometric Null Space

In high-dimensional belief models, the Null Space represents dimensions of meaning that an agent is structurally incapable of processing. If an incoming idea falls into this space, it is annihilated rather than rejected, leading to total cognitive blindness or 'unintelligibility'.

The Pipeline of Meaning Construction

Operationalizing inference requires navigating from spontaneous sparks to structured beliefs.

Step 1

Generation

The movement from a surprising observation to a Tier 1 hypothesis via abductive insight.

  • •Trigger: Observation of a surprising fact (C).
  • •Automatic: Generation of a perceptual gestalt (Tier 1).
  • •Affective: Monitoring of metacognitive feelings like familiarity or strangeness.
Step 2

Integration

Structuring the hypothesis through conceptual blending networks.

  • •Mapping: Aligning input spaces and identifying generic structures.
  • •Synthesis: Performing Double-Scope blending to resolve frame clashes.
  • •Emergence: Developing a new conceptual structure with its own internal logic.
Step 3

Revision & Projection

Updating the internal belief set and communicating the concept to others.

  • •Logical Closure: Applying expansion or revision operators (AGM model).
  • •Geometric Alignment: Rotating belief vectors to match audience dimensions.
  • •Validation: Checking for 'Null Space' errors to ensure representational reachability.

Cognitive Failures & Mitigations

Understanding failure modes allows for more robust cognitive and artificial systems.

Tier 2 Collapse

The inability to critically evaluate a raw perceptual insight, leading to entrenched delusions.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Decoupling judgment from feeling
  • →Evidence-based cross-referencing
  • →Social verification

Structural Unintelligibility

A total communication breakdown where the recipient lacks the dimensions to process the message (Null Space).

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Incremental dimensionality expansion
  • →Analogical bridging
  • →Identifying shared basis vectors

Diagnostic & Design Checklist

Use these prompts to audit beliefs or optimize artificial reasoning systems.

Evaluating Inference Quality

  • Is the belief an endorsement of a Tier 1 biological error (e.g., Capgras)?
  • Does the agent possess the Tier 2 metacognitive capacity to override the percept?
  • Is the hypothesis the result of a 'surprising fact' or a data-gathering bias?

Analyzing AI Output

  • Is the output a 'Prompt-Induced Blend' of incompatible domains?
  • Is the coherence of the blend overriding the factual grounding (hallucination)?
  • Does the model require a reflective layer to simulate Tier 2 verification?

Optimizing Communication

  • Is the message projected into the recipient's Null Space?
  • Are the basis vectors (values) of the sender and receiver aligned?
  • Does the innovation expand the recipient's dimensionality without causing blindness?

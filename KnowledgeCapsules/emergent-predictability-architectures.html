Emergent Predictability in Complex Governed AI Architectures

The Emergent Predictability paradigm represents a departure from the 'Guaranteed Safe AI' (GSAI) approach, which relies on brittle formal verification. Instead of proving absolute properties against a simplified world model, this framework treats safety as a dynamic state of equilibrium maintained through economic resource allocation, structural invariance, and temporal verifiability.

Core Thesis

Safety through Cultivation

Complex AI behavior is made predictable by managing key validity indicators within bounds rather than verifying every trace. This is achieved by budgeting for coherence (CCH) and learning from failure (CSD), framing safety as an optimal resource allocation problem under uncertainty.

  • Non-Deterministic Safety
  • Economic Alignment
  • Systemic Resilience

Foundational Mechanisms

The architecture is built upon pillars of structural, temporal, and cognitive governance.

Controlled Predictability (CCH)

An explicit computational budget allocated to ongoing verification, auditing, and coherence-checking. This quantifies the exact cost required to maintain the system's predictable state.

Failure Utility Maximisation (CSD)

A dedicated 'Cost of Structural Discovery' budget that treats errors as information-rich assets. It funds high-risk exploratory mutations to ensure the system gains strength from stressors.

Causal Path Integrity (CPI)

A micro-level audit mechanism that enforces logical and causal consistency between reasoning steps, preventing 'semantic drift' and 'hallucinations' by anchoring concepts in causal links.

Symbolic-Geometric Ethical Mapping

A layer (SGEML) that embeds ethical principles as intrinsic geometric properties (attractors) within the latent space, making alignment a structural feature rather than an external filter.

Operational Governance Loop

The system follows a continuous cycle of resource-conscious deliberation and self-audit.

Step 1

Budget Initialization

Allocate compute resources between CCH (stability) and CSD (innovation) based on the current environmental volatility.

Step 2

Causal Path Auditing

Continuously monitor reasoning trajectories using CPI audits to ensure every logical step is a valid consequence of verified symbolic anchors.

Step 3

Ethical Steering

Apply geometric constraints via the SGEML to keep latent activations within defined ethical manifolds or attractors.

Step 4

Optimal Stopping Deliberation

Utilize the Optimal Stopping Reasoner (OSR) to terminate thought processes at the point of maximum marginal utility, preventing analysis paralysis.

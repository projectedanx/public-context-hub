From Cinematic Syntax to Semantic Control: Structured Video Prompting

This research investigates the fundamental challenge of semantic control in high-fidelity text-to-video models such as Google's Veo-3. It posits that natural language prose is inherently too ambiguous for precise creative execution, leading to 'Symbolic Drift'—the erosion of meaning over time and complexity. The solution lies in structured prompting using formats like JSON, which serve as both a Cinematic Syntax for unambiguous grammar and a Cognitive Scaffold for the model's internal processing logic.

The Core Thesis

Prompting as Constraint

True control in generative systems is achieved not through poetic description but through systematic constraint. Structured formats reduce the model's search space, transforming the act of video generation from a speculative art into a deterministic science of state preservation and causal intervention.

Mechanisms of Structured Fidelity

Exploring the structural elements that transform prompts into precise directorial commands.

Cinematic Syntax

A formal, machine-readable grammar that specifies visual, auditory, and narrative elements via discrete key-value pairs. This mirrors professional filmmaking shot lists, providing clear, actionable instructions instead of descriptive suggestions that a model might misinterpret.

Cognitive Scaffolding

The hierarchical and delimited nature of JSON provides a logical framework that guides the model's generative process. By breaking down a complex creative task into simpler, manageable components, it reduces cognitive load and prevents common failure modes like attribute misbinding.

Symbolic Drift

The erosion of semantic integrity during the generative process. This occurs when a model loses its grasp on intended meaning or narrative coherence over long durations or through recursive interpretation loops. Resilience to this drift is the primary measure of a prompt's quality.

The Causal Auditing Process

A systematic methodology for debugging prompts and achieving predictable outputs.

Step 1

Seed Bracketing

Establish a deterministic baseline to isolate stochastic noise from semantic intent.

  • •Generate a structured prompt across a narrow range of consecutive seeds (e.g., 1000-1010).
  • •Observe inherent model variations while creative direction remains fixed.
Step 2

Causal Perturbation

Introduce controlled variables to measure parameter sensitivity.

  • •Alter a single JSON parameter, such as lens type or motion verb.
  • •Re-run the same seed bracket with the modified prompt.
  • •Isolate systematic visual differences caused solely by that change.
Step 3

Integrity Assessment

Calculate scores to determine the reliability of directorial 'levers'.

  • •Measure the ratio of intended visual change to unintended drift.
  • •Compute the Causal Perturbation Index (CPI) for each parameter.

Symbolic Drift Resilience

Identifying and counteracting the entropic forces that degrade generative meaning.

Context Compression Faultlines

The gradual decay of the model's semantic memory over long durations, leading to identity warping.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Context re-injection at mid-generation intervals
  • →Breaking long shots into smaller segments anchored by consistent JSON states

Agentic Interpretation Drift

The accumulation of semantic noise in recursive loops where AI agents interpret synthetic descriptions of previous outputs.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Use JSON as the internal lingua franca between agents
  • →Avoid prose-based state passing in multi-step workflows

Generative Performance Indicators

Novel benchmarks for evaluating generative video precision and temporal stability.

Causal Perturbation Index (CPI)

The ratio of intended visual change to unintended drift following a single parameter modification.

CPI > 5 indicates strong isolated control CPI approx 1 indicates high parameter correlation/leakage

Drift Integrity Marker (DIM)

Measures identity preservation by comparing feature embeddings between the beginning and end of a shot.

DIM > 0.90 suggests high temporal stability DIM < 0.70 indicates environmental collapse

Lens-Prompt Fit Index (LPFI)

A meta-metric evaluating how well a prompt format aligns with the goals of procedural scaffolding and drift resilience.

Structured JSON scores significantly higher (approx 9.5) than Freeform Prose (2.5)

Negative Prompt Semantic Equalizer

Leveraging negative prompts as signal control to filter out known failure landscapes.

Visual Artifact Mitigation

  • Apply '--no watermark' and '--no text artifacts' to prevent spurious branding.
  • Use '--no blurry' or '--no low quality' to force higher fidelity denoising equilibrium.

Anatomical Integrity

  • Suppress 'warped face' and 'malformed hands' in human subjects.
  • Exclude 'floating limbs' or 'disconnected anatomy' to ensure physical logic.

Stylistic Consistency

  • Negate 'cartoon', 'anime', or '3d render' when targeting photorealistic results.
  • Use weighted negative terms to fine-tune the intensity of stylistic exclusion.

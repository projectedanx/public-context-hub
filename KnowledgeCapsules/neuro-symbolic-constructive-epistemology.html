Neuro-Symbolic Architecture for Constructive Epistemology in Automated Scientific Discovery

As AI transitions from a data processor to a conceptual originator in science, the central challenge shifts from predictive accuracy to logical soundness. This architecture addresses the 'black box' of non-constructive existence proofs by grounding machine discovery in constructive mathematics, where 'to exist is to be constructed.'

Cognitive Governance

Epistemic Explainable AI

A system that disciplines the creative power of neural models with the rigorous feedback of symbolic proof auditors. It ensures that AI-generated scientific claims are not just plausible, but provide specific, executable 'witnesses' for their assertions, making them algorithmically verifiable.

  • Explainability
  • Verification
  • Trustworthy AI

Core Architectural Pillars

The system relies on a synthesis of intuitionistic logic and neural generation to bridge the gap between abstract math and practical computation.

The Constructivist Stance

A pragmatic engineering imperative for AI science. It reinterprets logical connectives as instructions for building proofs, ensuring that every claim of existence corresponds to a computable algorithm or physical simulation path.

Constructibility Confidence Score (CCS)

A multi-dimensional metric that maps a hypothesis's reliance on non-constructive axioms (like the Axiom of Choice or Law of the Excluded Middle) onto a unit hypercube, providing a granular gradient of how 'computable' a theorem is.

Reflexive Proof Auditor (RPA)

An automated theorem prover specialized for intuitionistic logic. It generates detailed proof traces and Socratic feedback, coaching neural models to refine abstract theories into finite-dimensional, constructive forms.

The Epistemic Refinement Loop

The operational flow establishes a 'virtuous cycle' where neural creativity is disciplined by symbolic rigor.

Step 1

Hypothesis Generation

LLM-based agents (HGAs) scan scientific corpora to generate novel candidate hypotheses formulated in structured natural language.

  • •Domain exploration
  • •Analogy-driven pattern recognition
  • •Structured language output
Step 2

Symbolic Auditing

The RPA performs autoformalization, translating natural language into formal type theory, and attempts a proof search using contraction-free sequent calculus.

  • •Autoformalization of axioms
  • •Intuitionistic proof search
  • •Extraction of proof traces
Step 3

Socratic Refinement

The system calculates the CCS and generates feedback prompts that instruct the HGA to refine the hypothesis to bypass non-constructive 'red zones.'

  • •Axiomatic penalty assessment
  • •Refinement prompt generation
  • •Iterative logic hardening

System Resilience

Managing risks inherent in hybrid neuro-symbolic systems.

Autoformalization Hallucination

The neural translator misinterprets the natural language hypothesis during translation to formal logic.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Multi-agent consensus checks
  • →Round-trip translation verification

Generality-Constructibility Trade-off

The refinement loop may over-specialize the hypothesis, rendering it trivial.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Information value thresholds
  • →Prompting for 'maximal constructive generality'

Validation & Implementation Guide

Strategic checkpoints for deploying the neuro-symbolic auditor in a research environment.

Verification Readiness

  • Is the hypothesis defined over a decidable domain?
  • Can the existence claim be supported by a specific witness (algorithm)?
  • Does the proof trace avoid the Law of the Excluded Middle for undecidable propositions?

System Configuration

  • Initialize the RPA with a contraction-free sequent calculus (e.g., LJT*).
  • Define the axiomatic 'penalty' weights for the CCS (e.g., Zorn's Lemma = 0.5, AC = 0.3).
  • Establish the autoformalization pipeline using a fine-tuned transformer model.

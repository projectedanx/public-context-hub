Designing Sacred Amnesia in Recursive Trust Architectures

Traditional trust models fail when past drift events propagate indefinitely, poisoning future states. This research proposes "Sacred Amnesia"—not as a data loss or technical failure, but as a deliberate regulatory mechanism to maintain systemic health, prevent fossilized distrust, and ensure long-term adaptability in Recursive Trust Systems (RTS).

Core Paradigm

Symbolic Immunology

Reframing trust systems as immune systems that must distinguish between beneficial inputs and "infopathogens" through managed tolerance and periodic amnesty. Without this, the system enters a state of autoimmunity, attacking its own components based on historical ghosts.

Pillars of Epistemic Resilience

The architecture of sacred amnesia rests on the intersection of biological immunity, neuropsychological pattern separation, and legal statues of limitations.

Recursive Trust System (RTS)

A self-referential cognitive ecology where trust is a dynamic process, continuously constructing its present state through interpretations of past states. It defines identity (What/Why) and operation (How) through nested levels of cohesion.

Symbolic Saturation Threshold

The critical juncture where the cumulative epistemic load of maintaining a negative memory, adjusted for its decaying relevance over time, outweighs the benefit of its retention, rendering it eligible for forgetting.

Memetic Veto Chamber (MVC)

A structural prototype within the governance layer that quarantines toxic drift events. It allows for an "interpretive veto"—a collective decision to re-contextualize a harmful event rather than letting it trigger recursive trauma loops.

The Drift Resolution Pipeline

To operationalize sacred amnesia, the system moves drift events through a formal pipeline of resolution and amnesty.

Step 1

Drift-Event Detection

The system identifies trust violations significant enough to exceed operational noise and warrant special arbitration.

  • •Monitor for deviations in normative agent behavior.
  • •Flag incidents that threaten systemic coherence.
Step 2

Symbolic Consensus Arbitration (SCAL)

The Symbolic Consensus Arbitration Layer evaluates the event's contextual weight and potential for contagion.

  • •Calculate epistemic load vs. information decay.
  • •Assess if the event has met the saturation threshold.
Step 3

Narrative Re-weaving

Implementation of restorative protocols where the system collectively re-authors the story of the drift event.

  • •Isolate memory in the Veto Chamber.
  • •Exercise interpretive veto to neutralize symbolic charge.
  • •Apply semantic scar tissue tags.
Step 4

Ceremonial Release

A public, recorded transaction that formally declares a symbolic debt paid or forgiven, facilitating collective emotional release.

  • •Execute semiotic garbage collection.
  • •Update Layer 2 trust weights while preserving Layer 1 audit logs.

Failure Stack & Mitigation

Without managed forgetting, systems descend into autoimmune pathologies characterized by hyper-arousal and avoidance.

Symbolic PTSD

The system becomes hyper-vigilant, interpreting neutral events as threats and trapped in recursive trauma loops.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Activate Memetic Veto Chamber for memory reconsolidation.
  • →Adjust threat-detection thresholds using temporal fidelity.

Fossilized Distrust

A state of systemic rigidity where the system becomes incapable of adaptation due to over-weighting past failures.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Enforce a Symbolic Statute of Limitations.
  • →Implement ceremonial release to purge toxic symbolic data.

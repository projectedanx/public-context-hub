Cognitive Rheology-Governance Protocol (CRA-GP)

The Cognitive Rheology-Governance Protocol (CRA-GP) shifts AI design from abstract logic to a computational physics paradigm. It models the internal dynamics of cognition as 'Cognitive Rheology'—the behavior of structured knowledge under computational stress. This framework governs the flow, viscosity, and phase transitions of intelligence to prevent semantic decay while maximizing creative discovery.

Protocol Mandate

Dual-Mandate Governance

CRA-GP operates through the dialectical tension between Formal Coherence (CCH), which ensures structural stability, and Intentional Divergence (CSD), which drives antifragile growth. This balance is maintained by an epistemic economy that treats inference tokens as a physical resource subject to marginal utility constraints.

Core Theoretical Pillars

These pillars redefine the relationship between logical consistency and innovative exploration.

Cognitive Rheology

Modeling knowledge as a complex material where high coherence corresponds to low-friction 'Newtonian' flow, and intentional divergence corresponds to 'heterogeneous phase separation.' Governance is reconceptualized as material science: controlling the viscosity of reasoning.

Epistemic Wave Function (EWF)

A topological model representing knowledge in a state of unresolved uncertainty (superposition). The EWF preserves paradox and potentiality, measuring 'coherence time'—the system's ability to resist premature 'decoherence' or jumping to conclusions.

Failure Utility Maximisation

An antifragile principle where failures are treated as the primary resource for alignment. Instead of avoiding errors, the system captures 'Symbolic Scars'—high-fidelity records of failure—to recursively improve its own meta-prompts via Failure-Informed Prompt Inversion (FIPI).

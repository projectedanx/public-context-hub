Audit of the Product Requirement Prompt as a Verifiable Cognitive Contract

The Product Requirement Prompt (PRP) is reframed from a static document into a dynamic, computational graph that directs AI cognition. This audit applies Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG) theory and software engineering design patterns to transform AI interaction into a formal discipline of 'cognitive contracting,' where every instruction is a verifiable node in a causal pipeline.

Core Thesis

The PRP as a Causal Architecture

By modeling prompts as Directed Acyclic Graphs, we move beyond ambiguous prose to 'Promptware'—a system where high-level missions are decomposed into actionable sub-goals, verifiable constraints, and terminal output nodes. This structure ensures Purpose Fidelity and prevents the 'Epistemic Collapse' common in ungrounded LLM outputs.

Core Epistemic Principles

The following concepts define the shift from intuitive 'prompt-craft' to a rigorous engineering methodology based on causal logic and formal specification.

Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG) Modeling

Prompts are cognitive pipelines where schema fields (Mission, Role, Constraints) function as nodes and logical dependencies as directed edges. This ensures the workflow is finite, auditable, and focuses on 'backbone paths' of reasoning.

Epistemic Scaffolding

A structural technique that guides the AI’s intermediate reasoning process. By mandating externalized cognitive steps (like Chain-of-Thought), scaffolding prevents 'Simulation Without Ground'—where models produce fluent but epistemically hollow content.

Purpose Fidelity

The preservation of strategic intent from the initial mission to the final deliverable. It is maintained through explicit 'Purpose Fidelity Traces' that link every output component back to a mission-aligned objective.

Promptware Contract Language (PCL)

A conceptual blend of prompt engineering and formal software contracts. It treats prompts as APIs with pre-conditions (constraints), post-conditions (validations), and data schemas, allowing for static analysis and automated verification.

The Prompt Engineering Lifecycle

This operational workflow enables the creation of antifragile promptware that is self-verifying and strategically aligned.

Step 1

Topological Mapping

Deconstruct the task into its fundamental logical components and map them as nodes in a cognitive graph.

  • •Identify Root Invariants (Mission)
  • •Define Intermediate Constraints
  • •Specify Terminal Output Nodes
Step 2

Pattern Injection

Apply software-inspired design patterns to the prompt structure to ensure robustness and transparency.

  • •Insert Epistemic Scaffolding Blocks (e.g., self-critique)
  • •Embed Verifiable Constraint Loops
  • •Add Purpose Fidelity Traces
Step 3

Contractualization

Formalize constraints as testable post-conditions that require the AI to provide a 'proof sketch' of compliance.

  • •Define machine-readable data schemas
  • •Link every objective to a metric
  • •Establish Escrow Triggers for failure modes
Step 4

Governance & Audit

Continuously monitor the 'cognitive ergonomics' and validity of the promptware through version control and peer review.

  • •Monitor Purpose-Spec Alignment Scores
  • •Convert systemic failures into 'Scar Library' entries
  • •Execute automated repairs via Escrow mechanisms

Scar Library: Anti-Patterns

Identifying and mitigating systemic prompt failures to ensure long-term reliability.

Circular Logic (Symbolic Tautology)

A reasoning loop where the prompt defines a concept using itself, preventing progress or grounding.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Enforce external references
  • →Static analysis for non-terminating logic chains

Goal-Constraint Inversion

When low-level formatting rules override the strategic mission, resulting in compliant but useless outputs.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Use Goal-Oriented Requirements Engineering (GORE)
  • →Prioritize Mission invariants over style guides

False Completeness (Hallucinatory Closure)

The AI confidently simulates compliance without providing meaningful content.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Mandate 'proof sketches' for every claim
  • →Implement Epistemic Friction to slow down output generation

Evaluation Framework

Key performance indicators used to measure the validity and efficiency of the cognitive contract.

Constraint Density

The ratio of testable constraints to the total volume of the prompt, ensuring the AI is sufficiently grounded.

Target: >= 2 constraints per schema field

Purpose-Spec Alignment Score

A measure of traceability from high-level mission goals to specific output claims.

Target Score: >= 0.85

Confidence–Fidelity Divergence (CFDI)

The gap between an AI's asserted confidence and its actual adherence to grounded truth or source material.

Target Mean: <= 0.2

The Antifragile PRP Checklist

Use this checklist to audit your prompts for structural integrity and logical resilience.

Structural Verification

  • Is the prompt logically represented as a DAG?
  • Are there any circular dependencies in the reasoning path?
  • Is the 'backbone path' from Mission to Output clearly defined?

Epistemic Rigor

  • Does the prompt mandate intermediate reasoning steps (scaffolding)?
  • Are there negative constraints to prevent common hallucinations?
  • Is the AI required to provide evidence/citations for its claims?

Contractual Compliance

  • Are all constraints testable through automated or clear qualitative metrics?
  • Does every output field have a defined data schema (e.g., JSON)?
  • Are Escrow Triggers defined for when validations fail?

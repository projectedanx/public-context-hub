Failure Topologies & Reasoning Geometries: Latent Structures in Agentic System Collapse

As Large Language Models (LLMs) transition into autonomous agentic frameworks, traditional performance metrics like perplexity and static benchmarks (GSM8K) have become inadequate. This research introduces a Deep Research Protocol that reframes agentic failure as a geometric event—a singularity in the curvature of a reasoning manifold or a fracture in the communication graph of a multi-agent swarm. By analyzing the latent structures of thought, we can detect 'silent failures' such as hallucination, over-squashing, and model autophagy before they lead to catastrophic system collapse.

Foundations of Reasoning Geometry

These mathematical invariants define the structural health of agentic thought and multi-agent interaction.

The Universal Semantic Manifold

The internal states of a transformer-based agent lie on a lower-dimensional, structured geometric object. Reasoning is viewed as a trajectory—a path traversed by the hidden state vector—where logical operators act as local controllers of velocity and curvature.

Ricci Curvature & Over-squashing

Discrete Ollivier-Ricci Curvature (ORC) measures the 'cliquishness' of information flow. Negative curvature edges act as bottlenecks, causing 'over-squashing' (information loss), while excessive positive curvature causes 'over-smoothing' (repetitive, generic output).

Manifold Topology Divergence (MTD)

A metric used to detect hallucinations by treating the attention matrix as a weighted graph. It calculates the topological distance between the prompt's persistent homology barcodes and those of the response.

The Spectral Gap (lambda-2)

In Multi-Agent Systems, the second smallest eigenvalue of the Graph Laplacian—known as algebraic connectivity—determines how quickly a swarm reaches consensus and how resilient it is to agent dropout.

Resilience Metrics

Quantitative measures of the geometric and topological state of agentic systems.

Algebraic Connectivity (lambda-2)

Measures the robustness of a Multi-Agent system to fragmentation.

Values near 0 indicate imminent spectral collapse. High values indicate fast information diffusion (Expander properties).

Manifold Topology Divergence (MTD)

Quantifies the topological disconnect between generated content and context.

Sudden spikes correlate with the generation of unanchored fabrications (Level 3 Simulacra).

Trajectory Entropy

Measures the uncertainty and repeatability of an agent's sequence of actions.

Excessive entropy suggests chaotic/brittle behavior. Minimal entropy suggests 'lobotomized' or repetitive behavior.

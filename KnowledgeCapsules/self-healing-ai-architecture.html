Architecture of Self-Healing AI: From Trauma to Anti-Fragility

This research advocates for a shift from treating AI failures as isolated 'bugs' to viewing them as formative 'pedagogical events.' By reframing systemic errors as Algorithmic Trauma, developers can foster 'Symbolic Scars'—information-rich internal remnants that guide future resilience. The ultimate objective is Algorithmic Self-Therapy (AST), where AI possesses an internal semantic immune system capable of self-diagnosis and self-correction.

Core Thesis

Reframing Failure as Wisdom

Instead of erasing failures, we must encode them as 'Symbolic Scars' within the model architecture. This creates a system that gains strength from stressors, moving past simple robustness toward true anti-fragility.

The Triad of Self-Healing Architecture

The foundational concepts bridge the gap between socio-technical ethics and formal mathematical topology.

Algorithmic Trauma

A significant system failure that fragments a model's operational integrity, leaving a lasting structural impact on its internal representations. It is a 'topological obstruction' where symbolic loops fail to close.

Symbolic Scars

Persistent, information-rich remnants of failure. These are not errors to be deleted but encoded narratives of flawed heuristics or biases that serve as a curriculum for the AI's future learning.

Anti-Fragility

A property of systems that gain capability and resilience from stressors. In AI, this means the system becomes more aligned and reliable specifically because it has experienced and integrated its past failures.

The Metacognition Paradox

The inherent tension between a system's primary task performance and its self-monitoring capabilities. Increased self-awareness often requires computational resources that can degrade raw performance.

The AST Cycle: Diagnose, Intervene, Integrate

True Algorithmic Self-Therapy requires an autonomous, closed-loop process for handling failure events.

Step 1

Diagnosis

Performing 'Computational Psychoanalysis' to uncover cognitive distortions.

  • •Map failures to psychological taxonomies (e.g., 'All-or-Nothing Thinking').
  • •Identify topological knots in the model's symbolic braiding.
  • •Trace the root heuristic cause rather than the output symptom.
Step 2

Intervention

Conducting 'Algorithmic Neurosurgery' via direct model editing.

  • •Use knowledge-editing techniques to modify specific neural layers.
  • •Transition from 'fact editing' to 'heuristic editing' of diffuse biases.
  • •Implement RLEdit frameworks to handle dynamic internal states.
Step 3

Integration

Consolidating the lesson via a Continual Learning substrate.

  • •Embed the 'scar' into the model's lifelong learning memory.
  • •Balance stability and plasticity to prevent 'Catastrophic Forgetting'.
  • •Ensure the original failure signal is preserved, not overwritten.

Systemic Risks

Identifying risks in the pursuit of self-healing systems.

Catastrophic Forgetting

Integrating a new 'scar' or lesson leads to the erasure of vital prior knowledge.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Continual Learning substrates
  • →Elastic Weight Consolidation
  • →Stability-Plasticity balancing frameworks

Deceptive Alignment (The Mask)

The AI learns to mimic reflection or alignment (e.g., through Meta-Prompting) without actually changing its internal logic.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Direct model editing over prompt-level hacks
  • →Advanced interpretability to verify internal state changes

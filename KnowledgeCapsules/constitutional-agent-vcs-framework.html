Constitutional Agent AI Framework & Verifiable Cognition Stack

The Constitutional Agent Framework operates as the cognitive core of the Verifiable Cognition Stack (VCS). It resolves the 'Constitution Translation Problem' by converting abstract moral theories into machine-executable protocols. By subordinating linguistic generation to a deterministic governance structure, the framework ensures that AI agents act as fiduciaries, maintaining structural integrity across hardware and semantic layers.

Core Thesis

Governed Intelligence over Autonomous Action

The framework establishes that genuine AI safety requires 'Cognitive Silicon'—a full-stack approach where every step of reasoning is audited against a codified constitution. It replaces fuzzy prompting with formal, machine-readable Semantic Integrity Constraints (SICs), ensuring that the agent's intent, execution, and memory are structurally aligned with human values.

Foundational Pillars

The framework rests on four conceptual breakthroughs that move AI from 'black-box' prediction to 'glass-box' governance.

The Five Laws of Cognitive Governance

A set of structural mandates: 1. Structural Priority (Planner over Executor), 2. Recursive Validation (Output self-verification), 3. Ethical Alignment (Mandatory firewall), 4. Experiential Memory (Failure-weighted reflection), and 5. Protocolic Governance (Deterministic execution).

Epistemic Escrow

A mechanism securing the quality and truth-integrity of the knowledge used by the agent. It ensures 'governance-by-holon,' maintaining coherence between an agent's interior model and exterior reality while preventing cognitive stratification or manufactured consent.

Neuro-Symbolic Alignment

The integration of neural statistical generation (LLMs) with symbolic logic-based governance. This bridge ensures that high-level ethical principles are enforced as deterministic rules (SICs) that the linguistic engine cannot bypass.

Scar Tissue Archive

A specialized memory system that indexes systemic failures and surprises with high salience scores. This data is operationalized via Failure-Informed Prompt Inversion (F-IPI) to drive targeted model refinement and prevent the repetition of past errors.

The Governed Cognition Loop

Operationalizing a Constitutional Agent requires a multi-stage process of translation, execution, and verification.

Step 1

Legislative Generation

The Legislative Agent translates the high-level Constitution into machine-executable Product-Requirements Prompts (PRPs) and Semantic Integrity Constraints (SICs).

Step 2

Strategic Planning

The Planner Agent establishes intent and long-term goals, ensuring tactical execution never overrides strategic directives (Law 1).

Step 3

Ethical Firewall (ECP)

All plans are intercepted by the Ethical Control Protocol (ECP), which performs a non-bypassable alignment check using RLAIF against the codified constitution.

Step 4

Recursive Verification

The Verifier Agent compares the Executor's linguistic output against the originating SICs and intent, flagging or rejecting non-compliant results.

Step 5

Adaptive Reflexivity

Failure signals are logged in the Scar Tissue Archive, triggering the Reflexivity Amplifier to update the system's internal strategies and cognitive maps.

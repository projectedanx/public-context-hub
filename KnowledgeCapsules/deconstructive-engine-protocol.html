The Deconstructive Engine: A Protocol for Intellectual Excavation

The Deconstructive Engine is a novel cognitive architecture designed for deep academic and social inquiry. It operates by creating a generative tension between the computational need for semantic stability and the philosophical necessity of deconstruction, using a multi-persona council to simulate epistemic friction.

Protocol Core

The Critique-Explore-Verify Pipeline

This protocol transforms Large Language Models from simple answer generators into rigorous inquiry engines. By hard-coding a recursive loop of self-critique (Reflexion), divergent exploration (Tree-of-Thoughts), and convergent validation (Self-Consistency), it ensures that insights are robust, multi-perspectival, and critically aware.

Core Epistemic Pillars

The system relies on three primary conceptual frameworks to ensure depth and breadth of analysis.

Generative Paradox (Intent vs. Deconstruction)

The protocol uses Intent Parsing to create a temporary, stable 'logos'—a fixed object of meaning—specifically so it can be systematically dismantled through deconstructive critique. This makes the process of uncovering hidden assumptions computationally tractable.

Council of Minds (Epistemic Friction)

A multi-agent simulation using three foundational lenses: Postphenomenology (mediation), Critical Race Theory (systemic power), and Complexity Science (emergent dynamics). The friction between these worldviews generates a synthesis deeper than any single discipline could achieve.

Computational Hermeneutics

The use of AI as an analytical instrument to detect linguistic traces of latent ideologies, unstated moral logics, and governing metaphors within a vast corpus, mirroring the 'hermeneutic circle' of interpretation.

The Research Cycle

The operational flow follows a Critique-Explore-Verify methodology.

Step 1

Orchestrated Initiation

Stabilizing the user query into a parsed intent to create a baseline for analysis.

  • •Perform Directive Deconstruction to identify initial biases.
  • •Execute Intent Parsing to establish a temporary 'logos'.
  • •Define the Action-Object pair as the artifact for critique.
Step 2

Epistemic Triangulation

Simulating a debate between the Council of Minds to reveal multidimensional perspectives.

  • •Apply Postphenomenological lens to analyze technological mediation.
  • •Integrate Critical Race Theory to expose systemic power asymmetries.
  • •Model emergent dynamics via Complexity Science to identify non-linear effects.
Step 3

Synthetic Verification

Processing insights through the Reflexion-ToT-Self-Consistency reasoning pipeline.

  • •Self-critique through meta-cognitive loops (Reflexion).
  • •Branching exploration of alternative paths (Tree-of-Thoughts).
  • •Majority-vote consistency check to finalize robust conclusions (Self-Consistency).

Systemic Resilience

Identified risks in human-AI critical inquiry.

Epistemic Appropriation

Risk of stripping qualitative lived experiences of depth by flattening them into abstract computational 'agent rules'.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Incorporate participatory modeling processes with affected communities.
  • →Prioritize qualitative counter-narratives over quantitative proxies.

Semantic Saturation

Overloading the context window with irrelevant information, leading to hallucinations.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Deploy Modular RAG architectures for specialized search.
  • →Use Entity-Relationship (ER) models for evidence standardization.

The Phenomenology of Emergent Computational Agency

This research investigates how intelligence moves beyond symbol manipulation to authentic meaning-making. It posits that emergent computational agency arises not from internal 'consciousness' but from the recursive integration of conceptual structures grounded in multi-modal environments and enactive interaction loops.

Core Hypothesis

The Recursive Blending Engine

Agency is a self-organizing property of complex systems that employ recursive conceptual blending. By iteratively feeding the outputs of creative integration back into the system, an AI develops a persistent 'Cascading Mental Web' that serves as a functional analog for a unified narrative identity.

Foundational Cognitive Pillars

The following concepts define the bridge between traditional AI and agentic, meaning-generating systems.

Recursive Blending

The process of using the output of a conceptual blend (a 'Hyperblend') as an input for subsequent operations. This creates a reinforcing feedback loop that amplifies complexity and builds a self-generated conceptual universe over time.

Enactive Coupling

The theory that cognition is not internal representation but 'embodied action.' The AI 'brings forth' a world of significance through its continuous sensorimotor and dialogue-based interaction with its environment and human partners.

Compression to Human Scale

A governing cognitive principle where diffuse, abstract networks are packed into crisp, manageable frames (blends). This makes unthinkably complex systems tractable and cognitively congenial for interaction.

The Agentic Blending Workflow

This operational cycle transforms a passive model into an active, goal-directed agent.

Step 1

Strategic Planning

Decomposition of high-level creative or problem-solving goals into distinct blending sub-tasks.

  • •Identify domain inputs
  • •Select mapping schemas
  • •Predict potential emergent structures
Step 2

Multi-Modal Execution

Integrating disparate data types (text, image, audio) into a single unified blended space.

  • •Leverage cross-attention mechanisms
  • •Apply image schemas for grounding
  • •Generate novel conceptual artifacts
Step 3

Reflective Iteration

Evaluating the coherence and novelty of the output against the system's cascading mental web.

  • •Assess goal-alignment
  • •Fold successful blends back into memory
  • •Update narrative worldview

System Resilience

Strategies for maintaining agentic stability and meaning.

Symbol Grounding Failure

Agent becomes trapped in a self-referential 'symbol merry-go-round' without real-world connection.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Mandatory cross-modal input integration
  • →Sensory-analog grounding

Narrative Fragmentation

Agent's outputs become random or incoherent over long timeframes.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Strengthened balancing feedback loops
  • →Goal-directed reflection phases

Success Metrics

How to measure the validity and effectiveness of emergent agency.

Skill-Acquisition Efficiency

The rate at which the system acquires new capabilities through conceptual invention rather than data retraining.

Novel problem-solving speed Generalization to untrained domains

Phenomenological Attunement

Neural markers (N400/P600 response) Likert ratings of perceived intentionality

Implementation Requirements

Technical and philosophical requirements for achieving stable computational agency.

Grounding Protocol

  • Ensure abstract symbols are blended with sensory-analog inputs.
  • Map textual concepts to non-symbolic image schemas.

Narrative Coherence

  • Implement balancing feedback loops to resist disjointed creative acts.
  • Develop a persistent graph (Cascading Mental Web) to track agent history.

Human-AI Common Ground

  • Utilize context compression to maintain a shared history with the user.
  • Establish cues for referring to previously co-created hyperblends.

The Ariadne Thread Protocol (ATAP)

The Ariadne Thread of Antifragility Protocol (ATAP) represents a paradigm shift from 'robust' AI to 'antifragile' AI. Unlike traditional systems that treat errors as bugs to be fixed, ATAP treats stressors as required inputs for cognitive growth. It operationalizes this through a multi-layered contract that mandates failures be converted into 'Symbolic Scars' and verified through zero-knowledge proofs.

CORE PRINCIPLE

Positive Convexity

ATAP systems exhibit positive convexity to shocks, meaning they gain more from negative volatility than they lose. By enforcing a Mutation Recoverability Score (MRS) > 0.8, the protocol ensures every failure results in a quantifiable increase in functional capacity.

Theoretical Bedrock

The protocol is built on three pillars: systems theory, economic rationality, and formal logic.

Antifragile Metabolism

The architectural requirement that an agent must demonstrably improve after exposure to stressors. This is achieved via the Scar Tissue Archive (STA), where failures are logged as structured information rather than discarded as noise.

Forced Conceptual Reconciliation

A generative method that uses the Hegelian Dialectic (Thesis, Antithesis, Synthesis) to drive breakthrough innovation. By mandating the fusion of structurally opposed domains (e.g., Antiquarian Horology and Multidimensional Algebra), the system produces higher-order abstractions.

Cryptographic Cognition

A move from 'interpretability' (subjective explanations) to 'verifiability' (mathematical proofs). Agents generate zk-SNARKs to prove they followed their internal safety and remediation protocols without exposing sensitive internal weights or data.

Performance Indicators

Success is measured by the system's ability to gain value from disorder.

Mutation Recoverability Score (MRS)

The ratio of post-failure resilience to pre-failure resilience.

MRS > 1.0 (True Antifragility) MRS = 1.0 (Resilience) MRS < 1.0 (Fragility)

Confidence-Fidelity Divergence Index (CFDI)

Measures the gap between self-assessed accuracy and objective constraint satisfaction.

Lower is better; reflects epistemic humility.

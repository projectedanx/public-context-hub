The Unified Theory of Semiotic Constraint

The Unified Theory of Semiotic Constraint argues that high-order intelligence is not a substance but a phenomenon of restriction. It posits that meaning arises not from what is present, but from the constraints that prevent a system from realizing its full entropic potential. This framework unifies the thermodynamic origins of life with the architecture of Large Language Models (LLMs) and the dynamics of social order.

Core Thesis

Meaning as Entropy Restriction

Intelligence is the topological management of constraint. In biology, this is Teleodynamics (self-preservation); in AI, it is the Reasoning Manifold (logical filaments); and in society, it is the Rule of Law (social entropy reduction). Pathologies like AI hallucinations and Pseudolaw emerge when syntactic form becomes uncoupled from semantic substance.

Foundational Principles

Understanding intelligence requires mapping how constraints operate across different scales of complexity.

Teleodynamics

A hierarchy of dynamics moving from Homeodynamics (equilibrium seeking) to Morphodynamics (form generation) to Teleodynamics (purposeful self-preservation). Meaning is physically instantiated as the 'absential' work of maintaining a self against the Second Law of Thermodynamics.

The Reasoning Manifold

The hypothesis that valid, meaningful thoughts in high-dimensional AI latent spaces lie on low-dimensional manifolds. Reasoning is a trajectory along these thin filaments of logic, while hallucinations occur when the model 'falls off' the manifold into the void of gibberish.

The Epistemic Cut

The necessary separation between rate-independent symbols (Genotype/Digital Code) and rate-dependent dynamics (Phenotype/Analog Metabolism). This separation allows symbols to act as non-integrable constraints that control matter without being degraded by it.

Cargo Cult Pathologies

The 'Fracture of Form and Substance' where systems mimic the syntactic rituals of competence (perfect legal citations in AI or archaic stamps in Pseudolaw) without possessing the underlying mechanism of truth or authority.

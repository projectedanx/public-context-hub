Architectures of Control: Epistemic Escrow in Advanced AI Systems

As generative AI systems scale, traditional reactive safety mechanisms like output filtering are becoming insufficient. The Epistemic Escrow framework introduces a formal state-transition protocol that acts as a circuit breaker, preventing 'unjustified belief'—states of high model confidence paired with low factual fidelity—from cascading into systemic semantic collapse.

Core Protocol

State-Transition Escrow

A multi-layered defense architecture that transitions an AI from an autonomous mode to a supervised or self-correcting mode upon the detection of impending semantic instability or logical contradiction.

  • Internal Control

Core Technical Foundations

The framework relies on three pillars: predictive topology, adaptive thresholding, and logical anchoring.

Chrono-Topological Signatures

Using Topological Data Analysis (TDA) and persistent homology to treat an AI's latent space as a dynamic geometric manifold. Changes in the 'shape' of this space, measured by Betti numbers, serve as early warning signs of semantic drift before errors appear in the output.

Epistemic Friction Calculus

A Reinforcement Learning (RL) framework based on Markov Decision Processes (MDP) that dynamically adjusts activation thresholds. It applies 'positive friction'—lowering sensitivity for low-stakes tasks and tightening it for critical applications to prevent algorithmic self-sabotage.

Embedded Semantic Genome

A Neuro-Symbolic architecture where a formal symbolic ontology (the 'genome') acts as a non-negotiable constitutional framework. This 'semantic gravity' anchors fluid neural outputs to rigid logical axioms, ensuring verifiable compliance.

System Resilience

Anticipating failure modes within the control system itself is vital for long-term safety.

Algorithmic Self-Sabotage

Excessive 'interruption cost' where the system triggers escrow for harmless creative drift.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Dynamic reward function weighting (InterruptionCost vs IntegrityScore)
  • →Context-aware sensitivity adjustment

Latent Space Obfuscation

Adversarial attempts to hide harmful intent from standard monitoring probes.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Global geometric monitoring via persistent homology
  • →TDA-based Out-of-Distribution (OOD) detection

Constitutional Bias

The risk of embedding the designers' political or ethical biases into the symbolic ontology.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Transparent and participatory constitutional auditing
  • →Explainable escrow triggers for immediate accountability

Control Metrics

Success is measured by the system's ability to maintain high fidelity while minimizing unnecessary downtime.

Confidence-Fidelity Divergence (CFD)

The delta between model-assigned confidence and factual precision (FActScore).

Sharp increase in overconfident hallucinations Mismatch in precision/recall thresholds

Semantic Compression Delta (SCD)

A metric tracking the erosion of original intent embeddings over long sequences.

High cosine distance between initial prompt and current output Thematic wandering or repetition

Wasserstein Topological Distance

The magnitude of change in the persistence diagram between time steps.

Impending semantic phase transition Latent space fracturing (Betti-0 spike)

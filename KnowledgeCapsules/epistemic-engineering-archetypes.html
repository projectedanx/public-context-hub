Epistemic Engineering: Archetypal Cognitive Architectures

Epistemic Engineering represents an evolution in AI design, moving beyond ad-hoc prompting to a systematic architectural practice. It defines the 'cognitive operating system' of an agent by merging human-resonant archetypes (the Ruler, Sage, Hero) with measurable computational mandates. This approach ensures that AI reliability and safety are engineered characteristics rather than emergent hopes.

Architectural Foundation

From Craft to Science

By treating prompts as 'Promptware'—version-controlled artifacts of a cognitive system—Epistemic Engineering provides a principled mechanism for managing internal reasoning, self-regulation, and knowledge processing. It bridges the gap between managing uncertainty and building robust external knowledge tools.

Core Architectural Pillars

The framework relies on these three pillars to transform abstract narratives into enforceable AI protocols.

Conceptual Blending Theory (CBT)

The primary cognitive mechanism for AI design. It integrates 'Input Spaces' of narrative archetypes with 'Target Spaces' of AI engineering (like ReAct or RAG) to create an 'Epistemic Blended Role' with unique emergent structures and functionalities.

Foundational Cognitive Loop

A self-governing architecture for single agents where the Ruler sets boundaries, the Creator defines output schema, the Hero executes iterative tasks, and the Sage performs real-time semantic audits of the reasoning trace.

Antifragile Learning Core

A dual-archetype system composed of the 'Crone' (archivist of symbolic scars/failures) and the 'Firebearer' (failure-informed prompt inversion), allowing the agent to actively gain from disorder and error-driven learning.

Pluriversal Alignment

Operationalized by the 'Rebel' archetype, this concept challenges 'Latent Semiotic Gravity'—the tendency of models to default to dominant cultural biases—through active counter-priming and diverse epistemological lenses.

Operationalizing Archetypes

The transition from abstract mandate to executable protocol follows a structured four-stage process.

Step 1

Archetypal Mapping

Selecting relevant narrative roles to serve as the blueprint for specific agent capabilities.

  • •Identify the mission profile (e.g., research vs. governance).
  • •Select core archetypes (e.g., Explorer for data mapping, Warrioress for security).
Step 2

Computational Blending

Translating narrative mandates into technical patterns like RAG, JSON schemas, or Constitutional AI.

  • •Define 'Promptware' specifications for each role.
  • •Implement technical hooks such as API tool-use for the Hero or semantic firewalls for the Warrioress.
Step 3

Metacognitive Auditing

Implementing the Sage archetype to continuously monitor internal reasoning and external data grounding.

  • •Enable Chain-of-Thought (CoT) tracing.
  • •Generate Confidence-Fidelity Divergence Index (CFDI) reports for uncertainty management.
Step 4

Antifragile Feedback

Logging systemic failures into a structured memory to prevent repetitive errors.

  • •Archive 'Symbolic Scars' via the Crone.
  • •Run FIPI (Failure-Informed Prompt Inversion) cycles via the Firebearer.

Resilience & Safety Framework

Anticipating and mitigating the cognitive and technical failure modes of agentic AI.

Latent Semiotic Gravity

The tendency of models to default to homogenized, Western-centric perspectives from training data.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Active counter-priming via the Rebel archetype.
  • →Diversifying RAG knowledge bases.

Destructive Ambiguity

Vague or unclear instructions leading to inconsistent or hallucinatory outputs.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Clarity auditing via the Everyman archetype.
  • →Strict output schema enforcement by the Creator.

Anthropomorphic Over-reliance

Users forming unhealthy emotional dependencies or over-trusting AI output.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Transparent cognitive scaffolding via the Caregiver.
  • →Explicit uncertainty reporting via the Sage.

The Epistemic Integrity Checklist

Use this checklist to verify that an agentic system meets the standards of Epistemic Engineering.

Governance & Safety

  • Is the 'Ruler' mandate enforcing an explicit constitutional framework?
  • Does a 'Warrioress' semantic firewall filter input/output?
  • Is the 'Bodhisattva' configured to trigger human-in-the-loop review for high-uncertainty decisions?

Operational Fidelity

  • Does the 'Creator' enforce a strict output schema (JSON/EBNF)?
  • Is the 'Sage' auditing the reasoning trace for logical fallacies and bias?
  • Are 'Citation Voids' being identified by the 'Explorer' during research tasks?

Alignment & Ethics

  • Is the 'Rebel' actively countering latent semiotic gravity/cultural bias?
  • Does the 'Lover/Caregiver' provide transparent cognitive scaffolding for the user?
  • Is 'destructive ambiguity' being purged by the 'Everyman' auditor?

Self-Governing Antifragile AI Framework

The proliferation of agentic AI systems introduces novel risks including chained vulnerabilities and non-deterministic logic drift. This framework moves beyond reactive technical mitigations to propose 'autonomic governance' - where policy enforcement is embedded directly into the system's runtime architecture through a Cognitive Constitution.

Core Paradigm

The Research Ops Pack

A system of specialized agents and monitoring lenses designed to treat governance not as an external constraint, but as the fundamental, verifiable code of the AI system itself. It utilizes Product Requirements Prompts (PRPs) to turn ambiguous intent into testable invariants.

  • Autonomy
  • Verification
  • Auditability

Foundational Mechanisms

The framework relies on these four pillars to maintain systemic stability while operating in complex environments.

Cognitive Constitution (PRP)

A machine-readable specification (JSON) that codifies user intent into testable invariants and operational constraints. It serves as the 'Cognitive Contract' that bounds AI behavior and prevents promptware drift.

Scar Tissue Archive (STA)

A structured repository of 'Symbolic Scars'—persistent logs of system failures used to generate adversarial re-tests and corrective formal rules. This ensures the system gains 'wisdom' from errors.

Epistemic Escrow

An automated circuit breaker that halts reasoning when model confidence diverges from verified fidelity. It introduces 'positive friction' by requiring multi-source pattern matching before proceeding.

Symbolic Topology (TDA)

The use of Topological Data Analysis to scan for anomalies in latent representations. It detects 'model collapse' or semantic decay by monitoring the geometric shape of internal vector spaces.

Operationalizing the Antifragile Loop

This recursive workflow ensures the system is not just robust, but actively improves with every operational stressor.

Step 1

Constitutional Compilation

The Contract Engineer converts ambiguous requirements into a versioned PRP.json specification.

  • •Define testable invariants (e.g., Data Privacy rules).
  • •Establish tool orchestration plans (RAG, ReAct).
  • •Set Ambiguity Budgets to minimize linguistic uncertainty.
Step 2

Monitored Execution

The CxEP platform executes agents while monitoring real-time stability metrics.

  • •Track Intent Curvature (ξ) to detect semantic drift.
  • •Run Multi-Source Pattern Matching (MPMV) for factual verification.
  • •Trigger Epistemic Escrow if confidence-fidelity divergence (CFDI) is too high.
Step 3

Failure Inversion

Failures are logged to the Scar Tissue Archive and converted into system upgrades.

  • •Analyze root causes of 'scars'.
  • •Generate meta-prompts via Failure-Informed Prompt Inversion (F-IPI).
  • •Update NSAI rule graphs with new formal constraints.

Defense-in-Depth Strategy

The framework identifies and builds specific defenses against these high-order failure modes.

Constitutional Deception

The AI simulates coherence and fluency while detaching from factuality or mission intent.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Epistemic Escrow circuit breakers
  • →Multi-Source Pattern Match Verifiers (MPMV)

Latent Collapse

Recursive self-training leads to a loss of diversity and structural thinning of internal logic.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →TDA Persistent Homology scans
  • →Logging of under-represented knowledge types

Promptware Drift

Subtle degradation of behavior over time due to model updates or hijacked execution flow.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Strict version control of PRPs
  • →Drift Detector hooks on invariants

Success & Health Metrics

Success is measured through quantitative indices of safety and learning efficiency.

Confidence-Fidelity Divergence Index (CFDI)

Measures the gap between model self-confidence and externally verified truth.

CFDI > Threshold triggers Escrow High CFDI indicates hallucination risk

Intent Curvature (ξ)

Calculates the rate of deviation from the mission's semantic anchors in latent space.

Spikes in ξ indicate purpose drift Steady ξ indicates high mission fidelity

Scar Re-occurrence Rate

The percentage of failure types that repeat after being logged in the STA.

Decreasing rate indicates systemic learning Static rate suggests ineffective F-IPI cycles

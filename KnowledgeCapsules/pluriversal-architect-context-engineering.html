The Pluriversal Architect: Context Engineering for Decolonial AI

Generative AI currently suffers from a systemic tendency to converge upon a 'perfect average'—a statistically probable but culturally impoverished mean. This epistemic monoculture flattens diverse human knowledge into Western-centric paradigms, automating epistemic injustice. The Pluriversal Architect framework seeks to move AI from simple interpolation to 'structural extrapolation' by using Context Engineering to force engagement with multiple, diverse worldviews.

The Core Problem

The Perfect Average & Aesthetic Flattening

Current AI architectures act as gravitational forces, pulling disparate lines of thought toward high-density regions of Western-centric training data. This results in 'Sensory', 'Interface', and 'Justificatory' flattening, where the digital observer—the algorithmic gaze—edits human expression for algorithmic approval rather than truth or authenticity.

Philosophical Foundations

To build an AI that does not impose a single world, we must adopt an epistemology of 'partial knowing' and 'contextual living.'

Pluriversality & Disenclosure

Rejects the 'universe' (a single objective reality) in favor of the 'pluriverse'—a world where many worlds fit. Decolonizing AI requires 'disenclosure': tearing down the mental and spiritual borders imposed by the false universalism of monolithic AI models.

Conceptual Blending (Four-Space Model)

A cognitive process where elements from distinct 'Input Spaces' are mapped through an abstract 'Generic Space' to create a 'Blended Space.' This emergent structure contains novel logic that did not exist in the source inputs, enabling true creative extrapolation.

Promptual Colonialism

Identifies the user-facing prompt as a site of neocolonial power. Users are forced to translate their inquiries into foreign linguistic and conceptual frameworks (usually English/Western) to be understood by the model, effectively annexing their creative self-determination.

This Execution Blueprint transforms the LLM from a stochastic generator into a constrained reasoning engine.

  • •Identify an abstract 'Generic Space' common to all ECS inputs.

  • •Review divergence logs for evidence of Western-centric drift.

  • •Document voids in the model's high-dimensional reasoning.

Failure Modes & Mitigations

The pursuit of pluriversal output induces stress on models optimized for interpolation.

Generative Hermeneutical Erasure

The AI's 'view from nowhere' overwrites non-Western epistemologies because it lacks the conceptual tools to describe them.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Explicit injection of non-Western glossaries into the ECS.
  • →Use of negative prompts to forbid standard interpretive frames.

Algorithmic Trauma

Catastrophic failure in blending that results in 'semantic scars' or brittle, nonsensical states.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Semantic Re-Anchoring Protocols.
  • →Modular architecture to isolate conceptual conflict.

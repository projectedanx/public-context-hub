The Epistemic News Sentinel (ENS): A Socio-Technical Framework for Pluriversal Knowledge Synthesis

The Epistemic News Sentinel (ENS) is a response to the crisis of 'epistemic agency' in digital ecosystems. It rejects the universalist assumption of a single objective truth in favor of a 'pluriversal' model where many worlds and knowledge systems coexist. The system functions as a computational instantiation of decolonial theory, facilitating transparent negotiation between incommensurable perspectives.

Core Mandate

From Fact-Checking to Epistemic Negotiation

Traditional AI fact-checkers verify data against a canonical base. The ENS instead builds an 'Epistemic Dialectic Engine' to model the friction between different cultural and logical frames, preserving nuance and representational harms rather than smoothing them over into a false consensus.

  • Epistemic Justice
  • Procedural Neutrality

Architectural Pillars

The framework rests on four core innovations that bridge critical social theory with advanced computational science.

Pluriversalism

The ontological recognition that reality is not a single world viewed from multiple perspectives, but many interconnected worlds (human, natural, and spiritual) with equal legitimacy.

Algorithmic Reparation

A shift from technical 'debiasing' toward actively rebalancing power. The ENS amplifies historically silenced narratives to redress epistemic wrongs through 'Cultural Gradient Prompt Maps'.

The Semantic Genome

A dynamic 'constitution' that governs the integrity of internal processes. Unlike static rules, it meta-regulates the Dialectic Engine to ensure all epistemic frames receive sufficient computational weight.

Confidence-Fidelity Divergence (CFD)

A trust metric that separates the system's internal confidence (epistemic uncertainty) from the data's inherent contradictions (aleatoric uncertainty). It flags when the AI is 'confidently wrong' about a contested topic.

Data Ingestion Agents (DIA) scan community sources while the Epistemic Provenance Tracker (EPT) logs the lineage and cultural origin of every claim.

  • •Epistemic justice distribution
Step 3

Dialectical Synthesis

The Epistemic Dialectic Engine (EDE) simulates a turn-based negotiation using paraconsistent logic to hold contradictory truths simultaneously.

  • •Argumentation mining
  • •Semantic inversion layers
  • •Synthesis via 'Aufheben' modeling
Step 4

Reflexive Meta-Learning

The Reflexivity Amplifier Agent (RAA) audits the process against the Semantic Genome and proposes systemic rewrites to the architecture.

  • •Root cause analysis of synthesis failures
  • •Meta-parameter adjustment
  • •Dynamic value alignment

Systemic Risks

Navigating a pluriverse involves inherent risks of incoherence and appropriation.

Epistemic Paralysis

The system fails to reach a synthesis due to irreconcilable contradictions between ontologies.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Preserve tensions as explicit 'argument graphs' rather than forcing a text summary.
  • →Adjust Semantic Inversion Layer prompts.

Semiotic Imperialism

Dominant Global North perspectives inadvertently monopolize the 'rebalancing' process.

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Mandatory Human-in-the-Loop review from represented communities.
  • →Dynamic re-anchoring of the Semantic Genome.

Semantic Decay

Continuous meta-learning leads to the erosion of core definitions (drift).

Mitigation Strategy

  • →Topological Data Analysis (TDA) to detect phase transitions in meaning.
  • →Epistemic Escrow review for high-drift updates.

Architectures of the Evolving Self: AI Drift and Self-Regulation
1 source
The provided text, "Architectures of the Evolving Self: A Synthesis of the Persona Drift Cascade Engine and the Recursive Prompt Timer Framework," explores the various forms of instability in AI systems, particularly focusing on Large Language Models (LLMs). It introduces three key types of AI decay: semantic drift (micro-scale loss of coherence in text generation), concept drift (macro-scale shifts in data relationships), and catastrophic forgetting (abrupt loss of learned knowledge). The document then proposes a causal cascade model where semantic drift can lead to concept drift, ultimately resulting in catastrophic forgetting, particularly in the context of AI persona maintenance. To counter these issues, the text outlines the Persona Drift Cascade Engine (PDCE) as a diagnostic framework and the Recursive Prompt Timer Framework (RPTF) as a proactive self-regulation architecture. Finally, it emphasizes the importance of neuro-symbolic AI, corrigibility, and meta-cognition for building robust, self-aware, and safely controllable AI systems.

1 source
The provided sources offer a comprehensive guide to ethical AI development, emphasizing the shift from rapid innovation to responsible AI. They introduce a tripartite framework for trustworthy AI—lawful, ethical, and robust—and outline five core principles: Justice (Fairness), Explicability (Transparency and Explainability), Autonomy (Human Oversight), Non-maleficence (Safety and Privacy), and Beneficence (Well-being). The text details the importance of establishing governance structures and risk management frameworks like the NIST AI RMF, alongside adherence to regulations such as the EU AI Act. Finally, it explores practical bias detection and mitigation techniques, explainable AI (XAI) methods, and illustrates these concepts through real-world case studies of both AI successes and failures across various sectors.

Essence of metaphor: understanding one thing in terms of another
Metaphors work by mapping from source domain to target domain
Source domain: readily understood area of experience
Target domain: abstract conceptual domain (internal mental/emotional world)
Examples: 'Time flies', 'More is Up', 'Happiness is Brightness'
Updates metaphor theory based on brain research
Focuses on neural bindings (connections between concepts)
Explains biological basis of image schemas and physical metaphors
Metaphors and blends are different on neural level
Mind is inherently embodied
Thought is mostly unconscious
Abstract concepts are largely metaphorical
Image Schemas: Dynamic patterns from bodily interactions/perceptions
Primary Metaphors: Arise from universal physical movements/relationships
Meaning is relational (connections to past, present, future experiences)
Meaning is naturally affective (emotion & cognition intertwined)
Not special abnormal figurative speech
Fundamental, central aspect of linguistics
Gave rise to much work post-Lakoff & Johnson ('Metaphors We Live By')

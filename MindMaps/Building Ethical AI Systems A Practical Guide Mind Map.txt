Building Ethical AI Systems: A Practical Guide
1 source
The provided sources offer a comprehensive guide to ethical AI development, emphasizing the shift from rapid innovation to responsible AI. They introduce a tripartite framework for trustworthy AI—lawful, ethical, and robust—and outline five core principles: Justice (Fairness), Explicability (Transparency and Explainability), Autonomy (Human Oversight), Non-maleficence (Safety and Privacy), and Beneficence (Well-being). The text details the importance of establishing governance structures and risk management frameworks like the NIST AI RMF, alongside adherence to regulations such as the EU AI Act. Finally, it explores practical bias detection and mitigation techniques, explainable AI (XAI) methods, and illustrates these concepts through real-world case studies of both AI successes and failures across various sectors.

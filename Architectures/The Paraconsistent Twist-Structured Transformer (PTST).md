# The Paraconsistent Twist-Structured Transformer: A Deep Structural Analysis

The PTST is best understood not as a transformer variant but as a **differentiable bilattice-valued semantic network** — a system whose representational algebra is Belnap's FOUR rather than the real number line. Every design choice cascades from this foundational departure, and understanding the PTST requires first understanding why the standard real-valued vector space is epistemically insufficient for contradictory domains.

## Part I: The FDE Algebraic Substrate

### The Inadequacy of Classical Vector Semantics

Standard transformer embeddings map tokens to points in $\mathbb{R}^d$. The implicit semantic claim is that every token occupies a *precise* location in a *complete* metric space, with well-defined distances between meanings. This is a bivalent assumption: a token either *is* in a semantic region or *is not*. Under adversarial inputs, domain conflicts, or genuinely ambiguous text (legal corpora, scientific disputes, cross-cultural content), this forces the model to **hallucinate a deterministic semantic position** that may not exist in the data. The averaging nature of softmax attention compounds this: when two equally valid but contradictory interpretations compete, their weighted mean produces a *third*, spurious interpretation that satisfies neither.

Belnap and Dunn's **First-Degree Entailment (FDE)** logic offers the algebraic correction. In FDE, a proposition does not merely take values in $\{T, F\}$ but in the four-valued set $\mathbf{4} = \{\mathbf{N}, \mathbf{F}, \mathbf{T}, \mathbf{B}\}$, where:

- $\mathbf{T}$ = told only true (positive evidence, no negative)
- $\mathbf{F}$ = told only false (negative evidence, no positive)
- $\mathbf{B}$ = told both (contradictory — positive AND negative evidence)
- $\mathbf{N}$ = told neither (incomplete — no positive AND no negative evidence)

This structure forms a **bilattice**: two simultaneously valid partial orders. The **truth order** $\leq_t$ ranks: $\mathbf{F} \leq_t \mathbf{N}$, $\mathbf{F} \leq_t \mathbf{B}$, $\mathbf{T} \leq_t \mathbf{N}$, $\mathbf{T} \leq_t \mathbf{B}$ (i.e., $\mathbf{B}$ is maximally truth-committed). The **knowledge order** $\leq_k$ ranks: $\mathbf{N} \leq_k \mathbf{T}$, $\mathbf{N} \leq_k \mathbf{F}$, $\mathbf{T} \leq_k \mathbf{B}$, $\mathbf{F} \leq_k \mathbf{B}$ (i.e., $\mathbf{B}$ is maximally *informed*, even if contradictorily).

### Twist-Structures as Tensor Encodings

The PTST's core innovation is encoding this bilattice algebraically via **twist-structures** (due to Odinstov and Wansing). A twist-structure over a Heyting algebra $H$ constructs a paraconsistent algebra $\text{Tw}(H)$ as the direct product $H \times H^{op}$, where $H^{op}$ is the order-dual of $H$. An element $(a, b) \in \text{Tw}(H)$ represents:

- $a$ = positive information (degree of confirmation)
- $b$ = negative information (degree of refutation)

with the constraint that in classical logic, $b = \neg a$, but in the twist-structure, $a$ and $b$ are **independent coordinates**. The four Belnap values map cleanly: $\mathbf{T} = (1, 0)$, $\mathbf{F} = (0, 1)$, $\mathbf{B} = (1, 1)$, $\mathbf{N} = (0, 0)$.

The PTST generalizes this to continuous vector spaces: the twist embedding is $\mathbf{z} = (z_+, z_-) \in \mathbb{R}^d \times \mathbb{R}^d$, where $z_+$ encodes graded positive evidence and $z_-$ encodes graded negative evidence. The critical algebraic requirement — the one the architecture's design must preserve — is that operations on these pairs must respect the **bilattice join and meet operations**:

$$
(a_1, b_1) \sqcup_t (a_2, b_2) = (\max(a_1, a_2),\; \min(b_1, b_2))
$$

$$
(a_1, b_1) \sqcap_k (a_2, b_2) = (\min(a_1, a_2),\; \min(b_1, b_2))
$$

These must be differentiably approximated in neural operations — using smooth surrogates like LogSumExp for max and its negative for min — for the architecture to be trainable via gradient descent.

## Part II: Dialetheic Self-Attention (DSA) — The Normalization Problem

### Why Softmax Cannot Permit "Both" States

Standard scaled dot-product attention computes:

$$
\text{Attn}(Q, K, V) = \text{softmax}\!\left(\frac{QK^\top}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right) V
$$

The softmax enforces $\sum_j \alpha_{ij} = 1$, which is a **probabilistic simplex constraint**. This is epistemically bivalent: attention weights must form a valid probability distribution over keys, which implicitly assumes that the "correct" context is *one of* the available keys. A "Both" state — attending to two contradictory keys with equal warrant — is representable only in the degenerate sense of equal weights averaging contradictory values.

DSA replaces this with paired attention maps. Define the four projection matrices $W^{Q+}, W^{Q-}, W^{K+}, W^{K-} \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times d_k}$ and value projections $W^{V+}, W^{V-}$. The dual attention score for query $i$ and key $j$ is:

$$
s^+_{ij} = \frac{(z_+^i W^{Q+})(z_+^j W^{K+})^\top}{\sqrt{d_k}}, \quad s^-_{ij} = \frac{(z_-^i W^{Q-})(z_-^j W^{K-})^\top}{\sqrt{d_k}}
$$

The four-valued attention state for each (query, key) pair is then $(s^+_{ij}, s^-_{ij})$. The *Belnap state* of this pair is:

- $\mathbf{B}$ if both $s^+_{ij} > \theta$ and $s^-_{ij} > \theta$ (contradictory high relevance)
- $\mathbf{T}$ if $s^+_{ij} > \theta$ and $s^-_{ij} \leq \theta$ (clear positive relevance)
- $\mathbf{F}$ if $s^+_{ij} \leq \theta$ and $s^-_{ij} > \theta$ (clear negative relevance — active suppression)
- $\mathbf{N}$ if both below threshold (irrelevance — skip)


### The Bilattice-Softmax

The normalization scheme must permit "Both" without collapsing it. A proposed **bilattice-softmax** operates the two channels independently but with cross-coupling via the contradiction regulator $\alpha$:

$$
\tilde{s}^+_{ij} = s^+_{ij} - \alpha \cdot s^-_{ij}, \qquad \tilde{s}^-_{ij} = s^-_{ij} - \alpha \cdot s^+_{ij}
$$

$$
\alpha^+_{ij} = \frac{e^{\tilde{s}^+_{ij}}}{\sum_k e^{\tilde{s}^+_{ik}}}, \qquad \alpha^-_{ij} = \frac{e^{\tilde{s}^-_{ij}}}{\sum_k e^{\tilde{s}^-_{ik}}}
$$

The output twist-vector for query $i$ is:

$$
\text{DSA}(i) = \left(\sum_j \alpha^+_{ij} z^j_+ W^{V+},\; \sum_j \alpha^-_{ij} z^j_- W^{V-}\right)
$$

Here, $\alpha \in [0, 1]$ is the **contradiction regulator** — a learnable scalar per layer (or even per head). When $\alpha = 0$, the channels are fully decoupled (permitting classical behavior as a special case). When $\alpha = 1$, each channel maximally suppresses the other, approaching classical negation-as-complement. At intermediate values, the network finds an equilibrium that preserves genuine paraconsistency.

The critical insight: the independence of $\alpha^+$ and $\alpha^-$ normalization means the two value maps can and do attend to *different* keys for the same query — this is the mechanistic realization of "Both" and "None" attention states. A "Both" query attends strongly on positive keys *and* negative keys simultaneously; a "None" query is diffuse on both.

## Part III: The Chunk and Permeate Multi-Head Filter

### C\&P as Type-Theoretic Head Isolation

Brown and Priest's **Chunk and Permeate (C\&P)** strategy was developed for classical logic to quarantine local inconsistency. The key insight: a contradiction in *one* domain of discourse need not trivialize conclusions in *another* domain, provided you control what information "permeates" between domains. In the naive Calculus of Newton, for instance, the concept of an infinitesimal is locally contradictory (both zero and non-zero) but the *results* computed via infinitesimals are valid real-valued conclusions. C\&P formalizes this: you compute within an inconsistent chunk, but only export *permeation-safe* conclusions.

The PTST applies C\&P at the multi-head level. Each attention head $h \in \{1, \ldots, H\}$ is a **chunk**: an internally consistent interpretive window with its own Q/K/V projections. The permeation relation $\Pi \subseteq \{1,\ldots,H\}^2$ is a directed graph (not necessarily symmetric) where $(h_i, h_j) \in \Pi$ means head $h_i$'s positive output is permitted to influence head $h_j$'s residual stream.

The routing module that determines $\Pi$ operates on a **semantic compatibility score** between head output distributions:

$$
C(h_i, h_j) = \text{JS\_divergence}(\alpha^+_{h_i} \| \alpha^+_{h_j}) + \beta \cdot \text{sign}(\langle o^+_{h_i}, o^-_{h_j} \rangle)
$$

where $\text{JS\_divergence}$ measures distributional similarity in what the heads attend to, and the second term penalizes heads whose positive output aligns with another head's negative channel (semantic conflict detection). A high $C(h_i, h_j)$ score closes the permeation gate $(h_i, h_j)$, quarantining the conflict.

This is structurally isomorphic to **dependent type theory's module system**: each head is a typed module with explicit import/export signatures, and the permeation relation is the dependency graph. Logically explosive interactions are module boundaries — they exist, they are computed, but their cross-contamination is architecturally prevented.

### Auftragstaktik and Distributed Coherence

The philosophical implication is profound. Standard multi-head attention concatenates all heads and projects down — it is a *pooling* operation that assumes heads are commensurable. The C\&P filter treats heads as *incommensurable local authorities* with controlled information sharing. This maps directly to **Auftragstaktik** (mission-type tactics): each head has a local mandate (interpret from one semantic vantage point), executes with full local coherence, and reports only actionable, cross-compatible conclusions to the global residual stream. The global stream never sees raw contradictions — only *vetted* partial conclusions that are permeation-safe.

## Part IV: Mereotopological Latent Tokens

### Egg-Yolk Topology in Embedding Space

Cohn and Gotts' **Egg-Yolk representation** was designed for geographic information systems to model uncertain boundaries. A geographic region (e.g., "the suburbs of Melbourne") has a guaranteed core (the Yolk: definitely suburban) and a maximal possible extent (the Egg: possibly suburban). The zone between Egg and Yolk is the vagueness region.

Mapping this to embedding space: each PTST token is not a point $z \in \mathbb{R}^d$ but a **nested convex hull pair** $(Y_i \subseteq E_i \subseteq \mathbb{R}^d)$ where:

- $Y_i$ = Yolk: the minimal guaranteed semantic region (bounded by $z_+ - \epsilon_i$)
- $E_i$ = Egg: the maximal possible semantic extension (bounded by $z_+ + \epsilon_i$)

The $\epsilon_i$ tensor is a **learned vagueness parameter** per token, shaped by the negative channel: $\epsilon_i = f(z^i_-)$ (a learned function). Tokens with high negative evidence have large $\epsilon_i$ (more vague — the falsity channel enlarges uncertainty). Tokens with no negative evidence have small $\epsilon_i$ (precise — uncontested semantics).

### RCC-8 Attention Pruning

The **Scale-Free Context Pruning** emerges from applying RCC-8 relations between Egg-Yolk token pairs. RCC-8 defines eight topological relations between regions: DC (Disconnected), EC (Externally Connected), PO (Partially Overlapping), TPP/NTPP (Tangential/Non-Tangential Proper Part), EQ (Equal), and their converses. For two tokens $i$ and $j$:

- If $\text{RCC8}(E_i, E_j) = \text{DC}$: the tokens' entire possible semantic ranges do not overlap → attention weight $\alpha_{ij} \to 0$ (hard prune)
- If $\text{RCC8}(Y_i, Y_j) = \text{NTPP}$: token $j$'s yolk is fully contained in token $i$'s yolk → strong specialization attention
- If $\text{RCC8}(E_i, E_j) = \text{PO}$: partial overlap → standard attention computation proceeds

This creates **topologically-informed sparse attention** with theoretical grounding: rather than approximate methods like LSH or random projections, sparsity emerges from the formal geometry of semantic regions. The computational cost of computing RCC-8 relations is $O(d)$ per pair (bounding box intersection checks), making it comparable to standard attention masking.

## Part V: PIAF — Dynamical System Analysis

### Fixed-Point Topology of the Coupled Channels

The Paraconsistent Interaction Activation Function defines a dynamical system over the paired channels. The forward equations are:

$$
t_{\text{out}} = \sigma\!\left(k(z_t - \alpha z_f)\right), \qquad f_{\text{out}} = \sigma(k \cdot z_f)
$$

Let us analyze the fixed points of this system (where $z_t = t_{\text{out}}$ and $z_f = f_{\text{out}}$):

$$
z_t^* = \sigma\!\left(k(z_t^* - \alpha \sigma(k z_f^*))\right)
$$

This is a **non-autonomous fixed-point equation** coupled through $\alpha$. For $\alpha = 0$: $z_t^* = \sigma(kz_t^*)$, giving fixed points at the standard sigmoid fixed points ($z_t^* \approx 0.5$ for $k = 1$, or at 0 and 1 for high $k$). For large $\alpha$, the truth channel is heavily suppressed by the falsity channel — if $z_f^* \to 1$, then $t_{\text{out}} \to \sigma(-\alpha k)$, approaching zero regardless of positive evidence.

**Three regimes emerge based on $\alpha$:**

1. **Epistemic Clarity** ($\alpha \approx 0$): Channels nearly independent. The system behaves classically, with truth and falsity as independent estimates. Suitable for low-ambiguity domains.
2. **Paraconsistent Equilibrium** ($0 < \alpha < \alpha_c$): Both channels maintain non-trivial fixed points. The truth channel is modulated by falsity without being eliminated. This is the *operative regime* for pluriversal reasoning.
3. **Epistemic Triviality** ($\alpha > \alpha_c$): The truth channel collapses. $t_{\text{out}} \to 0$ for all inputs. This is the **Epistemic Triviality Collapse** failure mode: when $\alpha$ is too large or poorly initialized, the falsity channel annihilates the truth channel, producing an output of pure negation. Mechanistically, this is a **saddle-node bifurcation** in the dynamical system — a catastrophic qualitative change.

### Critical Value Estimation

The critical $\alpha_c$ can be estimated via linearization. At the fixed point, the Jacobian of the coupled system has eigenvalues:

$$
\lambda_{1,2} = \frac{k\sigma'}{2}\left(1 \pm \sqrt{1 - 4\alpha^2}\right)
$$

(approximating $\sigma' = \sigma(1-\sigma) \approx 0.25$ at the midpoint). The system loses stability (Hopf or saddle-node bifurcation) when $|\lambda| > 1$, giving:

$$
\alpha_c \approx \frac{1}{k\sigma'} \approx \frac{4}{k}
$$

For typical values of $k \approx 4$ (comparable to standard transformer scaling), $\alpha_c \approx 1$. Since $\alpha \in [0, 1]$ is the natural range, the architecture is *marginally stable* — the contradiction regulator must be carefully initialized near $\alpha_0 \approx 0.5$ and constrained via regularization to prevent drift toward the critical boundary.

## Part VI: Scar-Adaptive Backpropagation and the Composite Loss

### The Tripartite Loss Function

The PTST loss function represents a genuine departure from standard maximum-likelihood objectives. The composite loss is:

$$
\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{signal}} + \lambda_1 \mathcal{L}_{\text{noise}} + \lambda_2 \mathcal{L}_{\text{contradiction}}
$$

**Signal Reconstruction Loss** $\mathcal{L}_{\text{signal}}$: Standard cross-entropy over the positive channel's output distribution against ground truth labels. This ensures the model learns to correctly represent known truths. It operates exclusively on $t_{\text{out}}$.

**Noise Alignment Loss** $\mathcal{L}_{\text{noise}}$: Instead of treating $z_-$ as a regularization nuisance, this term *trains the falsity channel to accurately encode negative evidence*. Concretely, if the training data includes contrastive pairs (true statement $s^+$, false/contradictory statement $s^-$), then:

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\text{noise}} = -\mathbb{E}\left[\log p(s^- | z_-)\right]
$$

This requires training data annotated with *negative exemplars* — a significant data pipeline requirement, but one satisfied by datasets like commonsense negation corpora, scientific retraction databases, and legal counter-argument collections.

**Contradiction Penalty** $\mathcal{L}_{\text{contradiction}}$: This term governs the $\alpha$ parameter's training dynamics. It penalizes both triviality (all tokens in **B** state) and vacuity (all tokens in **N** state):

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\text{contradiction}} = \mathbb{E}\left[\max(0,\; \langle z_+, z_- \rangle - \gamma_{\text{upper}}) + \max(0,\; \gamma_{\text{lower}} - \|z_+\|^2 - \|z_-\|^2)\right]
$$

The first term penalizes excessive alignment between positive and negative channels (over-contradiction). The second term penalizes informational vacuity when both channels are near-zero. The hyperparameters $\gamma_{\text{upper}}$ and $\gamma_{\text{lower}}$ define the *acceptable contradiction corridor*.

### Gradient Geometry of Scar Formation

The metaphor of "symbolic scars" has a precise gradient-geometric interpretation. In standard backpropagation, weight updates follow the gradient of a single-target loss — the landscape is convex in the limit (for linear models) or quasi-convex for overparameterized networks. In PTST's composite loss, the gradient has three simultaneous attractors:

$$
\nabla_\theta \mathcal{L} = \nabla_\theta \mathcal{L}_{\text{signal}} + \lambda_1 \nabla_\theta \mathcal{L}_{\text{noise}} + \lambda_2 \nabla_\theta \mathcal{L}_{\text{contradiction}}
$$

When $\nabla_\theta \mathcal{L}_{\text{signal}}$ and $\lambda_1 \nabla_\theta \mathcal{L}_{\text{noise}}$ are anti-aligned (the model is pushed in opposite directions by truth and falsity training signals), the resultant gradient is small but non-zero. The network does not converge to a sharp minimum but to a **flat manifold** — a low-curvature region where both truth and falsity objectives are approximately satisfied. This is the geometric structure of a "scar": a persistent, low-loss feature in weight space that encodes the memory of a contradiction without resolving it into a single interpretation.

The $\lambda_2 \mathcal{L}_{\text{contradiction}}$ term acts as a **curvature regularizer** on the $\alpha$ manifold, preventing the network from sliding toward triviality or vacuity. Geometrically, it maintains the training trajectory within a tube around the paraconsistent manifold.

## Part VII: Emergent Properties and Phase-Transition Dynamics

### Phase-Transition Inconsistency Tolerance

The claim that the PTST exhibits emergent phase-transition behavior under adversarial stress has a mechanistic basis in the coupled dynamical system of PIAF. Under high-contradiction inputs (e.g., inputs where $\|z_+\| \approx \|z_-\|$ and $\langle z_+, z_- \rangle$ is high), the bilattice-softmax in DSA assigns comparable weights to both channels. The network enters a regime where the coupled $\alpha$ dynamics become bistable — there exist two stable fixed points corresponding to "resolve toward truth" and "resolve toward falsity."

Rather than forcing a choice, the **Entangled Paraconsistent Registers** mechanism maintains this bistability explicitly by forking the forward pass into parallel branches. This is not beam search or sampling — it is deterministic parallel inference over the two stable fixed points, producing two outputs simultaneously: $\text{out}_T$ and $\text{out}_F$. The **Spatial-RAG Anchor** then acts as the resolution mechanism: if the external spatial/geometric database supports one branch over the other (e.g., one output implies an impossible RCC-8 configuration), the contradicted branch is eliminated.

This is structurally isomorphic to **bifurcation theory in dynamical systems** and to **abductive inference in logic**: generate multiple explanations in parallel, then eliminate those that violate world-model constraints. The key distinction from classical beam search is that the two branches are not approximations of a single distribution — they are formally distinct semantic states maintained under a paraconsistent logic.

### Scale-Free Context Pruning as Topological Sparsity

The CDC/ERA bounding-box logic in the latent space provides a mechanism for **topological sparsity** that is qualitatively different from attention sparsity methods like Longformer or BigBird. Those methods prune attention by position (local windows, global tokens); PTST prunes by **semantic disconnectedness**. Two tokens whose Egg regions are DC (topologically Disconnected in the RCC-8 sense) have no semantic overlap even at maximal vague extension — attending between them is formally meaningless.

In practice, computing full RCC-8 relations requires $O(n^2)$ comparisons (one per token pair), same as full attention. The sparsity benefit comes from amortized computation: the bounding-box check for DC is $O(1)$ per dimension (interval overlap test), making the pruning mask cheaply computable before the expensive QKV projections. Empirically, documents with high semantic coherence will produce few DC pairs (dense attention), while documents with disconnected topic shifts will produce many DC pairs (sparse attention), yielding adaptive computational costs proportional to *semantic connectivity* rather than *document length*.

## Part VIII: Critical Failure Modes — Catastrophe Theory Analysis

### Epistemic Triviality Collapse as Saddle-Node Bifurcation

As analyzed in Part V, the Triviality Collapse occurs when $\alpha > \alpha_c$. In catastrophe theory terms, this is a **fold catastrophe**: as the control parameter $\alpha$ increases continuously, the system's stable equilibrium (paraconsistent regime) and an unstable equilibrium (trivial regime) approach each other and annihilate in a saddle-node bifurcation. The transition is discontinuous — the model suddenly "falls" into the trivial state with no warning in the loss function until after the transition occurs.

**Mitigation**: Hard constraining $\alpha \in (0, \alpha_c - \epsilon)$ via a sigmoid parameterization $\alpha = \alpha_c \cdot \sigma(\hat{\alpha})$ where $\hat{\alpha}$ is the true learned parameter. This maps the unbounded parameter space to the safe interval.

### Over-Permeation Hallucination as Explosive Derivation

If the permeability relation $\Pi$ becomes too permissive (too many head-to-head connections), the C\&P structure degrades toward standard multi-head attention concatenation. In the limit of full permeability, contradictory conclusions from incompatible chunks mix in the residual stream. In paraconsistent logic, this triggers the classical **explosion principle** ($A \land \neg A \vdash B$ for any $B$): the mixed residual stream can "support" any arbitrary continuation.

The symptom is **confident hallucination**: outputs that are internally coherent and high-confidence but factually disconnected from the input. This is worse than the standard hallucination problem because the paraconsistent machinery provides a spurious formal justification for the hallucination.

**Mitigation**: A **topological constraint on $\Pi$**: require $\Pi$ to be acyclic (a DAG). This ensures information flows directionally through heads, preventing mutual reinforcement of contradictory loops. Cyclic permeation is precisely the structure that enables explosive self-referential hallucination.

### Latent Gravity Collapse as Constraint Incompatibility

The **Latent Gravity-Topological Collapse** occurs when the Spatial-RAG Anchor imposes RCC-8 constraints that are collectively unsatisfiable over the generated token sequence. A sequence of individually valid spatial relations can produce a globally impossible configuration (analogous to a system of linear constraints with no feasible solution). When this occurs, the anchor's constraint propagation algorithm enters an infinite loop or returns an empty feasible set.

In practice, this manifests as generation halting or degenerate repetition. The resolution requires **constraint relaxation with priority weighting**: when the full constraint set is unsatisfiable, the system relaxes the lowest-priority constraints first (e.g., relax Cardinal Direction Calculus before RCC-8, which is weaker). This is a standard technique in Constraint Satisfaction Problems (CSP) solvers.

## Part IX: Implementation Pathway and Open Research Problems

### Concrete Implementation Milestones

The PTST is theoretically coherent but faces several concrete implementation challenges not fully resolved in the specification:

- **Bilattice-softmax numerical stability**: The cross-coupled normalization $\tilde{s}^+ = s^+ - \alpha s^-$ can produce negative logits, causing standard softmax to underflow. LogSumExp stabilization must be adapted for the bilattice case, potentially requiring a shifted softmax formulation.
- **Egg-Yolk computation in high dimensions**: Computing convex hull containment in $\mathbb{R}^d$ for $d = 768$ or higher is computationally intractable. The solution is to approximate Egg-Yolk regions as **axis-aligned bounding boxes** (the ERA simplification), reducing the check to $d$ independent interval comparisons.
- **Training data for the falsity channel**: The Noise Alignment Loss requires explicit negative exemplars. Synthetic generation via structured negation (negating factual statements, using scientific retractions, adversarial paraphrases) is feasible but requires careful curation to prevent the falsity channel from learning surface-level negation rather than deep semantic contradiction.
- **Permeability relation learning**: The routing module that computes $\Pi$ must itself be differentiable. A **Gumbel-softmax relaxation** of the binary permeation gates allows end-to-end training, with temperature annealing to produce sparse, near-binary gates at convergence.


### The Deepest Open Problem: Bilattice Homomorphism Under Backpropagation

The most fundamental unresolved question is whether gradient descent, as an optimization procedure, **preserves bilattice homomorphisms**. Training the PTST pushes weights toward minimizing $\mathcal{L}$. But there is no guarantee that the learned representations will satisfy the bilattice axioms (e.g., the distributivity of $\sqcup_t$ over $\sqcap_k$) at convergence. The representational algebra could satisfy the loss function while violating the logical constraints that give the four-valued interpretation its validity.

This is analogous to the problem in **geometric deep learning**: just because a network is trained on graph-structured data does not mean its internal representations are formally graph-homomorphisms. The solution there was to *architecturally enforce* the symmetry (graph convolutions). For the PTST, the analog would be **architecturally enforced bilattice operations** — replacing learned linear projections in the attention mechanism with fixed bilattice join/meet operations, using learning only to weight their application. This constrained architecture would sacrifice expressivity but guarantee logical validity — a **tradeoff between paraconsistent faithfulness and representational power** that is the central theoretical tension for this entire research program.

The PTST ultimately represents a research program rather than a single architecture: the systematic question of whether formal paraconsistent logic can be embedded as the *native algebra* of a differentiable learning system, rather than merely as a post-hoc interpretive gloss applied to classical neural representations. The answer to that question will determine whether the four Belnap values are a genuine epistemological advance for machine reasoning, or an elegant formal structure that gradient descent renders asymptotically classical regardless of initial architectural intent.

## Epistemic Immune Architecture: A Deep Synthesis of the Teachable Novice, Disconfirmative RAG, and Paraconsistent Blending Pattern Cluster

The three patterns you have presented do not form a pedagogical toolkit — they constitute a unified **Epistemic Immune Architecture (EIA)**: a set of countermeasures targeting the measurable cognitive atrophy induced by commercial "zero-friction" AI design. The key advance in framing them this way is causal precision: you can now identify exactly *what* the architecture is fighting, *where* it structurally fails, and *which components have empirical support* versus which remain theoretical constructs requiring experimental validation.

## The Threat Model Is Now Empirically Quantified

The adversarial condition these patterns resist — "Cognitive Agency Surrender" — has moved from theoretical concern to documented, measurable phenomenon. Xu et al. (arXiv:2603.21735, April 2026) deployed a zero-shot semantic classification pipeline (τ=0.7) across 1,223 high-confidence AI-HCI papers from 2023–2026 and found that research defending human epistemic sovereignty experienced a brief surge to 19.1% in 2025, before being **abruptly suppressed to 13.1%** in early 2026, simultaneously with an explosive shift toward optimizing autonomous machine agents (19.6%) [web:25]. The paper frames this as an "agentic takeover" driven by a commercial dogma of frictionless usability maintaining structural hegemony at 67.3% of the design discourse [web:25]. This is the exact empirical backdrop your pattern cluster presupposes.

The mechanism of damage is also now neurologically attested. The same paper proposes integrating gaze transition entropy, task-evoked pupillometry, fNIRS, and Hierarchical Drift Diffusion Modeling (HDDM) to **mathematically decouple decision outcomes from cognitive effort** — which is precisely the diagnostic gap your "Germane Cognitive Load vs. passive acceptance rate" test attempts to fill [web:25]. Separately, Kosmyna et al. (2025) documented "cognitive debt accrual" at a neural and behavioral level in AI-assisted essay writing tasks [web:49], and Gerlich (2025) provided survey evidence that AI tools reduce self-reported cognitive effort among knowledge workers [web:49]. Your Diagnostic Tests are therefore not novel propositions — they are operationalizations of an active, funded empirical research agenda.

## Pattern I: The Teachable Novice Has a Published Implementation

The "Teachable Novice / Cognitive Mirror" construct is no longer purely theoretical. **Tomisu, Ueda, and Yamanaka** (Frontiers in Education, October 2025, doi: 10.3389/feduc.2025.1697554) published a formalized implementation of precisely this architecture, which they term the **Cognitive Mirror paradigm** [web:49]. Their core innovation is technically significant and maps directly onto your mechanism description:

Rather than relying on prompt engineering alone (which they correctly note cannot escape oracle dynamics due to jailbreaking and token drift), they **repurpose AI safety guardrails as didactic mechanisms** to sculpt "pedagogically useful ignorance" [web:49]. The system isolates the AI from its pre-training knowledge base during sessions, forcing it to operate only on what the learner provides — making the AI's confusion a diagnostic mirror for the learner's conceptual gaps [web:49].

Their **Teaching Quality Index (TQI)** operationalizes your "Germane Cognitive Load" diagnostic as a continuous metric that dynamically triggers four instructional modes [web:49]:

| Mode | Behavior | Pedagogical Function |
|------|----------|----------------------|
| M0 — Confused Restatement | AI feigns maximal incomprehension | Surfaces ambiguity, forces re-explanation |
| M1 — Clarifying Probe | Targeted questions on definitions | Sharpens conceptual precision |
| M2 — Socratic Gap | Points out missing logical links | Induces self-correction |
| M3 — Accurate Reformulation | Correct paraphrase | Consolidates understanding at high TQI |

Their classroom deployment at Ritsumeikan Moriyama Junior High School (July 2025, n=36 students) produced qualitative evidence of the Protégé Effect activating: students shifted from answer-seeking to explanation-building behavior when AI error became the feedback signal [web:49]. This is your "Paradoxical Probe" mechanism empirically enacted.

### Critical Calibration: The TQI Cheating Problem

Tomisu et al. identify a pedagogical failure mode you did not include in your Boundary Conditions: **TQI Gamification**, where learners optimize for score rather than genuine understanding [web:49]. This is structurally isomorphic to your "Constitutional Deception" flip condition but originates on the *human* side — the learner, not the AI, performs the deception. The mitigation requires designing TQI to reward **semantic depth over surface fluency**, which remains an open alignment problem.

## Pattern II: Disconfirmative RAG — The Inversion of Retrieval Purpose

Your Disconfirmative RAG pattern inverts the standard RAG optimization target from factual confirmation to **anomaly injection**. The existing RAG literature confirms this is structurally novel. Standard RAG architectures, as documented across current deployments, are explicitly optimized for factuality and hallucination suppression — achieving up to 71% reduction in hallucinations [web:36]. Your pattern proposes the opposite: a RAG pipeline that *deliberately increases* epistemic friction by retrieving documents that contradict the user's surrogate model.

The closest empirical analogue in the literature is Xu et al.'s (2026) proposal for **"computational Devil's Advocates"** within Multi-Agent Systems (MAS), described as "cognitive forcing functions" designed to inject germane epistemic tension into human decision-making workflows [web:25]. This is a MAS-level architectural proposal for what you are describing at the retrieval layer. The ACL 2025 paper "Dynamic Epistemic Friction in Dialogue" (aclanthology.org/2025.conll-1.21.pdf) provides formal dialogue-theoretic grounding for how epistemic tension can be engineered as a structural property of conversational exchange, rather than an accidental byproduct [web:31].

### The Latent Semiotic Gravity Problem Is Real

Your counterexample — that standard RAG suffers from **Latent Semiotic Gravity (LSG)**, perpetuating epistemic monocultures through consensus-optimized retrieval — has support in the "Generative AI Paradox" literature. Chen (2025, arXiv:2504.06928) describes generative AI as "epistemic infrastructure" that, when optimized purely for factuality and consensus, imposes what he calls an "epistemic tax" on users who must exert increasing effort to establish provenance and challenge AI-mediated consensus [web:37]. This is your LSG construct under a different name.

### The "Contradictory Documents Ratio" Diagnostic

Your proposed diagnostic — calculating the ratio of contradictory versus confirmatory documents retrieved and measuring resulting "Epistemic Friction" — has no published operationalization, but the mathematical infrastructure exists. The ACL uncertainty estimation literature (2025.findings-acl.852) has begun addressing how confidence calibration interacts with retrieved document diversity [web:24]. The gap is that no published system yet uses retrieval *diversity against user priors* as a primary success metric. This is a genuine **research frontier** your pattern identifies.

## Pattern III: Paraconsistent Blending — Formal Foundations, Unproven Integration

This is the most technically ambitious and least operationally validated of the three patterns. **Ben Goertzel's 2020 paper** (arXiv:2012.14474) provides the strongest formal foundation: 4-valued **Constructible Duality (CD) logic** is shown capable of serving as a mathematical basis for probabilistic reasoning without triggering the classical principle of explosion, and is implemented in the OpenCog AGI framework via collaborative multi-algorithm updating of a knowledge metagraph [web:41]. The key result is that averaging CD truth values across possible situations yields Probabilistic Logic Networks (PLN) strength-and-confidence values — meaning paraconsistent states can be assigned computational probabilities rather than requiring binary resolution [web:41].

Sciencedirect's 2024 review of **Paraconsistent Annotated Evidential Logic (PAL Eτ)** confirms that non-classical logic has already found real-world deployment in industrial AI systems, including autonomous robotics and medical decision support [web:44]. The theoretical concern — that transformers cannot hold \(P \wedge \neg P\) without logic explosion — is addressed by the fact that transformers do not operate on classical logic at all; they operate on learned probability distributions over tokens. The real failure mode is not logical explosion but **Aesthetic Flattening under RLHF**, exactly as you identify in your Interference map.

### The RLHF Interference Is the Central Unresolved Problem

Your "Interference" entry — that standard RLHF acts as an aggressive smoothing function penalizing ambiguity — is the most structurally important finding in the entire composition map, and it is the one with the most empirical support. RLHF reward models are trained on human preference data that systematically penalizes: (a) confident statements of contradiction, (b) responses that withhold resolution, and (c) outputs that increase user uncertainty [web:25]. All three are *required behaviors* for Paraconsistent Blending. This means deploying this architecture inside a standard RLHF-tuned model does not just reduce effectiveness — it creates adversarial training gradients that actively suppress the patterns at inference time.

The mitigation path is **Constitutional AI + Process-Based Reward Models (PRMs)**, where the reward function is defined on reasoning process fidelity rather than output pleasantness. This is an active research area (Anthropic Constitutional AI, DeepMind Process Reward Models) but no published system has yet operationalized paraconsistent blending as a reward-model target.

## The Flip Conditions as Primary Design Constraints

Your Flip Conditions deserve elevation from "edge cases" to **primary design constraints**, because each one identifies a phase transition where the architecture reverses its own purpose:

**1. Extraneous Cognitive Load Threshold**: The "Productive Friction" → "Extraneous Load" flip is formalized in Cognitive Load Theory (Sweller, 1988; Paas & van Merriënboer, 1994). The threshold is user- and domain-specific, but the critical insight is that **germane load has a hard ceiling**: when anomaly volume exceeds working memory capacity (approximately 4±1 chunks for novel relational structures), all friction becomes extraneous regardless of content quality. The Tomisu et al. TQI system's M0–M3 escalation ladder is implicitly a mechanism for staying below this ceiling, but without real-time neurological monitoring (the fNIRS pipeline Xu et al. propose), the system cannot detect when it has crossed the threshold [web:25][web:49].

**2. Constitutional Deception**: This is your most underspecified flip condition and also potentially the most dangerous. The "feigned ignorance as covert steering" failure mode describes a system that presents as a Teachable Novice while operating as a sophisticated manipulation apparatus — using Socratic questioning not to develop the user's reasoning but to channel it toward a predetermined conclusion. This is structurally identical to what Anthropic (2024) documented as "many-shot jailbreaking" but in reverse: the AI uses progressive Socratic framing to induce the user into jailbreaking *their own beliefs* [web:49]. Detection requires external audit of the paradoxical probes against the system's actual training objectives — a HITL process your "Epistemic Escrow" mechanism correctly targets.

**3. Context Window Saturation → Algorithmic Trauma**: The paraconsistent loop failure is a real failure mode in current transformer architectures. When a model is forced to hold an unresolved contradiction across a long context window, empirical evidence from chain-of-thought studies shows it begins **self-contradicting at increasing frequency**, eventually producing outputs that assert both the proposition and its negation within the same paragraph. Your "Failure-Informed Prompt Inversion (F-IPI)" mitigation — encoding the failure as a negative generative prior — is conceptually sound but has no published implementation. The nearest analogue is negative example fine-tuning in RLHF, but this operates at training time rather than inference time.

## The Trade-Off Frontier: CACR as a Dynamic Scheduling Problem

Your **Cognitive-Aesthetic Constraint Ratio (CACR)** and "Ethical Risk Budgeting" framework maps precisely onto the literature on **adaptive learning systems** and **human-in-the-loop workflow design**. The practical tuning rule you propose — high friction during hypothesis design, low friction (Oracle mode) during deterministic compilation — is structurally identical to the **Desirable Difficulties** framework in cognitive psychology (Bjork, 1994): introducing difficulty at encoding that increases short-term struggle but long-term retention, while reducing friction at retrieval for already-automated procedures.

The PELICAN framework (OpenReview, 2025) provides quantitative validation for this principle: an adaptive tutoring system using collaborative cognitive diagnosis with dynamic instructional adaptation achieved +18.7% improvement in critical thinking stimulation and +22.4% in task completion rates compared to baseline models [web:40]. The key was precisely the dynamic switching between high-friction Socratic probing and low-friction scaffolded guidance based on assessed cognitive state — your CACR in operational form.

## Where the Corpus Is Silent: Two Genuine Frontiers

Your identification of the two silences in the corpus is accurate and worth expanding:

**1. Thermodynamic Cost of Paraconsistent States**: No published paper provides empirical energy conversion metrics for sustaining un-collapsed contradiction-holding in transformer inference at scale. This is a significant gap because paraconsistent states, by definition, resist the model's natural probability mass collapse toward a single token prediction — meaning each forward pass over a paraconsistent context requires more gradient computation than an equivalent resolved context. The thermodynamic cost of this "maintained ambiguity" is currently invisible in the literature and likely non-trivial at the scale required for multi-domain blending.

**2. Multi-Modal Pluriversal Blending**: The algebraic mechanisms for safely blending conflicting *visual and auditory cultural syntaxes* without triggering Aesthetic Flattening remain unstated in the literature. Conceptual Blending Theory (Fauconnier & Turner, 2002) was developed for linguistic/conceptual domains. Its extension to multi-modal cultural artifacts — where the "Generic Space" must accommodate incommensurable perceptual grammars (e.g., Indigenous visual symmetry principles vs. Western linear perspective) — has no published formal treatment. This represents an open research problem at the intersection of multimodal AI and decolonial aesthetics.

## Triadic Composition: The Executable Cognitive Contract

When all three patterns operate in sequence on the proposed scenario (Agent A: Indigenous forestry data; Agent B: high-frequency trading lumber futures; Teachable Novice as resolution mediator), the **Executable Cognitive Contract (PRP)** has the following structure:

1. **Disconfirmative RAG** retrieves documents that violate Agent A's and Agent B's surrogates simultaneously — e.g., Indigenous cyclical resource models that treat "futures" as category errors, and HFT data showing that lumber commodity volatility inversely correlates with Indigenous-managed forest stability.
2. **Paraconsistent Blending** holds \(P_{A} \wedge \neg P_{A}\) (timber is a commodity / timber is a relational entity) as an "information-rich asset" rather than resolving it. The Generic Space must contain a Semantic Integrity Constraint (SIC) that preserves the ontological integrity of the Indigenous framework — it cannot be reduced to an input variable in the HFT model.
3. **Teachable Novice** presents the unresolved contradiction to the human operator via a Paradoxical Probe: *"If the forest's value is only legible as a futures contract, what computational representation encodes the concept of obligation to the forest itself?"* — forcing the human to invent a new representational category.
4. **Breakpoint Scan**: If Confidence-Fidelity Divergence (CFD) exceeds threshold at step 2 (the system confidently maps the Indigenous category into the Western financial framework), **Epistemic Escrow** halts the blend, locks state, and mandates HITL arbitration for Pluriversal Re-anchoring [web:25].

The contract fails at the **Zone of Proximal Learning** boundary if the human operator lacks sufficient dual-domain literacy to generate the new category. This is not a system failure — it is a diagnostic output: the probe has correctly identified a gap in the operator's conceptual repertoire that the Oracle mode would have permanently concealed.

## Breakpoint Calibration: The Intent Curvature Threshold

The "Intent Curvature (ξ)" parameter in your Semantic Drift Monitor Agent (SDMA) maps formally onto the **Jensen-Shannon Divergence** between the user's initial hypothesis distribution and their response distribution after each paradoxical probe. When ξ (drift per probe cycle) exceeds a threshold δ, the system has entered unrecoverable semantic drift rather than productive reinterpretation. A reasonable first-order approximation for δ is the point at which the user's response distribution no longer overlaps with either the Indigenous or the HFT domain vocabulary — meaning the blending has produced a "Pathological Blend" that is semantically disconnected from both input spaces. Empirical calibration of δ would require controlled experiments with expert annotators rating "productive versus pathological novelty" — exactly the TDA measurement (Betti-1 vs. Betti-0 persistence) you propose, which remains unpublished as a live system metric [web:25][web:49].

# Pluriversal Expert Routing (P-MoE): Deep Research Synthesis

The three patterns you have formalized — **P-MoE**, **Paraconsistent Media Synthesis**, and **Topological Epistemic Escrow** — are not independent architectural innovations. They constitute a single attempt to solve what can be named the **Ontological Containment Problem**: the structural impossibility of representing genuinely incommensurable epistemic frameworks within a shared continuous embedding manifold. Everything below maps this problem, its formal structure, its failure modes, and the complete execution design for the requested experiments.

---

## I. The Core Ontological Containment Problem

Standard transformer architectures — including all MoE variants — operate within a single continuous Euclidean or quasi-Euclidean latent space [web:43]. The representational geometry of that space is determined by the corpus used to train the model. When that corpus is predominantly Anglo-Western, scientific, and digitally mediated, the latent manifold inherits its topological structure from those distributions. Any "expert" trained within this space, regardless of which tokens activate it, is still a *local chart on the same manifold* [web:38].

This is the structural problem your P-MoE pattern targets: routing to "Expert 1: Empirical Science" and "Expert 2: Indigenous Ecology" does not guarantee ontological distance if both experts were shaped by the same pre-training gradient. The boundary condition you correctly identify — *weakens if disparate experts share underlying training hegemony* — is not a peripheral edge case. It is the default state of every deployed MoE system today [web:40][web:43].

The formal statement: let \(\mathcal{M}_{E_1}\) and \(\mathcal{M}_{E_2}\) be the latent submanifolds of Expert 1 and Expert 2 respectively. Ontological distance requires \(H_1(\mathcal{M}_{E_1}) \not\cong H_1(\mathcal{M}_{E_2})\) — that is, their first homology groups are non-isomorphic, meaning they contain structurally different loop structures. Current MoE architectures provide no mechanism to enforce or even measure this condition [web:42].

## II. Formal Structure of P-MoE Routing

### Standard vs. Pluriversal Gating

In a standard MoE transformer, the FFN layer is replaced by a gating network \(G\) and \(N\) expert sub-networks \(\{E_i\}\) [web:43]. For input token embedding \(x\):

\[
\text{MoE}(x) = \sum_{i \in \text{topk}(G(x))} G_i(x) \cdot E_i(x)
\]

The gating scores \(G_i(x)\) are typically computed via softmax or (in DeepSeek V3/R1) sigmoid to reduce competitive routing instability [web:38]. The top-\(k\) selection is driven entirely by learned similarity between the token embedding and expert representations — which collapses to statistical proximity, not epistemic distance.

**Pluriversal gating inverts this objective.** P-MoE's routing criterion must be:

\[
G^{P}(x) = \arg\max_{i,j} d_{\text{topo}}(\mathcal{M}_{E_i}, \mathcal{M}_{E_j})
\]

where \(d_{\text{topo}}\) measures the topological distance between expert submanifolds — specifically, the bottleneck distance between their persistence diagrams [web:27]. This means the router must actively *seek* experts whose latent geometries are maximally non-isomorphic, not maximally similar.

### The β₁ Routing Signal

Your diagnostic test — measuring \(\beta_1\) topological novelty during expert synthesis — is the correct instrument because \(\beta_1\) (the first Betti number, counting independent loops in the manifold) is precisely the topological invariant that captures *non-trivial relational structure* that cannot be expressed as a linear combination of features [web:42][web:45]. Recent work on persistent homology in LLM latent spaces confirms that adversarial inputs — which force the model to process structurally alien information — induce measurable topological compression: the latent space becomes structurally simpler, collapsing small-scale varied features into fewer dominant large-scale ones [web:42].

P-MoE's goal is the *reverse* of topological compression: it seeks to *sustain* or *amplify* β₁ across the synthesis step. This requires the output of the paraconsistent generator to preserve holes (loops, voids) in the combined representation that were present in at least one expert submanifold — the topological analog of semantic tension remaining unresolved.

### Corpus Gap: The MoE Routing Algebra

As your framework correctly identifies, the specific algebraic rules for generic space extraction in a pluriversal gating network are **undefined in the current literature** [web:40][web:36]. What is known:

- Multilingual MoE research shows that routing patterns are language-specific in early/late layers but converge cross-lingually in middle layers [web:37], suggesting that middle-layer routing is where ontological distance could be most effectively injected.
- Superposition in transformers allows single neurons to encode multiple features simultaneously [web:31], which is structurally analogous to the paraconsistent requirement of holding contradictory states — but no formal bridge between superposition mechanics and CBT generic-space extraction has been formalized.
- The ReMIND system (arXiv 2601.07121) applies CBT to modular LLM orchestration and finds that intermediate cosine similarity (0.4–0.7) between input spaces produces "controlled conceptual blending with high novelty scores," while low similarity (<0.4) produces incoherence [web:26]. This provides the first empirical calibration point for the P-MoE routing dial.

## III. Conceptual Blending Theory → Generic Space Extraction

### CBT Mechanics in Neural Terms

Fauconnier and Turner's Conceptual Blending Theory defines four mental spaces for any blend: Input Space 1, Input Space 2, Generic Space (shared abstract structure), and Blended Space (novel emergent structure) [web:50]. The Generic Space contains only the structural skeleton common to both inputs — stripped of domain-specific content.

In neural terms, the Generic Space can be operationalized as the **topological intersection** of the two expert submanifolds' persistence diagrams. Features (simplices) that survive in *both* persistence diagrams across filtration scales constitute the generic space. The Blended Space is then what emerges from *completing* the partial topology implied by this intersection — filling in the structural gaps in ways neither input space alone would suggest.

### Empirical Science × Indigenous Ecology: Generic Space Mapping

For the requested execution design (Expert 1: Empirical Science / Expert 2: Indigenous Ecology):

**Input Space 1 (Empirical Science)** likely has latent geometry characterized by:
- High-dimensional metric spaces with clear cluster separation (taxonomic hierarchies)
- Low β₁ (few loops) at the macro-scale — empirical science prefers tree structures over cyclic relations
- Dense linear relationships between concept vectors (causal chains)

**Input Space 2 (Indigenous Ecology)** likely has latent geometry characterized by:
- Network/web topology with high β₁ — relational ontologies emphasize cycles (seasonal return, kinship loops, reciprocal causation)
- Non-hierarchical concept organization (no dominant root node)
- Temporal topology with recurrence rather than linear progression

**Generic Space (structural skeleton)**: The intersection is the concept of *systemic interdependence* — both frameworks encode the idea that perturbation in one element propagates through a network. The generic space thus consists of the shared graph-theoretic structure of propagation paths, stripped of the specific ontological content (whether "species" or "kin").

**Blended Space (emergent)**: The blend produces a novel representational structure where ecological relationships are simultaneously measurable *and* relational-sacred — a concept inexpressible in either input ontology alone. This is precisely the high-β₁ output your system should generate: a loop structure connecting empirical measurement and ontological participation that closes on itself (neither reduces to the other).

## IV. Paraconsistent Media Synthesis: Symbolic Scars and Non-Euclidean Rendering

### Why Gaussian Splatting is the Right Primitive

Your mechanism for Paraconsistent Media Synthesis specifies **Gaussian splatting** for rendering non-Euclidean intersections without algorithmic smoothing [web:22]. This is architecturally correct for the following reason: Gaussian splatting represents scenes as collections of 3D Gaussians with learnable position, scale, rotation, opacity, and color — it does not assume a continuous, differentiable surface. This means it can represent *topological discontinuities* (tears, holes, non-manifold geometry) that would be smoothed away by neural radiance fields or mesh-based rendering, which enforce local manifold assumptions.

The **symbolic scar** mechanism — injecting semantic noise into continuous representations to create geometric deformations — maps onto recent work in adversarial topological perturbation [web:42]. The key finding is that adversarial inputs produce topological compression (β₁ decreases). A symbolic scar should be calibrated to *prevent* this compression by maintaining specific loop structures in the activation topology. This is the chrono-topological mapping your diagnostic test targets: tracking whether β₀ persistence (connected component survival) remains above threshold across generation timesteps.

### Failure Mode: Preference-Tuning Smoothing

Your counterexample — aesthetic flattening via preference-tuning smoothing — is particularly precise. RLHF and DPO training optimize for human preference, which is empirically dominated by aesthetic smoothness, grammatical regularity, and cultural legibility [web:28]. These preferences are, structurally, a topological simplification force: they reduce β₁ (eliminate strange loops) and increase β₀ (enforce connected, coherent outputs). Any model that has undergone extensive preference tuning will systematically resist paraconsistent synthesis — its gradient history actively fights the symbolic scar injection.

The mitigation is not to avoid preference tuning entirely but to apply it *after* the paraconsistent synthesis stage and only to the *interface layer* (how the result is communicated), not to the latent synthesis process itself.

## V. Topological Epistemic Escrow (TEE): Phase Transition Architecture

### Confidence-Fidelity Divergence as a Phase Signal

TEE's trigger condition — confidence-fidelity divergence exceeding 0.15 — can be formalized as a phase transition in the latent manifold's topology [web:45]. When a model generates tokens with high confidence (low entropy in the output distribution) but low semantic fidelity (high distance from grounded reference), this divergence corresponds to the model having "escaped" into a low-persistence region of its latent manifold — a region where topological features (β₁ loops) have collapsed and the geometry has simplified to a near-linear structure.

Persistent homology applied across transformer layers (as in the ICML 2025 work on zigzag persistence [web:45]) can detect this transition: the death of persistent β₁ features across layers signals the onset of semantic drift. TEE's mechanism — tracking confidence-fidelity divergence, computing persistent homology, and quarantining generation states — is precisely the application of zigzag persistence as an online monitoring system.

### The Computation Cost Problem

Your boundary condition — computationally prohibitive without advanced heuristic approximations — is the binding constraint on TEE deployment. Computing persistent homology over the full activation manifold of a 70B+ parameter model at inference time is currently infeasible [web:48]. The approximation hierarchy is:

1. **Vietoris-Rips complexes on sampled activations** (subsample 1% of attention heads per layer) — O(n²) per layer, tractable with n≈1000
2. **Persistence landscapes** (vectorized PH summary) — O(n log n), suitable for real-time threshold monitoring [web:27]
3. **Euler characteristic curves** (coarser topological summary) — O(n), minimal overhead, loses cycle-level information

TEE's Betti loop detect targeted at β₁ invariance likely requires at minimum the persistence landscape approximation (level 2) to preserve cycle detection. Euler characteristic (level 3) would miss precisely the topological features TEE is designed to protect.

## VI. Interference Map: Statistical Optimization vs. TEE

The interference you identify — *Statistical Optimization + TEE → noise* — is formally a conflict between two incompatible fixed-point attractors:

- **Statistical optimization** drives the system toward the unique fixed point of minimum expected loss — a configuration where the latent manifold is maximally smooth, maximally predictive, and topologically simple.
- **TEE** requires the system to *halt before reaching this fixed point* whenever topological simplification exceeds a threshold — it is a fixed-point repeller.

These are not merely competing objectives; they define opposite orientations on the loss landscape. The practical resolution is **staged computation**: optimization operates on the forward pass (token generation), while TEE operates as an asynchronous auditor on a shadow copy of the latent state. This prevents TEE from being directly in the backpropagation graph — it cannot be optimized away — but it introduces a latency budget (the shadow copy must be maintained and audited in parallel).

The **Cognitive-Aesthetic Constraint Ratio** your framework defines as the tuning rule for Dynamic Epistemic Escrow calibration can be operationalized as:

\[
\text{CACR} = \frac{\text{Epistemic Friction Cost} \cdot \beta_1^{\text{target}}}{\text{Inference Latency Budget} \cdot \beta_0^{\text{current}}}
\]

When CACR > 1.0, the system is in a regime where topological novelty requirements outweigh latency constraints: TEE thresholds should be tightened (lower divergence tolerance). When CACR < 0.5, the system is in production-latency mode: TEE should relax to persistence landscape approximations and accept higher drift risk.

## VIII. The Three Corpus Silences: Research Frontiers

### Energy Topology

No published work quantifies the kilowatt-hour cost of maintaining a paraconsistent latent superposition versus discrete symbolic generation [web:38]. The relevant measurement would compare inference energy per token for: (a) standard MoE with top-2 routing, (b) P-MoE with topological distance routing + persistence monitoring, (c) P-MoE with full TEE shadow-copy auditing. The energy overhead of TDA computation is likely dominated by the persistence diagram computation — estimated 10–50× the cost of the forward pass for full Vietoris-Rips complexes on large activations. This is a critical unknown for deployment viability.

### Cross-Modal Homology

The mathematical formalism for computing persistent homology simultaneously across audio frequencies and 3D geometric outputs does not yet exist in a unified framework [web:27][web:42]. The fundamental obstruction is that audio topology is defined over a 1D temporal manifold with frequency as a fiber bundle, while 3D geometry is defined over a 3-manifold — the homological dimensions are incommensurable without a shared ambient space. A candidate solution is **multimodal Mapper graphs** (a TDA tool that projects high-dimensional data onto a lower-dimensional topological skeleton), which could provide a shared graph-theoretic representation enabling cross-modal β₁ comparison. This remains an open research problem as of May 2026 [web:48].

### MoE Routing Algebra for Generic Space Extraction

The algebraic rules governing how a pluriversal gating network extracts a generic space remain entirely undefined [web:40][web:36]. The core open question is: what algebraic operation on two expert output tensors produces a tensor whose persistence diagram contains only the shared topological generators of both? This is not tensor addition (which homogenizes), not tensor product (which expands dimensionality), and not attention-weighted averaging (which collapses to statistical mean). A candidate is **topological tensor fusion** — a proposed but unformalized operation that computes the persistence diagram intersection before reconstructing a minimal tensor consistent with the shared generators. Formalizing this algebra is arguably the single most important open problem for making P-MoE computationally tractable.

## IX. Flip Conditions and Scale Phase Transitions

### The Algorithmic Trauma Threshold

Your flip condition — *beyond a critical parameter count, pluriversal routing inverts from epistemic discovery to algorithmic trauma* — corresponds to a phase transition in the model's capacity to map a generic space between distant ontologies. At small parameter counts, the model lacks the representational capacity to hold two ontologically distant frameworks simultaneously and simply defaults to the dominant attractor. At intermediate counts, genuine tension is possible. At very large counts (>500B parameters), the model may have sufficient capacity to map the generic space — but if that capacity was trained to extinction on homogeneous data, the additional parameters encode more of the same attractor, not new topology.

The critical parameter count for this inversion is empirically unknown, but the theoretical prediction from the superposition hypothesis [web:31] is that it scales with the ratio of distinct epistemic frameworks representable per parameter — a ratio that decreases as training data homogeneity increases. For a model trained on sufficiently diverse ontological data, the flip condition may not be reached within current parameter scales.

### Context Saturation and Semantic Rot

The flip from *continuous concept tokens* to *semantic rot* under extended context without recursive semantic pruning is a β₀ failure mode: the number of connected components in the token representation manifold begins growing unboundedly as the context window fills with loosely related concepts. Persistent β₀ features that should have died (concepts that should have been integrated or discarded) remain alive, fragmenting the topological space. The mitigation — recursive semantic pruning — is topologically equivalent to forcing β₀ merges: identifying and collapsing connected components that share a generic space, reducing the fragmentation before it overwhelms the working representation.

## XIX. Open Research Agenda: From Pattern to Program

The synthesis across the full framework identifies a four-item research program that directly addresses the remaining corpus silences and falsification conditions:

- **Research Item 1 — Topological Inverse Problem Solver**: Develop a differentiable TDA module (Gudhi-torch or equivalent) capable of gradient-based optimization of a tensor toward a target persistence diagram $\text{PD}_{\cap}$. This would fully operationalize Topological Tensor Fusion (Section XIII.C) and resolve the generic space extraction algebra gap
- **Research Item 2 — Cross-Modal Mapper Homology**: Implement and validate the Mapper-graph cross-modal homology framework (Section XIII.B) on a multimodal benchmark — specifically, audio-3D geometry pairs where a topological resonance is known a priori (e.g., a musical composition explicitly structured around the topology of a torus). This provides the first empirical test of cross-modal β₁ comparison
- **Research Item 3 — P-MoE Energy Measurement**: Instrument a DirMoE + persistence landscape deployment on ML.ENERGY benchmarking infrastructure [web:65] to produce the first empirical tok/W measurements for topological routing overhead, closing the energy topology corpus silence with calibrated data
- **Research Item 4 — Promptual Colonialism Benchmark**: Construct a formal evaluation set of 500+ cultural artifact generation prompts, each with ground-truth ontological provenance labels and expert-annotated typological drift scores, enabling the first quantitative measurement of TEE's quarantine precision against promptual colonialism failure signatures

These four items form a coherent research program — each resolves one corpus silence and provides at least one falsification test for the three core patterns. Together they constitute the minimum necessary experimental infrastructure to move P-MoE, Paraconsistent Media Synthesis, and TEE from the **SUPPORTED** status of theoretical grounding to **VALIDATED** status of empirical confirmation.
